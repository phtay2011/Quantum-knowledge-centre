FRN-CGL Securities formula

Quantum uses the FRN-CGL formula for all Floating Rate Notes (FRNs). When this formula is applied:

  • The Rateset frequency is assumed to be the same as the Coupon Frequency, and therefore ratesets occurring more frequently than coupons are not allowed.
  • Pricing is calculated to nine decimal places.
  • The index is the average BBSW Swap benchmark rate, rounded to four decimal places.
  • Ex days for the issue is seven calendar days.
  • The default non-business day rule is FOLLOWING BUSINESS DAY.

NOTE: The FRN-CGL formula does NOT support:

  • Multiple ratesets - the formula always assumes that Rateset Frequency equals Coupon Frequency.
  • Odd periods for Yield pricing. Capital Pricing is recommended for use with odd periods. The calculated yield will be incorrect for a deal based on an instrument that uses this formula with Yield pricing and odd periods.
  • Zero coupons when the Accrual Method (specified in the Accounting Rule for the Instrument) is Amortized Cost.

Special Features

Some features are available only with this formula. For example:

  • European FRN date cycling

    If the Coupon Dates field on the Pricing tab is set to EURODOLLAR CONVENTION, the system calculates coupon payment dates and rateset dates relative to the issue date of the instrument, and applies the MODIFIED FOLLOWING BUSINESS DAY rule to non-business days.

  • Asset-Backed Securities
  • Overnight Index functionality

Final period pricing

Pricing in the final period for instruments using the FRN formula is defined in the Final Period Pricing section of the Pricing tab on the Money Market or Securities instrument page.
