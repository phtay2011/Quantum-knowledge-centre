Money Market

Use the Money Market Instrument page to create and maintain instruments that can be used to provide defaults and settings for different types of Money Market deals.

A Money Market deal is similar to a Securities deal. They both are financial deals that have an original maturity of less than a year, are generally highly liquid, and are used by participants as a means for borrowing and lending in the short term. But unlike a Securities deal, a Money Market deal:

  • cannot be bought or sold,
  • has no settings related to tradable instruments (for example, an issuer),
  • cannot be callable,
  • cannot have stock flows, but
  • can be generated from, or linked to, a Money Market Deal Request (see below).

Examples are non-tradable loans and deposits, annuities, negotiable certificates of deposit (CDs), bankers acceptances, bonds, commercial paper, municipal notes, and federal funds,

NOTE: Prior to Version 6, Money Market and Securities deals shared both instrument and deal entry user interfaces.

Example

To create a Money Market (MM) that uses spread exclusive

You can create a MM instrument that uses the ISDA-defined Spread Exclusive Compounding method.

  1. Open Instrument > Money Market.
  2. In the Details tab, select Formula Timing = Yield Periodic.
  3. Select the Overnight Index check box.
  4. On the Pricing tab, select a Calculation Method to specify the method Analytics uses to calculate the average rate. Choose from Arithmetic Average and Compounding on Business Day.
  5. Complete the other fields for this instrument.
  6. Save.
