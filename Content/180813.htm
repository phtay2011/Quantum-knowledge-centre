Adding a Cash Transfer

Where you have multiple bank accounts, you may sometimes need to transfer Cash Flows between bank accounts for reconciliation purposes.

This topic covers the two methods for making a Cash Transfer adjustment after a Prior Day or Intraday Matching run.

TIPS:

  • This action uses the standard Quantum Cash Transfer page, with some fields predefined - for example, Entry Date and values relating to the source and target bank accounts.
  • Unless explicitly stated, references below to menus and grids relate to those in the page you initiated the adjustment from.

To create a Cash Transfer for a selected Cash Flow in the current account

This function is not available when Resource Security applies and you are not Exempt or do not have Write access to the Bank Account, OR when you select an External Cash Flow.

  1. On the Matching page's Overview tab, optionally select an account in the Accounts summary and, in the grid menu, select New Transfer.

    TIP: You can also select this function in the Matches grid menu on the Matches tab (the account has already been selected). If

    The Quantum Cash Transfer page is then displayed. If an account was selected in Step 1, the details for that account are displayed.

  2. On the Deal Details tab, specify the necessary data and Save.

    TIP: When Resource Security applies, the second account and other Resources will be filtered based on your allowed access.

If the transfer is successful:

  • a new reversing cash flow is added to your current work area,
  • that reversing cash flow and the original one are marked as matched,
  • a new cash flow for the transferred amount is created in the other account, and
  • the relevant G/L entries are created for the transfer.

To find a Cash Flow in another account and create a Bank Account Transfer for it

  1. Specify search criteria to find the Cash Flow you want.
  2. Select the Cash Flow you want in the results list.

    The Quantum Cash Transfer page is then displayed, showing the details for a transfer of your selected Cash Flow.

    TIP: Because you can transfer only positive amounts, your selected account is shown as either the Source account if the amount is positive, or the Destination account if the amount is negative.

  3. On the Deal Details tab, specify any other necessary data and save.

If the transfer is successful:

  • a new cash flow for the transferred amount is added to your current work area,
  • a new reversing cash flow is created in the other account,
  • that cash flow and the original one are marked as Matched in the other account, and
  • the relevant G/L entries are created for the transfer.
