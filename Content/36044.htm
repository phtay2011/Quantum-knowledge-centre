Futures closeout formula

When two or more Futures contracts are closed out, the system applies the formula:

Closeout Gain/Loss = (Lot Value at Price of Opening Deal - Lot Value at Price of Closing Deal) * Number of Lots Closed Out

When a Cash Settled ETO is exercised, the system applies the formula:

Exercise Gain/Loss = (Premium Value at Contract Price - Premium Value at Exercise Price) * Number of Lots Exercised

Posting of Profit and Loss

  • Futures closeouts

    The gain or loss on closeout is posted to the closing deal (the system uses the relative deal dates to determine this deal).

    If Deal Dates are the same, the deal with the higher deal number is, by default, the closing deal. Where you are able to modify closeout matches, you can change this in the Open/Cls field if necessary.

  • Options closeouts

    Options (ETO) closeouts effectively post no gain or loss.

    If the Premium Type is DEAL DATE, the system posts a premium on the opening deal, and a premium on the closing deal.

    If the Premium Type is CLOSEOUT, the system posts a net premium.

    The net premium on closeout is posted to the closing deal, using the same relationship logic as for Futures closeouts.

  • Options exercises

    Exercise gain/loss is posted to the same General Ledger account codes as Futures gain/loss. The Exercise gain/loss is in addition to any premium income/expense that applies to the deal.

Variation Margin

The system calculates the incremental Variation Margin required. It first calculates the total Variation Margin required for the open lots, then deducts any that has already been posted. To do this, it applies the formula:

Variation Margin = [(Lot Value at Today's Price - Lot Value at Deal Price) * Today's End Of Day Lots] - Previously Posted Variation Margin amounts

NOTE: When a deal is closed out, the Variation Margin relating to the closed deal is not reversed until the Futures Margin Inquiry is run again.

Contingent accounting

If the Apply contingent accounting option on the Accounting Rule is selected (see Futures and Options closeouts or Futures and Options closeouts), the system posts contingent accounting entries for Futures and Options as follows.

Opening deals

The entries establishing the contingent exposure are posted on Deal Date. Reversal entries are posted on the contract Settlement Date.

  • Closing deals

    When a deal is closed, the system deletes the Settlement Date contingent entries on the opening deal. It replaces them with reversal entries for the closed lots (dated the Closeout Date) and reversal entries for the remaining lots (dated the contract Settlement date).

    The amount of the contingent entries depends on the Accounting Rule's contingent method selected - if:

    • Settlement (Futures and Exchange Traded Options) or Settlement Deferred (Exchange Traded Options) - the amount of the contingent entries is the notional settlement value at the deal or strike price, multiplied by the number of lots, or if...
    • Nominal (Futures and Exchange Traded Options) or Nominal deferred (Exchange Traded Options) - the amount is the Face Value (specified in Futures Control), multiplied by the number of lots.
