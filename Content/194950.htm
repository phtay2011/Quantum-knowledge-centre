Commodities in Quantum Six

Quantum Six supports your management of the risk exposure for the commodities you trade, between purchase, production, and sale.

Commodity Swaps

Commodity swaps are financial transaction that allow you to hedge the price of an underlying commodity over a specified period or periods. Commodity swaps enable both producers and consumers to hedge commodity prices. The consumer is usually a fixed payer and the producer a floating payer. Quantum Six enables you to create and maintain two types of Commodity Swap.

  • Fixed for Floating - guarantees a commodity's fixed price over the trading period, in exchange for a commodity's floating price over the same period. The floating price is an average over the period of an underlying commodity like oil, sugar, metals, etc.
  • Floating for Floating - guarantees a commodity's floating price over a specified period, in exchange for a commodity's floating price over a different period. The floating price for each period is an average price of an underlying commodity like oil, sugar, metals, etc. and quite often there is a spread used between the two periods. It is common for the price index (LME, CME, etc.) to be different for each period as well as the price reference (Cash, 1 month, 3 month, etc.) to be different.

A Commodity Swap deal:

  • is a financial deal that moves the risk of price fluctuation from your organization (Entity) to a Counterparty;
  • does not involve the buying or selling of the actual commodity (the physical deal*);
  • locks in a fixed price at an earlier date, eliminating price uncertainty between then and the date the commodity is required; and
  • will, on the future date, have a Settlement Amount of the difference between the actual price then and the locked-in fixed price, multiplied by the agreed quantity.

*NOTE: The Commodity Swap deal does not record or track the delivery of a physical commodity. A physical commodity transaction can be represented notionally in Quantum as a Commodity Exposure for hedging and risk manager reporting.

Commodity Exposures

With Commodity Exposure deals, you can record and report on your organization's exposure to forecasted purchases and sales of commodities. No trading applies, so no cash flows nor General Ledger entries are generated. Quantum Six enables you to create and maintain two types of Commodity Exposure:

  • Fixed - deals with a fixed forward price for the commodity throughout the trading period, or...
  • Floating - deals with a variable future price within the trading period.

Static Data and Rules

New Static Data types have been introduced to support Commodities - namely, Commodity, Measure, and Measure Conversion.

A Transaction Type for Commodity Swaps (CMS) has been added to Rules pages related to Straight-Through Processing, except for the following, as Commodity Swap deals do not generate broker or stock flows:

  • Broker Account Defaulting Rule,
  • Counterparty Stock Account Defaulting Rule, and
  • Stock Account Defaulting Rule

Other rules also apply as for other Transaction Types - for example, Analysis Code Defaulting Rules.

A subset of rules also support Commodity Exposures - namely, rules NOT related to transactions, accounts, and accounting - for example, Counterparty Contact and Limits rules.

Accounting

Accounting and Posting Rules support Commodity Swaps.

History

Previously, commodity trades could only be recorded as FX deals. The Currency static data record was used to register the commodity, which needed to match predefined RiskMetrics codes in Analytics.

TIP: This legacy functionality remains supported for sites previously using it (and for those not licensed for the full Commodities functionality).
