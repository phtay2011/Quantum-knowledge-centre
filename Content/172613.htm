Long First Coupon example (30/360)

This example illustrates how a Long First coupon period is priced for a deal with the following settings:

  • Face Value is EUR 1,000,000
  • Coupon Day Count is 30/360
  • Pays 10% coupon in equal installments
  • Semi-annual equal coupon payments on 30 April and 31 October
  • Issue Date is the same as Dated Date (the date the first coupon period interest starts accruing on new issues)

Calculation of the total period using 30/360 adjustment rules

Normally, Quantum calculates a long odd coupon by breaking it into two periods: the Notional coupon period and the Odd coupon period. It then adds these two together.

However, this method cannot be used for calculating the numerator day count in long odd coupon periods when using the 30/360 Day Count convention. Because of the way its day count rules work, some situations would result in a different numerator day count when the period is broken into two. Instead, the numerator day count is the number of days between the Notional coupon'sStart End dates.

In the above example. the numerator day counts are calculated using the 30/360 formula, as follows:

360*(y2 - y1) = 30*(m2 - m1) + (d2 - d1), where d2 is adjusted to 30 if it is 31 and d1 is 30 or 31

  • Normal full coupon period 31 October to 30 April:

    360*(2005 - 2004) + 30*(4 - 10) + (30 - 30) = 180 days

  • Odd coupon period 27 September 2004 to 30 April 2005: = 213 days

    360*(2005 - 2004) + 30*(4-9) + (30-27) = 213 days

Coupon received on 30 April:

5%*(213/180)*FV = EUR 59,166.67

Accrued income on Deal Date 8 October:

5%*(11/180)*FV = EUR 3,055.56

Accrued income on Deal Date 14 February:

5%*(137/180)*FV = EUR 38,055.56
