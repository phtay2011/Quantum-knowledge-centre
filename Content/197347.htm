Price Method visible on Bond deals

With the transition from Classic Quantum (V5) to the Web version of Quantum (V6), the code moved from a State-full to a Stateless environment. The outcome of this meant that where dependencies existed between multiple fields, for example in triangulation calculations, it became very difficult for the code to "remember" which field was the last one used in a calculation. For example, if a deal was reset and the user selected and entered a Price, when the original deal would have been stamped with a Yield, the deal would be saved with a recalculated Price determined by the Yield and not the Price you entered.

To assist in making the calculations accurate, this strategic enhancement introduces the Price Method field so that the system knows what the you are trying to achieve. For example, you may want to calculate a Yield from the Price.

For the 2019.4 (6.8.2), the bond pricing method will be exposed as the Price Method field when setting up the security instrument and when entering a deal for the Bond Periodic formula.

The behavior is as follows:

  • When you enter the price, the price method is set to PRICE.
  • When you enter the yield, the price method is set to YIELD.
  • When you change the price method from one to the other, the bond is repriced accordingly.
  • When your save the deal, the price method is persisted to database.
  • When you copy an existing deal, the Price Method from the deal is copied.

On the Instrument > Money Market and Instrument > Security pages, the Price Method field:

The Dealing > Money Market and Dealing > Security pages display YIELD and PRICE radio buttons when the selected instrument has Formula Timing set to BOND PERIODIC. The default option is the Price Method setting on the instrument, and you may change it for a deal IF the instrument's Modifiable setting is YES.
