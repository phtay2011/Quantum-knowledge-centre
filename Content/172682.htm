Callable Bond Instruments

In Quantum the term Callable Bonds usually refers to both Callable and Puttable Bonds, unless explicitly indicated otherwise.

Callable Bonds

A Callable Bond is a fixed-rate bond with an embedded call option. The Issuer of the bond has the option to mature the debt early, thereby exercising their option to call in the bond.

When the bond is issued, the issuer effectively purchases a call option from the investor. The option may have more than one possible exercise date (Bermudan style) with varying strike or redemption prices.

A call privilege in Quantum can be exercised:

  • for Bermudan style callable bonds - on any date between the First Exercise Date (or the Settlement Date, if it occurs after the First Exercise Date) and the Last Exercise Date.
  • for European style callable bonds - on any date that is between the Settlement Date and the Exercise Date.

The callable bond holder has sold a call option to the bond issuer that gives the issuer protection against decreases in market interest rates. For example, assume that an issuer sells 10-year bonds with a 10% annual coupon, but with the right to call the issue at par on the coupon payment date in year five, and on every subsequent coupon date prior to maturity. If interest rates have declined to 6%, say, on the first callable date, then the value of the bond will exceed the par value and the issuer's call option will be deep in-the-money. That means that the issuer can effectively retire the expensive debt costing 10% and re-issue bonds at the prevailing rate of 6%.

This right is obviously valuable to the issuer, and the embedded option premium is typically reflected in a higher coupon rate than would otherwise be paid to the holder of a corresponding bond with no call provision. Put another way, if two bonds are issued with the same credit quality and the same coupon rate but where one of the bonds contains a callable feature, then the callable bond will be priced at a higher yield than the equivalent non-callable bond.

Exercise or early redemption of Callable Bonds

Callable Bonds can be exercised or redeemed early on, depending on the exercise style defined:

  • Bermudan
    Where the holder or issuer has the right but not the obligation to exercise the option on any date between the First Exercise Date (or the Settlement Date, if it occurs after the First Exercise Date) and the Last Exercise Date.

    NOTE: When a Bermudan style Callable Bond is exercised on a non-coupon date, the interest is calculated based on the number of days between the previous coupon date and the date the bond is exercised.

  • European
    Where the holder or issuer has the right but not the obligation to exercise the option on any date that is between the Settlement Date and the Exercise Date.

    NOTES:

    • When a European style Callable Bond is exercised on a coupon date, all subsequent coupon payments are removed and the value paid out as the REPAY PRINCIPAL
    • When a European style Callable Bond is exercised on a non-coupon date, the interest is calculated based on the number of days between the previous coupon date and the date the bond is exercised.

Callable Bond deals generate the following cash flows upon exercise:

  • Settlement Amount
  • Coupon Amount
  • Repay Principal

Business days conventions and early exercise

Upon early exercise of the Callable Bond, if the cash flow date generated by system falls on a non-business date, the applicable payment date settings determines which day the cash flow is moved to:

  • If payment date is set to Currency Default, the payment date rule specified for the currency will be applied.
  • If payment date is set to Following business day and cash flow payment falls on a non-business day, the payment date will be moved to the next business day defined for the currency /location.
  • If payment date is set to Modified business date and cash flow payment falls on a non-business day, the payment date will be moved to the next business day; If the next business date occurs in the next calendar months, payment date will be moved back to the first preceding business day.
  • If payment date is set to Preceding business date and cash flow payment falls on a non-business day, the payment date will be moved back to the first preceding business day.

G/L entries and early exercise

When a European or Bermudan style Callable Bond is exercised early, all G/L entries will be adjusted for the new exercise date and any account postings prior to the exercise date will be deleted or reversed.

Restrictions

The following restrictions apply to the Callable Bonds functionality.

  • Exercise dates must occur on coupon dates or notional coupon dates for Bermudan style Callable Bonds. However, not all coupon dates require a corresponding exercise date.

    A notional coupon date is a date that would be a coupon date if the Coupon Frequency was the same as the Exercise Frequency of the embedded option. For example, if Coupon dates are six-monthly, say Jan 5, June 5, Dec 5, then valid possible monthly frequency Exercise dates would be Jan 5, Feb 5, March 5,April 5, May 5, June 5, July 5, and so on.

  • Exercise is available for full Face Value only - that is, partial exercise is not supported.
  • Options on callable/puttable bonds are not available.

Puttable Bonds

A Puttable Bond is similar to a Callable Bond, except it gives the bond holder the right to sell the bond back to the Issuer on a predetermined date, at a predetermined price.

The holder of a puttable bond has purchased a put option on the bond as well as the bond itself. Because the put option increases the value of the bond to the holder, bonds with put features provide lower yields than bonds with no put features.

Lockout Period

A lock-out period may apply during which time the bond may not be called. This can vary from a month to many years. A bond with more than one call date has a Bermudan style embedded option, and redemption or strike prices may vary on the different call dates.
