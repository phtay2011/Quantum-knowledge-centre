Brazilian Market Functionality

Although YIELD - CDI is used to calculate interest earned for business days only, it is also used to define and value Brazilian Yield Compounding Instruments, or TJLPs (Taxa de Juros de Longo Prazo).

Although TJLPs are not CDI trades, the interest calculation formula is the same. However, where the CDI is usually based on the business days convention ACTUAL/252BD, TJLP trades use the ACTUAL/360 convention. These conventions differ as follows:

  • ACTUAL/360

    The actual number of days in the calculation period divided by 360. It is assumed that all years have 360 days. When using ACTUAL/360, one year' interest (evaluated as 365/360) is higher than the amount of interest calculated when the annual interest rate is calculated disregarding days.

  • ACTUAL/252BD

    This is used to calculate only business days with the YIELD - CDI formula. No interest is earned over holidays or weekends. This is virtually the same as the ACTUAL 360/BD convention, however the denominator is 252 (days) instead of 360. Simple and compounding interest is calculated on a business days /252 basis.
