Valuation

Use the Valuation Rule page to create, view, and maintain Analytics Pricing valuation rules.

About the rules

Each rule record is identified by a unique Code value, has a descriptive Name, and:

  • applies to a specific combination of common settings - namely Entity (a single entity or All) and Base Currency (a single currency only), and
  • specifies, for each type of deal or Pricing (on separate tabs*):
    • the specific Instrument, Entity, Counterparty, Currency (or Currency Pair, for two-sided deal types*), and any other deal-type-specific parameter values for selecting deals the valuation rules apply to, and
    • the relevant rate names, (Discount) Zero Curve, (Discount) Margin Rates and Types, accrual methods, revaluation methods, identifiers, and other settings to be used in valuations for that deal type, as applicable.

NOTE: Two-sided deal types have two tabs: one for basic valuation and a Pair tab to enable additional rules for specific Currency Pairs.

Accurately capture the different risk levels

To accurately capture the different risk levels associated with different counterparties, add a series of credit spreads to the discounting curves applied in deal valuations. These spread curves (Yield/Deal Margins) can be generated at the level of counterparty and/or counterparty group.

  1. Open the Rules > Valuation Rules page.
  2. Open the rule you are interested in.
  3. Open the Matching Criteria dialog box by adding a new rule or editing an existing rule.

The order of the fields in the Matching Criteria from v6.8.1 is:

  • Instrument
  • Entity
  • Counterparty (excludes Futures and Future Options)
  • Currency (excluding CCS Pair, CO Pair, FX Pair, Futures, Futures Option)
  • Currency Pair (CCS, CCS Pair, CO, CO Pair, FX, FX Pair only)
  • Pay/Receive (IRS, CCS only)
  • Payment Frequency (MM, Security, CCS, IRS, Cash Flow only)
  • Rateset Frequency (MM, Security, Cap/Floor, CCS, IRS only) currency, or pay/receive setting. But this will not take precedence over a rule configured with both entity and currency.

The system matches a deal to each rule. The system applies these matching criteria in the above order, but with additional criteria:

  • A match to multiple criteria has further priority over a single match or a match to a lower number of criteria.
  • The system applies a diminishing priority for group structures. For example, where you have a transaction-level instrument as the only matching criteria in a rule, this criteria takes precedence over a rule configured with either the instrument group, the deal entity, currency, or pay/receive setting. But this will not take precedence over a rule configured with both entity and currency.
  • We enter counterparties into the system in a similar way to entities. A transaction-level counterparty is assigned to a deal, but may also be part of a group structure.

Curves for FRN / MM revaluation

From v2019.4 (v6.8.2) there are separate projecting and discounting curves for valuing FRN and Money Market deals. This applies to valuations from the Pricing pages, Deal Analyze, and all queries.
