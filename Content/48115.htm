Book Value, FX Deals with Imported Market Values

If the revaluation method for an FX Deal is Imported or Imported Separate, the accrual of forward points determines how Quantum calculates the book value for the deal.

The revaluation currency determines the currency used for the revaluation calculations.

Revaluation method = Imported

If the Accrue forward points option in Accounting Rules is:

  • Selected - the Book value is equal to the amount of the leg that is in the other (traded) currency, converted to the revaluation currency at the dealt Spot rate.
  • Cleared - the Book value is equal to the amount of the leg that is in the other (traded) currency, converted to the revaluation currency at the dealt Contract rate.

    As there are no forward points, this is the same as the amount of the leg that is in the revaluation currency.

Revaluation method = Imported Separate

Quantum:

  • Automatically sets the Revaluation currency to Separate
  • Makes the Accrue forward points setting unavailable in Accounting Rules
  • Calculates a separate book value for each leg of the deal

    This is the amount of that leg, net of its last revaluation amount.
