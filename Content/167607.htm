Money Market

Use the Money Market Deal page to record and/or to edit Money Market transactions, including simple interest borrowings and investments. These transactions cover all physical deals, whether they are domestic or foreign currency, simple or structured flows.

The fields on the page vary, depending on the formula and timing of the Instrument you select, and on other key settings.

NOTE: Security deals were previously included in the Money Market deals user interface in classic Quantum.

External References

External Reference numbers and text appear as read-only values on the Deal Details tab, but only when:

  • you imported or input the deal data via an API, and
  • the system populated the External Reference fields or properties.

Deal Allocation

The Deal Allocation process is optional for Quantum Money Market deals, and enables you to link a master deal (the Source deal) to one or more automatically-created additional deals of a similar type.

See Deal Allocation for Money Market and Security Deals.

Closeouts

When you input a Buy deal and a Sell deal based on the same tradeable instrument, closeout can occur. To determine which deals are to be closed out, Quantum matches deals using the Closeout Method specified on the underlying Money Market instrument.

See Money Market and Securities Closeout Methods.

Hedge Relationship Deals

To add and maintain any of the deals that are in a Hedge Relationship, use the Linked Deals tab on the Hedge Relationship page.

The Dealing page shows a Hedge Relationships tab if the current deal is a Linked Deal within one or more Hedge Relationships. If so, you can quickly access the Hedge Relationship from this tab by clicking on the HR Deal Number in the Allocations grid.

Filling Money Market Requests

The system can automatically create Money Market deals from Money Market Requests, using the Fill action. See Filling and Resetting Money Market Requests.

Range Accrual Deals

A Range Accrual deal is based on a Money Market Instrument with:

  • Formula Timing set to YIELD PERIODIC,
  • the FLOATING option selected, and
  • the Range Accrual option selected.

For an overview, see the Range Accruals release note topic.

For Rate Setting differences versus other Money Market deals, see Rate Setting for Range Accruals.

Overnight Index Deals

In general, an overnight index deal is based on either:

  • a deal with YIELD PERIODIC as the formula timing and the Overnight Index checkbox enabled, or
  • a swap instrument with OVERNIGHT INDEX PERIODIC as the formula timing.

For example, the Money Market deal with Formula Timing = YIELD PERIODIC and the Overnight Index checkbox enabled.

You can perform most of the usual functions on these deals. Examples include:

  • manually setting the rate on these deals using the Rate Set menu item and using the deal map to see the interest calculated,
  • customizing the deal. The accounting method for customized deals persists, for both major and minor customizations.

Yield - CDI Periodic Deals

For Rate Convention = DAILY and Annual Margin is set to Base Rate or Margin, when you save the deal, as Quantum generates the Rate and Rateset items it calculates the effective base rate and the effective margin as the daily value and not an annualized value.

Deals convert rates to daily rates in certain circumstances. When:

  • Rate Convention = DAILY, and
  • Formula Timing = YIELD - CDI PERIODIC, and
  • Annual Margin is set to Base Rate or Margin,

we calculate:

  • Effective Base as a percentage, based off the day count convention used, either ACTUAL\360, ACTUAL\252BD, and
  • Effective Margin as a percentage, based off the day count convention used, either ACTUAL\360, ACTUAL\252BD.

In other words, when you save a deal, as Quantum generates the Rate and Rateset items it calculates the effective base rate and the effective margin.as the daily value instead of a annualized value.
