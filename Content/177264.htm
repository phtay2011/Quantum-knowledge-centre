Hedge Relationships and Accounting Rules

A Hedge Relationship - in total - does not require accounting settings.

However, deals that are part of a Hedge Relationship are expected to be revalued using their normal Accounting Rule settings. The only exceptions are Projected Cash Flows, which have no Accounting Rule settings, and rely instead on settings on the Linked Deals tab of the Hedge Relationship.

To satisfy FASB requirements, all transaction types are required to:

  • enforce a market valuation method, and
  • identify which rate curve will provide the data necessary for the revaluations.

To compute effectiveness, revaluations must be calculated for each deal in a Hedge Relationship even if that Transaction Type would not normally be revalued.

Reapplying Cashflows and G/L Entries

Settings on the Reapply Cash Flows and Accounting Utility page enable the system to apply changed Accounting Rules to open deals. This is useful when Hedge Accounting is first introduced. For example, FX deals previously accounted for using Spot revaluations and accruing forward points must be revalued at Fair Value, probably using Forward or Forward NPV.
