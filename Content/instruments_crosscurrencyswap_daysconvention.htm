Days Convention area

Use this area to enter the default Day Count settings. for this leg of Swap deals based on the selected Instrument.

TIPS:

  • Day counts include the start date but exclude the end date.
  • In each option in a Day Count drop-down list, the term before the slash indicates how the number of days in a period are calculated, and the term after the slash indicates how the number of days in the year are calculated.
  • For details of the options, see Day Count Conventions.

For all Formula Timings exceptBOND PERIODIC:

  • Day Count - select a Day Count Convention to determine the basis for calculating and accruing interest.

For the BOND PERIODIC Formula Timing only:

TIP: For most Bonds, the three Day Counts here will be identical for a Swap leg; however, Canadian and South African Government Bonds use different day counts for each of these fields.

  • Income Day Count - the basis for the calculation of accrued income and coupons for settlement; indicates how days and year-days (the number of days per annum) will be handled.
  • Dirty Price Day Count - the convention used to generate discount factors to NPV future cash flows.
  • Coupon Day Count - the basis for the calculation of coupon amounts to be settled and potentially used in the Yield calculations.
  • Use dirty price day count (applies only when Income Day Count and Dirty Price Day Count differ).

    TIP: The Coupon Day Count setting is used for the coupon calculations, regardless of this setting.

    • Select this option to use the Dirty Price Day Count setting to calculate the Accrued Income for figuration purposes (Not Settled) in calculating the Yield. This methodology is used for Canadian Govt. Bonds.
    • Clear this option to use the Income Day Count setting to calculate the Accrued Income, Stub, and/or Exact Coupons for figuration in the Yield and Settlement calculations; AND to use the Dirty Price Days Convention setting to generate discount factors to NPV future cash flows for the Yield calculation. This methodology is used for South African Government Bonds.
