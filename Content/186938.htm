General Ledger Journals with no Cash Flows

You can now enter a G/L Journal against a bank account where no cash flows are involved - for example, to "true up" the General Ledger Bank Account balance, or to reclassify the balance from an asset account to a liability account.

NOTE: The ability to suppress cash flows is available for Standalone and In deal set Journal Types only.

Implementation

On the G/L Journal page, the new Suppress Cash Flows option now appears, as highlighted in the example below.

Use of the option is subject to the new specific Module Security rights for G/L Journal (Suppress Cash Flows).

When Suppress Cash Flows is selected for a G/L Journal, and an entry is created where the G/L account is for a Bank Account:

  • the Bank Account field is hidden on the New Entry and Edit Entry dialog boxes,
  • you can enter multiple Bank Account-only entries in one journal, and
  • no cash flows will be generated for any Bank Account entries (so none are shown on the Cash Flows tab).

If you add any journal entries to the G/L Journal:

  • the Suppress Cash Flows option becomes disabled, and
  • the journal entries must be cleared to change the setting.

Restrictions and Limitations

The Suppress Cash Flows option is NOT applicable when Journal Type is Linked to deal.
