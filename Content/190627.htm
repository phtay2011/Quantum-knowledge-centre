IRR (Point in Time Column Formulas)

(Position or Cash Flow queries only.)

For instruments valued off a Zero Coupon Curve, the Newton-Raphson method is used to determine the IRR, which is an iterative approach to solving for a single Yield where the NPV of the cash flows of a transaction is equal to the Market Value.

An annual IRR is calculated and converted to semi-annual or quarterly, as appropriate.

NOTES:

  • 360-day rates are first converted to a 365-day rate for consistency. For example, a 6% 360-day rate converts to a 6.0833% 365-day rate (6 * 365/360).
  • The IRR for instruments valued at a Market Rate - that is, directly off a Par Rate Curve (for example Bonds) - is determined directly from the Par Rate Curve.
  • The IRR for Foreign Exchange transactions (single cash flow transactions) is determined directly from the Zero Coupon Curve.
  • The IRR for Futures transactions is determined directly from the Market Rates table.
  • The IRR for Futures Option (ETO) transactions is determined directly from the Market Rates table for the underlying Futures contract.
  • The frequency of all these rates is converted as appropriate.
