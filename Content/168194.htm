Deal Entry and WAC Processing

This topic gives a brief outline of the how the Weighted Average Closeout (WAC) Processor processes the portfolio deals for the following scenarios.

TIP: For definitions of the acronyms and terms used here, see WAC Terminology.

New Deal to New Position

In this scenario the assumption is that there is no previous balance in the True Transactional Entity (TTE) for the bond stock in the deal.

The following workflow applies to this scenario.

  1. The new deal is entered into the TTE.

    All general ledger entries will be netted into the general G/L account (both Balance Sheet and P/L) except for postings to the Bank Account and its Currency Translation account.

  2. The WAC Processor program is then run to
    • Replicate the original bond deal in the Notional Transactional Entity (for example Z-TE) using the Notional Counterparty (for example Z-WAC).
    • Settle into the Notional Bank Account - this Bank Account and its Currency Translation will be pointed to the general G/L account.
    • Post other general ledger entries to their true G/L accounts.

New Deal Increasing the Current Position

In this scenario the assumption is that there is a pre-existing position (short or long) in the True Transactional Entity (TTE) for the bond stock in this deal. This new deal increases the current position. The following workflow applies to this scenario.

  1. The new deal is entered into the TTE.

    All general ledger entries will be netted into the general G/L account (both Balance Sheet and P/L) except for postings to the Bank Account and its Currency Translation account.

  2. The WAC Processor is then run to:
    • Replicate an equal and opposing deal (Z-WACC deal) to the current Weighted Average position - where the current Weighted Average position is recorded in Notional Transactional Entity (for example Z-TE) as a single deal with the Notional Counterparty (for example Z-WAC). This replicated deal:
      • is with the Notional Closeout Counterparty (Z-WACC)
      • is in the Z-TE
      • fully closes out the Z-WAC position
      • settles into the Notional Bank Account - this Bank Account and its Currency Translation will be pointed to the general G/L account.
      • posts its other general ledger entries to their true G/L accounts
      • is struck at a price/yield giving a nil gain/loss on closeout - this price/yield will be determined by the accrual method set on the Z-WAC
    • Create the new Weighted Average position. This deal:
      • is with the Z-WAC> counterparty
      • is in the Z-TE
      • settles into the Notional Bank Account - this Bank Account and its Currency Translation will be pointed to the general G/L account
      • posts its other general ledger entries to their true G/L accounts
      • is struck at a price/yield that gives a Capital (FV +/- Premium/Discount) value representing the unamortized value of the (now closed out) Z-WAC position plus the unamortized value of the additional new deal

New Deal Reducing/Reversing a Current Position

In this scenario the assumption is that there is a pre-existing position (short or long) in the True Transactional Entity (TTE) for the bond stock in this deal. This new trade decreases the current position resulting in a full closeout, partial closeout, or position reversal (short to long, or long to short). The following workflow applies to this scenario.

  1. The original deal is entered into the TTE.

    All general ledger entries will be netted into the general G/L account (both Balance Sheet and Profit and Loss) except for postings to the Bank Account and its Currency Translation account.

  2. The WAC Processor is then run to replicate the new deal into Quantum. This replicated deal:
    • is with the Z-WAC counterparty
    • is in the Z-TE
    • closes out against the Z-WAC position - depending on the size of the new deal, this will result in any of a partial closeout or a full closeout against the current Z-WAC position, or a new reversed Z-WAC position
    • realizes the gain/loss

      The Realized gain/loss on closeout is calculated using the Capital Value of the new deal and the unamortised capital of the Z-WAC deal being closed out.

      The remaining position is valued as the unamortised capital portion of the open Z-WAC deal as per the standard Quantum closeout method functionality.

    • settles into the Notional Bank Account - this Bank Account and its Currency Translation will be pointed to the general G/L account
    • posts its other general ledger entries to their true G/L accounts

Order of Processing Deals

You can select the order in which deals are processed when the Weighted Average Closeout (WAC) Processor is run as follows:

  • Buy before sell (default) - Select this option to process deals grouped by Buy first and then by Sell. When this option is selected, deals are processed in the following order:
    • Settlement Date
    • Buy
    • Sell
    • Deal Number
  • Chronological - Select this option to process all deals sequentially. When this option is selected, deals are processed in the following order:
    • Settlement Date
    • Input date time
    • Deal Number
