About Cash Concentration and Thresholds

(Applies to transaction-level Bank, Broker, or Money Market Fund accounts.)

Cash concentration:

  • is the amalgamation of funds from several accounts into one or more control accounts,
  • is used to sweep surpluses and fund deficits, using centralized bank accounts where the funds can be more effectively managed,
  • simplifies cash management,
  • enables economies of scale, and
  • helps to avoid short-term borrowing and interest payments.

You can specify Cash Concentration settings for a Bank, Broker, or Money Market Fund account on the Worksheet Settings tab of the applicable account page.

IMPORTANT: These settings apply only to the CashXplorer worksheet, and the calculated concentration flows are only simulated flows for the worksheet. The real concentration flows may be created in one of the following ways:

  • by using CashXplorer's Concentrate Cash function,
  • automatically as Zero Balancing Bank Account (ZBA) Transfers by the Auto Matching facility of Bank Reconciliation, to match imported ZBA flows (where the bank has reported these and explicitly identified them), or
  • manually entered as Cash Transfer deals.

Each cash concentration creates two cash flows: one for the source account (where the funds will come from) and one for the destination account (where the funds will be moved to). Since a worksheet may not include all accounts, the worksheets may not show all of the concentration flows. For cash concentration to be calculated fully, all accounts involved must be included in the Data Version.

  • The cash concentration account must itself have no cash concentration or a Processing Phase that is higher than the account you are setting up (where 1 is the lowest processing phase, 5 the highest).
  • If the currency of the cash concentration account is different from that of the source account, the generated concentration flow is translated into the target account currency. This is done using the worksheet's Translation Rate Type or, if no translation is requested, the default rate type for the Worksheet.

    In CashXplorer, you can view, but not action, these simulated concentration flows on the Cash Concentration Transfers dialog box (Cross Currency tab).

About Threshold Amounts

A Threshold Amount is the amount an account balance can reach before the funds are transferred to or from the concentration account. Threshold amounts help to minimize the number of cash transfers and their associated costs.

If a threshold amount is set, cash concentration will occur based on this amount. If no threshold amount is set, or if it is zero, cash concentration will occur based on the Target Balance. Over time you can monitor the average balance that results from this process and make any necessary adjustments.

Use the Threshold Amount fields (also in the Cash Concentration section of the Worksheet Settings tab) to enter the value/s to enable threshold concentration.

TIP: These amounts are always absolute values.

When the difference between the balance and the target balance allows, you can specify that a surplus amount is moved out of the account, or a deficit amount is funded, or both. You can also specify any rounding to be applied in each case, as follows:

  • set up up the rounding defaults for the Bank Account (in the Cash Concentration section of the Worksheet Settings tab), or
  • set Cash Concentration rounding ONin Cash Management Worksheet, Additional Settings.
