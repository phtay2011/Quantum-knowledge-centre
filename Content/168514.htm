Beneficiary Details tab (Settlement; Counterparty Instructions)

Use this tab to view and optionally enter or edit details of the beneficiary (if allowed). The beneficiary is the individual or entity to whom the payment will be made, and is specified in the Beneficiary Name field in the Header section.

See also: Changing Counterparty Instructions.

The Beneficiary Details tab contains the following fields.

Postal Address

(Read-only)

The postal address of the beneficiary, from their Counterparty record.

Comments

(Read-only)

Optional free-format text from the beneficiary's Counterparty record.

Delivery Narrative

Optional free-format text; defaults to that on the beneficiary's Counterparty record.

If required (and allowed), add or edit the delivery instructions for the cash flow.

The text is free-format, but you should restrict it to codes that are acceptable to the applicable banking system. You may enter multiple lines, and any continuation lines must start with two slashes (//). The output is restricted to four lines of up to 35 characters each, with any longer lines truncated.

This information is used in Field 70 of MT101, MT103, or MT103+ SWIFT message formats. Refer to the SWIFT documentation for information about allowable code-words and formats for this field.

See also: Supported instruction codes.

Bank to Bank Instructions

Optional free-format text; defaults to that on the beneficiary's Counterparty record. This field enables you to record a request for the beneficiary's bank to act on receipt of the EFT payment (for example, Please phone or telex the beneficiary by 10:00am).

If required (and allowed), add or edit the text for the cash flow.

This field is free-format, but you should restrict the text to codes that are acceptable to the applicable banking system. You may enter multiple lines, and any continuation lines must start with two slashes (//). The output is restricted to four lines of up to 35 characters each, with any longer lines truncated, and any invalid codes ignored.

This information is output Tag72 of applicable SWIFT messages (for example, MT101/103/103+/202 payment messages). For information about allowable code-words and formats for this field, refer to the SWIFT documentation.

The use of dynamically updating variable values to override this text in SWIFT output has been implemented in conjunction with some Payment Methods. Contact your FIS Consultant for more details.

See also: Supported instruction codes.

Payment Category

This setting relates to EFT output and is defaulted from the Counterparty account record. Select Standard Settlement Instructions or EFT Not Required to specify whether or not to pass the cash flow on for EFT Processing, respectively.

TIP: If you change this setting and want your change to apply to ALL existing cash flows for this Counterparty, run Bank/Counterparty Delivery Instruction Conversion with the Refresh counterparty Instructions option selected.

Overseas Bank

This option affects only international (SI and UI) NatWest Payment Manager EFT export files.

Its setting governs the Payment Category code used for overseas transactions for the selected counterparty (when output via NatWest Payment Manager).

  • If this option is selected, transactions for this counterparty will carry a Payment Category of A, indicating that they are payments to an overseas bank.
  • If cleared, transactions for this counterparty will carry a Payment Category of O, indicating that they are non-Sterling payments to another UK bank.

Charges

These settings define the amount and currency of bank charges, for both the Sender (the Entity) and Receiver (the bank holding the Entity's cash account). These values default to the Charges information entered on the Payment Details tab of the Bank Account record.

  • In Account mode, you cannot change the default values in these fields.
  • In Adhoc Instruction or Third Party mode, use these fields to enter new details as required.

The Charges information is utilized when:

  • an EFT payment is made to a beneficiary that is not a Financial Institution, and
  • the payment method is SWIFT or SWIFT-compatible - that is, one that outputs SWIFT MT101, MT103, or MT103+ Payment messages.

When you action a payment of this type that has Charges applied (and provided you have sufficient Module Security rights), a Bank Account Adjustment is automatically created when the payment is output, to reflect the Charges entries. For more details, see Details of Charges below and Account Adjustments.

Charges do not have to be in the same currency as that of the payment. However, the system-generated Bank Account Adjustment for the Charges will be in the same currency as the payment and, where required, the amount is converted using the currency Translation Rate Type specified for the Bank Account (on the Payment Details tab).

Details of Charges

Use this field to specify how charges are to be allocated for a particular EFT payment using SWIFT or a SWIFT-like Payment Method. The default setting is that specified for the Counterparty Bank Account.

Select one of the following (where valid for the relevant Payment Method):

NONE

Excludes Field 71A from EFT export files created for this Counterparty (not valid in many cases - for example, for SWIFT-style Payment Methods when Payment Category is Standard Settlement Instructions

BEN

All transaction charges are to be borne by the Beneficiary customer (see Note below)

BEN;BEN

Same as BEN (converted to BEN for SWIFT output; see Note below)

BEN;OUR

For SWIFT output: same as SHA (converted to SHA)

In other cases, opposite to SHA: charges on the Sender's side are to be borne by the beneficiary customer; those on the Receiver's side by the ordering customer

CLM

Charges are for the account of the bank claiming reimbursement

OUR

Charges are to be borne by the ordering customer (see Note below)

OUR;BEN

Same as SHA (converted to SHA for SWIFT output)

OUR;OUR

Same as OUR (converted to OUR for SWIFT output)

SHA

Shared - transaction charges on the Sender's side are to be borne by the ordering customer; those on the Receiver's side by the beneficiary customer

NOTE: When Detail of Charges is changed, it is considered a Modified SSI, even if it is subsequently changed back to its original value.

For more information, see MT101/103/103+ Counterparty Mapping.

  • Where the EFT Processing page is used to output SWIFT Payment messages (and provided you have sufficient Module Security rights*), a Bank Account Adjustment will be automatically created (by the EFT Payments Interface program) when the payment is output, to reflect the charges applied (BEN or OUR only).
  • The required Module Security rights are Add and Bank Mode for Bank Account Adjustments. If you do not have these, a Security Access message is written to the Message Log.

Sender's Charges

Select a Currency and enter the amount of the sender's charges.

NOTE: The amount is finalized when payment occurs, not at the time of deal entry.

Receiver's Charges

Select a Currency and enter the amount of the receiver's charges.

NOTE: The amount is finalized when payment occurs, not at the time of deal entry.

Charges comments

Use this free-format text box to enter and record optional information regarding anything that may affect the actual amount of a charge applied to a payment.

Charges can differ from bank to bank, depending on the circumstances - for example:

  • whether or not the payment leaves the bank, and/or
  • in EURO currency countries, if the amount is paid to another country.
