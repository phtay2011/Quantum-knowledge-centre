Deal Date Accounting Enhancement

(First introduced in Release 6.4.1 SP, March 2017.)

Deal Date (Trade Date) Accounting has been extensively changed to more closely fit with common practise.

Deal Date Accounting applies only to Money Market or Security deals whose Accounting Rule has the Balance Sheet Timing option set to DEAL DATE, and the main changes for such deals are summarized below.

Position Date is not stepped forward

When revaluing a deal using Deal Date accounting, the Position Date is no longer stepped forward by the number of business days between Deal Date and Settlement Date, and the valuation now reflects the current Position Date.

Capital Amortization is not deferred

Capital Amortizations are now posted directly from the Capital Accrual Account to P&L, and the additional revaluation postings* that transferred an amount of capital to the Deferred Capital Account no longer occur. Accruals therefore now post against the Accrual account not the Deferred account.

*TIP: These additional postings started from Deal Date and finished a set number of days before Maturity Date (calculated using Settlement Date - Trade Date).

Deal Position is reduced on Deal Date of Closeout

(Applies to Bond-style deals supporting Closeout.)

In Deal Revaluation, Position Reporting, and Position Processing, deal positions now show as reduced from the Deal Date, indicating that the position is open to further Market Value movement, and preventing double-counting of the Gain/Loss. Previously, the stepping forward of the Position Date artificially reduced the remaining position on closeout in the report results.

Now:

  • Capital Accrual reflects closed out deals from Deal Date.
  • Coupon Accrual reflects closed out deals from Deal Date.
  • Revaluation End Date is recorded as Deal Date for fully closed deals.
  • Book value between Deal Date and Settlement Date equals the Settlement amount.
  • When a deal is closed out between Deal Date and Settlement Date:
    • the Gain/Loss calculation is consistent with that calculated using Settlement Date Accounting, and
    • the Gain/Loss is booked against the Deal Date of the closing deal.
  • For a deal that has been partially closed out:
    • the outstanding principal balance and cash flows (of the deal being closed) are reduced from the Deal Date of the trade that closed it out,
    • the Book value of the deal is proportionately reduced by the Face value of the closing deal(s), and
    • the Revaluation Gain/Loss is calculated against these reduced balances.

    For a deal that has been fully closed out:

    • there will be no revaluations on or after the Deal Date of the trade that closed it out.

Revaluation is consistent for Market Cum / Market Ex Methods

(These methods apply to Bond-style Security deals only.)

When performing a Market Cum or Market Ex valuation, the Market Value and Book Value can now be reconciled to the General Ledger book balance, to ensure consistency between your ledger and Position reports.

In the period before Settlement Date:

  • the Market Value is calculated as of Settlement Date,
  • the Settlement cash flow is excluded from the Market Value calculation,
  • for consistency, the NPV and Duration Analysis also exclude the initial Settlement cash flow,
  • the Book Value is set equal to the Settlement Amount,
  • the Gain/Loss is calculated as the difference between the Market Value and Book Value,
  • the Gain/Loss is NPV-ed from Settlement Date to Position Date.

    TIP: Settlement Date Accounting NPV's the Market Value (on the assumption the Book value equals zero).

In the period between Deal Date and Settlement Date:

  • the Market Value is calculated as of Position Date and
  • the Book Value is also calculated as of Position Date.

In the period after Settlement Date, the revaluation is the same as for Settlement Date Accounting (previously this was not the case).

Revaluation is consistent for Projected and Bills Revaluation Methods

When performing a Projected, Projected Net, Bills, or Bills Net valuation, the Market Value and Book Value can now be reconciled to the General Ledger book balance, to ensure consistency between your ledger and Position reports.

In the period before Settlement Date:

  • the Market Value is calculated as of Position Date,
  • the Settlement cash flow is excluded from the Market Value calculation,
  • the Book Value is set equal to the Settlement Amount,
  • the Gain/Loss is calculated as the difference between the Market Value and Book Value,
  • the Gain/Loss is NPV-ed from Settlement Date to Position Date.

In the period after Settlement Date:

  • the Market Value is calculated as of Position Date, and
  • the Book Value is also calculated as of Position Date.

History

Previous implementation of Deal Date Accounting in Quantum was based on specific client requirements that do not apply to the majority of sites requesting this functionality.
