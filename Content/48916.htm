Swap section: Compute remainder of Swaps-based Zero rates

The remainder of the six-monthly zero rates are computed using a quadratic bootstrap procedure.

  • If a futures strip is used, the first rate derived from this procedure has a maturity of 2.5 years.
  • If no futures strip is used, the first rate has a maturity of 1.5 years.

Example

This part of the example will concentrate on construction of the AUD swap curve, which at this stage already consists of four rates that have been interpolated from the combined cash and futures curve. That is, we have already calculated the zero rates for maturities of 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, and 2.0 years.

The first two zero rates given by the bootstrapping procedure are therefore those with maturities of 2.5 and 3.0 years.
