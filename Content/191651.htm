Bank Accounts (Adaptiv)

Use the Adaptiv Bank Account page to view and maintain individual bank account records and groups in the Analytics database.

This data is shared between Analytics and Adaptiv perspectives, so that changes you save in Adaptiv also show in Analytics and vice versa. However, the Adaptiv page contains an additional group of Adaptiv-specific optional settings for Collateral Accounts that have no relevance (and therefore do not show) in the Analytics perspective.

You must set up Collateral Accounts for netting and collateralization required by Adaptiv Analytics (AA). The amount posted on the collateral account will be mapped to the Opening_Balance field in AA.

To define a new collateral account

  1. On the Adaptiv Bank Account page, click New.
  2. Enter the account Code, Name, and Account Number.

    TIP: Include Collateral or Coll in the account name for easier recognition.

  3. Select the Bank, Currency, and G/L Owner for the account.
  4. Select the Netting-Collateral Pool option.

    • The new check-box Master Agreement is now available. With Master Agreement we define the link between the Bank Account and a Margin Pool defined in the Static Data file used to run reports such as an SA-CCR. All the settings required for netting are in the Static Data file. The Margin Pool field is the combination of: Master Agreement ID, Netting Pool ID, Variation Margin Pool ID, separated by period (.).

  5. Additional fields for the Credit Support Amounts are then available.

  6. Select the Independent Amount Reference: Bank or Counterparty.
  7. Enter the following amounts for collateralization:
    • Independent Amount,
    • Received Threshold,
    • Posted Threshold,
    • Minimum Received, and
    • Minimum Posted.
  8. Enter the Collateral Call Frequency Term (number of time units) and select the time units in Collateral Call Frequency Term Type (Days, Weeks, Months, or Years).
  9. Click Save.

Bank Account Grouping

All Bank Account records, including Groups, are shared between Analytics and Adaptiv perspectives, so the procedures and their results are identical in either perspective.

While you may prefer to group your Collateral accounts separately, any account may be in any group (or in none).
