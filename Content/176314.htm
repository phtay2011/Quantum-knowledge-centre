OCI calculation rules

OCI applies only to CASH FLOW hedge types. There is no concept of OCI for a Fair Value hedge.

Other Comprehensive Income (OCI) is an accounting presentation term for a Balance Sheet account. The account is in the Shareholder Equity section of the Balance Sheet. If a Cash Flow hedge is effective, FAS 133 allows you to defer the effective portion of the Mark-to-Market posting from Profit and Loss to OCI. When the amounts are transferred back to the Profit and Loss, the net effect on the Balance Sheet accounts is nil.

For any given hedge relationship, the balance in OCI at any time is always the lesser of:

  • the cumulative profit/loss on the exposure, and
  • the cumulative profit/loss on the hedge.

For example, if the total gain on Hedge deals linked to the hedge relationship is $100 and the total loss on linked Exposure deals is $120, the OCI balance is $100 - the lower amount.

Under-effective hedge relationships

Where the level of effectiveness achieved by a hedge relationship is below the percentage specified, the Hedge deal is under-effective relative to the Exposure. The OCI balance then simply equals the full gain or loss experienced by the Hedge deal.

For example, if linked Exposure deals lose $300 and linked Hedge deals gain only $160, the OCI balance for that hedge relationship is $160.

TIP: For the required level of effectiveness settings, see the Hedge Relationship page's Effectiveness tab.

Over-effective hedge relationships

Where the level of effectiveness achieved by the hedge relationship is greater than the percentage specified, the Hedge deal is over-effective relative to the Exposure. The OCI balance then equals the full gain or loss experienced by the Exposure, with the remainder of the Fair Value of the Hedge being passed directly to earnings (P&L).

For example, if linked Exposure deals lose $50 and linked Hedge deals gain $350, the OCI balance is $50, and $300 goes straight to earnings.

NOTE: You can never defer to OCI more than the change in the value of the Exposure.

Direction (sign) of the OCI balance amount

The correct OCI balance is maintained by successive incremental postings. The direction of the postings is determined by whether the cumulative P&L on the Hedge deal is a gain or loss, as follows:

  • if a gain (credit accounting direction) - the OCI balance is a Credit or liability, or...
  • if a loss (debit accounting direction) - the OCI balance is a Debit or asset.

Postings to OCI are performed by Position Inquiry. The system moves the calculated amounts to OCI, and ensures that the correct amounts remain in earnings.
