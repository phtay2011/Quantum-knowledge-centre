Valuing European Bond Options

This section provides a detailed description of the Bond Option valuation procedure. An example of both a Put and Call option is included each of which is written on the same underlying bond. The process comprises the following steps.

The associated calculation involves a number of steps:

  • Determining Underlying Bond Settlement Value

  • Determining discount factor for period (between bond settlement and option maturity)
  • Calculating Forward Bond Price

2. Determining the Bond Strike Price

The underlying bond Strike price is determined by valuing the bond as if a trade occurs on the maturity date of the option, at a yield equal to the strike Yield. The bond is valued in exactly the same manner as was used to determine the settlement value, but using the dates and remaining cashflows as they will exist at the option maturity date.

However, if the bond is purchased on the option maturity date, then settlement will not occur for the specified number of business days after that date.
