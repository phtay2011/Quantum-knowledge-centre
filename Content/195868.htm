Commodities: Hedge Accounting support

CMS = Commodity Swap; CE = Commodity Exposure.

The Hedge Accounting module has been extended to include support for Hedge Accounting of Commodity Swaps.

On the Hedge Strategy and Hedge Relationship pages, you can now select a Designated Risk value of Commodity Price Risk.

This setting enables you to link Commodity Swaps, Commodity Exposures, and hypothetical Commodity Swaps into the Hedge Relationship.

When you link a Commodity Swap or Commodity Exposure into a Hedge Relationship, the additional fields Commodity, Quantity Hedged, Measure, and Price Fix Date are displayed. The Hedge Ratio calculation is based on the allocated Quantity.

Hedging at a period level

As a Fixed Floating Commodity Swap may include multiple hedged quantities maturing at different dates (referred to as periods), Quantum provides Hedge Accounting support at the period level.

When designating a multiple-period Commodity Swap deal into a Hedge Relationship, Quantum creates a separate link for each period. 

It is possible to allocate a single period in more than one Hedge Relationship by using the Allocation % to control which Hedge Relationship covers which portion of the period within the Commodity Swap.

Realized Gain Loss

You can apply Hedge Accounting to a Commodity Swap period from the Deal Date of the Commodity Swap through to the Price Fix Date of the Commodity Swap period.

Set Maturity Gain Loss on the Hedge Relationship's Details tab to Realized, and the system takes the Realized Gain Loss recorded through the Price Fixing event to OCI.

De-designating a Commodity Swap Hedge Relationship

Control the de-designation of a Commodity Swap period from a Hedge Relationship by specifying a Quantity or Percentage.

The system calculates the amount reclassified on dedesignation (discontinuation) by determining the percentage reduction in total quantity hedged.

For example:

  • if a Quantity of 100 tonnes of Commodity Y was designated into a Hedge Relationship, and
  • 20 tonnes was discontinued as it was no longer expected to occur, then
  • the OCI Percentage to Release would be calculated as 20%.

Hypothetical Commodity Swaps

To create a hypothetical Commodity Swap, set the Deal Type to Hypothetical when creating a Commodity Swap deal.

Hypothetical Commodity Swaps differ from actual Commodity Swaps in that they do not have Cash Flows or General Ledger entries.

Add By Query and Add By Deal: expanded to include Period Number

When you select a Designated Risk of COMMODITY PRICE RISK in a Hedge Relationship deal, you can link CMS and CE periods into the Hedge Relationship using Add by Query and Add By Deal.

In the Linked Deals tab menu, select Add By > Query. The Add By Query dialog box opens. Filter by Entity, From Date, and To Date. In the Deals area, find the column Period Number next to the column Deal Number. Select the periods/deals you want to link to the Hedge relationship and click OK. You can save the Hedge Relationship with previously selected periods.

Similarly, in the Linked Deals tab menu, select Add By > Deal. The Add by Deal dialog box opens. Insert the deal numbers of the CMS or CE you want to link into the Hedge Relationship and click OK. All periods defined in the CMS/CE are linked into the Hedge Relationship.

In the Linked Deals tab, you can see the Period column and which period is linked to the Hedge Relationship. The column is populated for CMS and CE deals, but is zero for other deal types.

Remove CMS and CE periods from linked deals

If you don't want to hedge all the periods that are in the Hedge Relationship, or you wrongly selected some periods, you cn remove (delete) periods from linked deals by selecting the period and in the Linked Deals tab menu, selecting Delete. You'll be asked to confirm the deletion.

Period Number in Edit Linked Deal dialog box

You can now change details of linked periods. Select the period and in the Linked Deals tab menu, select Edit. The Edit Linked Deal dialog box shows the Period Number next to the Deal Number. For other types of risk and other types of deals, the field is empty.

Previously, the Period Number field was not displayed in the Edit Linked Deal dialog box.

Allocation validation

When you select a Designated Risk of COMMODITY PRICE RISK in a Hedge Relationship deal and allocate a period of the CMS or CE more than 100% for the same date, the system displays a validation message - for example:

Deal no 5839 Period no 2 is allocated greater than 100%

Save Hedge Relationship with one or multiple CE and CMS periods

When you select a Designated Risk of COMMODITY PRICE RISK in a Hedge Relationship deal, you can now save that Hedge Relationship with one or multiple CMS or CE periods linked into it.

'In HR' badge shown when periods linked to Hedge Relationship

On the Commodity Swap Deal page and Commodity Exposure Deal page you can now see an In HR badge on a deal with periods are linked into a Hedge Relationship, as in the example below.

You can also see a new Hedge Relationships tab with an Allocations area showing the:

  • Deal Number, with a link to the Hedge Relationship and the Period Number linked to it,
  • Start Date and End Date for every period linked into the Hedge Relationship, and
  • Allocation % for every period linked to the Hedge Relationship.

Imported Valuations page includes Period Number

In the Utility > Imported Valuations page, you can now import, replace, and retrieve records related to specific CMS and CE periods. Find the Period Number field in the Filter area.

You can also see the Period Number in the New Revaluation Detail dialog box. Open the New Revaluation Detail dialog box through the Revaluation Details area. For CMS and CE, Period Number is a mandatory field: lack of period number will cause a validation error. For deal types other than CMS and CE, it is not mandatory and will default to 0 (zero) if left blank.

In the Bulk Replace dialog box and Bulk Insert dialog box find Period Number as the last column heading. For CMS and CE, Period Number is mandatory: lack of period number will cause a validation error. For deal types other than CMS and CE, it is not mandatory and will default to 0 (zero) if left blank.

Previously, you could not import period revaluations.

Set effectiveness Inception Valuation to Zero

For CMS and CE Hedge Relationships, when you select an Effectiveness Test with Designated Risk set to COMMODITY PRICE RISK:

  • if the Period Link Start Date is the deal date, the system sets Imported Market Value to zero, or
  • if the Period Link Start Date is different from the deal date, the system sets Imported Market Value to the value at the end of the prior day.

Position: Post OCI based on Rate Fixed Settlement Amount

When you select a Designated Risk of COMMODITY PRICE RISK in a Hedge Relationship deal and Maturity Gain/Loss is Realized, the system doesn't require the Market Value Accounting records on the price fix date. Instead, the system takes the Settlement Amount as the market value - that is, the amount posted to OCI at the maturity of a Hedge Relationship is calculated against the settlement amount of periods that are linked into that Hedge Relationship.

Validation: Position should not process CMS period linked into Hedge Relationship if not price fixed

If you want to revalue a Hedge Relationship with a Designated Risk of COMMODITY PRICE RISK on a period's fix date, or on a date later than the fix date, the Price Fix event must have been actioned.

In the Reporting > Position page, the Logged Messages dialog box will display a message for the Price Fix events that need to be actioned.

If you want to run a position for a Hedge Relationship on a period's fix date, or on a date later than the fix date, this cannot be done until the period linked to it is NOT price fixed.

Dedesignate Hedge Relationship dialog box: single periods

When Designated Risk is COMMODITY PRICE RISK in a Hedge Relationship deal and you select a Dedesignate function in the page menu, the Dedesignate Hedge Relationship dialog box is displayed. Select the Dedesignation Date and chose an option from Defer/Immediate. The system displays the CMS and CE periods linked to the Hedge Relationship and with an End Date up to that Dedesignation Date.no period. You can edit the dedesignation details (Amount, New Allocation, New %).

Save the dedesignation and a message informs you about which periods are dedesignated and in which Hedge Relationship.

Deal Revaluation: Drill Down

You can now use the Deal Revaluation page to see details about separate periods that are linked into a Hedge Relationship, in both the Retrospective tab and Prospective tab.

For example, in a Deal Revaluation page, run a query for the specific Hedge relationship, open the Retrospective tab, and in the Deal column look for the deal and period numbers, separated with a period - for example, 2.2 (as highlighted below).

Hedge Accounting Reporting page: Periods shown

The Reporting > Hedge Accounting-Query page now shows details of CMS and CE periods that are linked to the Hedge Relationship.

In the View area, use the drop-down to select a Link Deal Table and see details about market value, accounting gain/loss, and so on for separate periods. Find the Period No column heading next to the Link Deal No column.

CMS Deal: G/Ls reversed on updating a period linked to a HR

When you make certain financial changes to periods in a CMS or CE deal that is linked to a Hedge Relationship, on the Hedge Relationships page > G/L Entries tab you'll see the system reverse the GL entries associated with these periods. Financial changes include: change of counterparty, amount, currency, quantity, or dates.

If you make a non-financial change to a CMS or/and CE deal, the system does not reverse the GL entries.
