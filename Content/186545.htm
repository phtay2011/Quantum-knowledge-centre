Calculating and Posting Bank Account Interest

Bank Account Interest is calculated and posted using a Bank Interest Statements Processing query run, in order to mirror the interest cashflows calculated by the bank.

The Bank Interest Statement run generates interest cash flows by creating Account Interest (AI) deals. AI deals have deal numbers in the standard Quantum deal sequence, and can be viewed and edited using the Account Adjustments Cash Management page.

TIP: For details of the G/L codes used for interest-related postings, see Interest-Related G/L Postings.

Delivery Instructions for Account Interest Deal Cash Flows

Bank Account Interest (AI) is available as a Transaction Type in Counterparty Bank Account Defaulting Rule records.

The Counterparty and the transaction Entity (G/L Owner) specified in Bank Accounts (on the Entities tab) for the interest-bearing account (the account triggering the interest being posted) determine the Counterparty and Entity on an AI cash flow, and subsequently, the Counterparty Account Defaulting Rule record whose instructions will be applied (for the applicable Effective Date), as follows:

  • if a matching rule record for Transaction Type AI is present, the cash flow instructions will default to that record's instructions; or
  • if not, but a matching default record for Transaction Type ALL is present, the cash flow instructions will default to that record's instructions; or
  • if neither, the Counterparty Delivery Instruction will not be defaulted.

For Third Party Interest, account balances are calculated based on a Bank Account Group and its interest settings. For group-level account deals, the Counterparty and transaction Entity specified for the bank account being posted to determine the Counterparty Account Defaulting Rule record whose instructions will be applied. If either of these is blank, no Counterparty Account Defaulting will apply and the Counterparty Delivery Instruction will not be defaulted.

The Bank Account to which the interest is being posted determines the default External Method for payment.
