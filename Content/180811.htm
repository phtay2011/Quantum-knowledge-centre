Adding an Adjusting Projected Cash Flow

This topic covers making a Projected Cash Flow adjustment after a Prior Day or Intraday Matching run.

TIPS:

  • Any differences between the two types of Matching are indicated within the procedure and under Prior Day v Intraday Matching below.
  • This action uses the standard Quantum Projected Cash Flow page.
  • Unless explicitly stated, references below to menus and grids relate to those in the page you initiated the adjustment from.

To add an adjusting Projected Cash Flow

This function is not available if Resource Security applies and you are not Exempt or do not have Write access to the Bank Account.

  1. On the Matching page's Overview tab, select an account in the Accounts summary.

    The Flows and Matches tabs are then displayed.

  2. (Optional) On the Flows tab, select the relevant item or items in the Cash Flows grid.
  3. Select New Projected from the Cash Flows grid menu.

    The Quantum Projected Cash Flow page is then displayed.

  4. On the Deal Details tab, enter the required values.

    TIP: When Resource Security applies, Resources on the deal form will be filtered based on your allowed access to them.

  5. (Intraday Matching only) If you are adding this Projected Cash flow from Intraday Matching and selected one or more items in Step 2, select either of the following options (on the Deal Details tab):

    Adjust and Match - to Intraday-match the selected items (including the adjusting entry), or

    Create Adjustment - to create the adjusting entry only, without specifying an Intraday match (the only option available when no grid items are selected).

  6. Click OK.

Your Projected Cash Flow deal is then added, with the specified details.

Prior Day v Intraday Matching

For Prior Day purposes, Projected Cash flows are excluded, and:

  • are not displayed in the Cash flows grid,
  • cannot participate in any matching, and
  • cannot be marked as Reconciled.

For Intraday Matching purposes, Projected Cash Flows are included, and:

  • are displayed in the Cash flows grid (unless filtered out), and
  • can participate in Intraday matching.
