About Deal (Trade) Date Accounting

Deal or Trade Date accounting is used for Quantum Money Market and Securities deals when Balance Sheet Timing is set to DEAL DATE in the applicable Accounting Rule record. Deal Date accounting operates as follows.

Recording Settlement Amounts

Settlement amounts are posted to Balance Sheet accounts on Deal Date with the contra account being posted to either the Unsettled Payables (Buy) or Unsettled Receivables (Sell) account. The Unsettled accounts are cleared on the deal's Settlement Date via the Bank Account.

Contingent Accounting

Not applicable to Deal Date Accounting.

Accruals

If either Accrue capital or Accrue interest is selected, accruals will post to Profit and Loss (P&L) accounts from Settlement Date.

If Accrue capital is selected and Revaluations are enabled, the Capital Premium/Discount portion of the total revaluation (calculated by Position Processing*) is deferred from Deal Date to the Capital Premium/Discount Deferred account, and amortized from Settlement Date to the P&L (from that account) by Accruals Processing*. For more details, see Deal v Settlement Date Accounting - Treatment of Capital.

*TIP: Position and Accruals Processing are accessed via the Accounting menu. To view run results without updating the database, access these queries via the Reporting menu.

Revaluations

Deals can be revalued from Deal Date, and the revaluation on Deal Date will be as at the Settlement Date. Going forward, the Revaluation "as at" date will be determined by adding the number of days between the deal's original Deal Date and Settlement Date, to the Position Inquiry's Position Date.

Where the Position Date falls on a non-business day, the calculated as at date is adjusted to the following business day. For example, if the Position Date is Thursday 10th and there are three days between Deal and Settlement Dates, the calculated as at date is Tuesday 15th EOD.

Both Book Value and Market Value will be as at the Position Date + X days (where X is the number of days between Deal Date and Settlement Date, adjusted for non-business days if required).

This also means that the deal is considered fully revalued at the date that represents the actual Maturity Date - X.

Between Deal and Settlement Dates

The following conventions apply between the Deal and Settlement dates:

  • ad hoc interest and ad hoc amounts can be added and accrued,
  • deals can be closed out, and
  • revaluations can be posted.

Deal Date Accounting for Hedged Deals

When Deal Date accounting is applied with the appropriate Revaluation Settings* for the Accounting Rule:

  1. Position Processing moves amortized Premiums from the Capital Premium Accrual account to the Capital Premium Deferred account, from the Deal Date.
  2. Accruals Processing then moves these postings from the Capital Premium Deferred account to the P&L, from the Settlement Date.

*TIP: To apply the appropriate Revaluation Settings:

  • for Money Market Deals - select the Revalue money market deals option and a Money Market Reval Method from the drop-down list, and/or
  • for Security Deals - select the Revalue security deals option and a Security Reval Method from the drop-down list.

For Hedge Accounting, however, an Exposure deal should not be revalued except as part of a Hedge Relationship. Therefore, for Hedge Accounting, the Revalue money market, using option should not be selected - in this case the postings required for Deal Date accounting will not take place, even when Balance sheet timing is set to Deal Date in the linked deal(s).
