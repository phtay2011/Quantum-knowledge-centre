Transaction vs time-period hedges (treatment under IFRS 9)

When hedging under the IFRS 9 accounting standard, the fair value of the change in the time value (or forward value, for Forwards) is first recognized in OCI. The subsequent treatment depends on the nature of the transaction.

Transaction related hedged items

Treatment

Transaction related hedges are usually created for hedging the price of an existing inventory or the future purchase of a commodity. For such transactions, the time value (or forward value, for Forwards) represents part of the cost of the transaction (such as the premium paid).

Under IFRS 9, the amount held in OCI is removed over the life of the hedge.

Example

A manufacturing company plans to purchase a certain commodity for its manufacturing process in the third quarter of the year. The purchase price will be in New Zealand Dollars (NZD) 5 million. The entity buys a call option to purchase NZD 5 million and designates only the intrinsic value of the call option in a cash flow hedge. The critical terms of the option match the terms of the forecast transaction.

At inception, the time value of the option is NZD 50,000. At the end of the first quarter, the time value amounts to NZD 25,000 and NZD 5000 at the end of the third quarter.

Time-period related hedged items

Treatment

Time-period related hedges are usually created when the risk being hedged is for a period of time and the cost must be attributed over that period (for example, the hedge of a commodity inventory over a six-month period, or hedge of the exchange rate for a foreign currency debt). For such transactions, the time value (or forward value, for Forwards) acts as the cost of protection against the risk over the period.

Under IFRS 9, the change in fair value is accumulated in a separate component of Equity and amortized on a rational basis over the term of the hedge relationship.
