Analytics Zero Curve Generation

Generating zero coupon curves typically involves blending together two or more specific-type component curves. Component curve types (rate types) currently supported by Analytics are:

  • Yield (both Cash rates and Swap rates)
  • Futures/FRA rates
  • FX rates
  • Security rates

Discount Factor and zero coupon rate calculations for each of the component types are described in the following topics.

Cash Rates

A Cash Rate is a Yield Rate with the frequencies of all points set to Maturity.

Yield Rates are defined with a Days Offset, typical values being 0, 1, and 2.

FX Rates

Calculation of the Discount Factors for the FX terms is based on the concept of Interest Rate Parity.

For a USD/AUD FX rate for example, this means that if you buy Spot USD and invest the money at USD dollar rates for one month (say), this is the equivalent in terms of money earned to enacting an USD/AUD FX deal in one month's time, at the rate one month from now.

  1. Get the Zero Curve points for the Base Rate ID and Base Currency.
  2. For each term date from the FX curve, calculate the Discount Factor by linearly interpolating the Zero Curve points returned in Step 1.

Security Rates

On every Bond coupon date (starting from the Position Date) a Discount Factor is generated, either by:

  • (initially) interpolating from Discount Factors generated from preceding curves, or
  • (when the previous curves no longer cover the coupon date) - solving for the missing Discount Factor algebraically, or using the Newton-Raphson method.

The Discount Factors for Coupon Dates 1 through 3 are interpolated from previous points. The Discount Factors for the remaining coupon dates are calculated depending on the Interpolation method specified in the Zero Curve definition, as follows.

  • Logarithmic

    The remaining Discount Factors are logarithmically interpolated between the Discount Factor for the last known coupon date (Coupon Date 3) and an initial estimate of the Discount Factor for the last coupon date. The Newton-Raphson method is then used to converge to an acceptable value within specified limits.

  • Linear

    Zero rates for the remaining coupon dates are linearly interpolated between the Zero Rate for the last known coupon date (Coupon Date 3) and an initial estimate of the Zero Rate for the last coupon date. These Zero Rates are then converted to discount factors using the formula above, then the process continues as for the Logarithmic method.
