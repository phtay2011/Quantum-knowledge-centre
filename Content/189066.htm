Unwinding Money Market or Security Deals

An Unwind action lets you reduce the Face Value or Principal amount of a Money Market or Security deal at any time in its life, and account for any resulting gain or loss.

An Unwind is similar to a Closeout, which is available with tradeable deal formulas. You can set up an instrument to be unwound or closed out, but not both. You can also set up an instrument without Unwind or Closeout selected.

NOTE: You cannot unwind Deals in some circumstances.

In this topic:

  • Restrictions
  • About the Unwind dialog box
  • Unwinding a deal
  • Revaluing and accruing unwound deals

Restrictions

The following restrictions apply to the Unwind action on a Money Market or Security deal.

NOTE: These restrictions apply whether deals are accounted for on a Settlement Date or a Deal Date basis.

You cannot Unwind a deal:

  • if it is awaiting Deal Approval;
  • if it is already fully Unwound;
  • on or after its Maturity Date;
  • before its Settlement Date (the date of the initial Principal cash flow);
  • before an existing Unwind on that deal;
  • more than once on the same date;
  • if it is a TIPS deal using an Indexed Bond instrument;
  • on a non-interest calculation date if it uses the DISCOUNT - PERIODIC formula (because interest for deals using this formula is paid in advance);
  • on a non-interest payment date if it uses the PRINCIPAL & INTEREST, ANNUITY, or INDEXED ANNUITY formula (because the amortization formula only works for complete periods);
  • if it uses a Brazilian Yield Compounding Instrument;
  • if it uses an Instrument with associated stock flows; nor
  • partially, on a Rateset or coupon payment date (and not at the end of a compounding period), if it uses the YIELD COMPOUND formula.

For restrictions that apply to Interest Rate Swap deals, see About Interest Rate Swaps.

Unwinding a Deal

NOTE: Some restrictions apply for Money Market or Security deals, and Interest Rate Swap (IRS) deals.

Unwind a deal

To unwind the Money Market, Security, or IRS deals

  1. Open the deal and in the page menu select Unwind. The Unwind dialog box opens.
  2. Change the default Unwind Date, Settlement Date, and Unwind Face Value if necessary.
  3. For deals that use OVERNIGHT INDEX PERIODIC formula timing or have Overnight Index enabled, Compound Rate or Average Rate appears. Check the rates are present and realistic. If not present or realistic, enter a value.
  4. Enter a Settlement Amount value.

    This is the amount of the settlement cash flow to be posted to the bank account on the Unwind Date. You can choose whether the cash flow is for the Face Value of the Unwind only or the Unwound Face Value plus accrued interest and any associated fees, penalties, or charges.

    The system calculates and displays the Book Value of the portion of Principal or Face Value being unwound and the Realised Gain/(Loss) that will be posted for the Unwind gain or loss (the difference between the Book Value and the Settlement Amount). The Book Value amount is broken down into Accrued Interest, Accrued Capital, and/or Accrued WTax and/or Accrued Capital components, depending on the type of deal being unwound.

  5. (Optional) To preview the revaluation effects of the Unwind, click Valuation.

    The Deal Revaluation page opens in a separate window, so you can run a query to see the results of the Unwind before accepting it.

  6. When you are satisfied with the Unwind details, click OK (on the Unwind dialog box) to action the Unwind and save the deal.
    • The system adjusts the Face Value or Principal of the deal to reflect the change, along with future interest or coupon cash flows.
    • The system generates General Ledger entries for the Unwind principal, interest, and capital, and the gain/loss.
    • When you recall the deal, a Fully Unwound or Partially Unwound badge at the top of the deal page indicates the fact that the deal was unwound, and shows the remaining Face Value (if any).

Unwind an IRS or CCS (OIP) deal

When unwinding an Interest Rate Swap deal, or a Cross Currency Swap deal, with the Formula Timing = OVERNIGHT INDEX PERIODIC (OIP), be aware that:

  • The functionality of full and partial unwinds are similar.
  • On the date of the partial unwind, the 2nd INT CALC and INTEREST is blank until you perform a rateset for the interest period, since the system does not yet know the rate for the interest period, unlike unwind on IBOR swaps.
  • The systems warns you if there are missing rates.

The field Compound Rate - Pay or Compound Rate - Receive appears for OIP deals. Find the field by opening Dealing > Interest Rate Swap and in the page menu selecting Unwind.

For more information, read About the Unwind Dialog Box.

Partially unwind a deal

When partially unwinding a deal, such as a Money Market (MM) deal, that has overnight index functionality:

  • The deal map generates a new Unwind Rate component, which sits above the Unwind Notional component.

    The effective date and action date of the new rate is the unwind date.

    The rate is the compounded rate / average rate that Quantum called from Analytics, displayed in the Unwind dialogue box.

  • As you edit values, Analytics updates the other fields in the dialog box.

  • Enter an unwind date within the first, intermediate or last interest period or on the date an interest period ends or starts.

  • Multiple partial unwinds can occur within the same interest period or across different interest periods.

  • We don't support same day unwinds.

This partial unwind lets you reduce the face value of the deal.

Revaluing and Accruing Unwound Deals

When you unwind a deal, the system deletes any accrual, revaluation, and currency translation General Ledger entries posted for that deal.

NOTE: Accruals may not be deleted if Smart G/L Reversal and Deletion currently applies - that is, the Disable smart G/L reversal and deletion option is cleared (on the Operational Settings page).

The next time you run Accruals and Revaluations, separate entries are posted as follows:

  • The interest and any tax on the Unwind Face Value (the amount of the Unwind) are accrued for the period up to the Unwind date, and these accrual entries are posted on the Unwind date.
  • The interest and any tax on any remaining Face Value are accrued for the full period, and posted on the accrual period end date.
