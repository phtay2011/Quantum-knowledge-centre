Hedge Accounting, imported market values

Regular and frequent market revaluations are required for Hedge Accounting. Imported market values provide swift access to up-to-date market values. Where deals are regularly marked to market - for example to meet FASB requirements, the Quantum API provides valuable speed and automation.

Firm commitments and Forecasted transactions that are entered as Actual or Projected Cash Flows can be revalued as part of a Hedge Relationship, or use an imported market value.

  • A Firm commitment is an agreement with an unrelated party that is binding on both parties and usually legally enforceable. It has the following characteristics: The agreement specifies all significant terms, including the:
    • quantity to be exchanged,
    • timing of the transaction, and
    • Fixed price, expressed as a specified:
      • amount - in the Entity's functional currency or in a foreign currency,
      • interest rate, or...
      • effective yield.
  • A Forecasted transaction is one that is expected to occur, but for which there is no firm commitment.This means that when the event does occur, it will be at the prevailing market rates.

The agreement includes a disincentive for non-performance, significant enough to make performance probable.

Multiple market values can be applied to a single deal during its life. If necessary, you can enter a market value before the inception date of the Hedge Relationship.

Linked deals

  • You can import a market value only for a deal linked to a Hedge Relationship, not for the Hedge Relationship as a whole.
  • If an invalid market value record is entered for any deal that is linked to a Hedge Relationship, no revaluation of that Hedge Relationship can take place.
  • If a single deal is linked to multiple Hedge Relationships, it can use a different imported market value within each Hedge Relationship.

Nature of risk

An imported market value relates to Overall Market Risk, not to individual risks such as Interest Rate Risk. You cannot import component revaluation amounts.

Regression

If you select a Prospective effectiveness method of Regression, a series of rate dates is generated:

  • If the market value of a deal is calculated by Quantum, those rate dates are used to locate the rates that determine the market value of the deal at each date.
  • If market values are imported, however, there are no rates or rate dates to draw on. Instead, each date generated by the regression calculations is regarded as a position date. The system uses these position dates to locate the corresponding imported market values for each date.

Adjustments

During Hedge Accounting processing, all necessary adjustments are made to an imported market value. These include, for example, cash flows that have taken place. See the Position page in the Accounting menu.
