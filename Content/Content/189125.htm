Closeout Accounting

Closeout can occur if you enter a Buy deal and a Sell deal for a tradable instrument.

The instrument that you are using determines both the closeout selection method and the accounting method that is applied on closeout.

Closeout method

Quantum selects deals for closeout in terms of a specified Closeout Method. You define this when you set up the Instrument Type.

For more information about available closeout methods in Quantum for:

  • Futures and Options, see Futures and Options closeouts.
  • Money Market Instruments, see Money Market Closeout Methods, Matched closeouts, Indexed Bond Closeouts, and Details tab, MM Instrument Type.
  • Equity / Mutual Funds Instruments, see Closeout Methods for Equities / Mutual Funds.

Quantum automatically selects the individual deals that are to be closed out, unless you choose the manual method (MATCHED).

Accounting method

The accounting method that Quantum applies on closeout depends on the selection made in the Accrual Method field on the Accounting Rule for the selected Money Market instrument.

Quantum uses this accounting method to calculate accruals on the Coupon and on the Premium / Discount for any deal that uses the instrument.

The same method is used to calculate the accruals on closeout. The method used determines how the resulting Gain or Loss is calculated, which in turn influences the amount posted to the deal that is being closed out.

For Money Market accounting, the available options are:

  • Amortized Cost
  • Straight Line
  • Yield Cum
  • Yield Ex

For a description of these accounting methods see Accrual method (Money Market).

A tradable security that uses any of these can be closed out.

NOTE: The closeout method for an instrument is specified on the Details tab of the Money Market Instrument page.

Calculating Gain or Loss

When two or more deals are closed out, Quantum accounting combines the deals to give the correct accounting result. The accounting method associated with the instrument type determines how Gain or Loss is calculated.

A Gain or Loss entry is calculated and posted to the deal that is left fully closed out. Assuming that the Sell deal is the closer deal, this is:

  • a Gain if the book value of the Sell deal is greater than that of the Buy deal
  • a Loss if the book value of the Sell deal is less than that of the Buy deal

Accruals

If accruals are run up to the end of day prior to closeout, no reversals of these accruals will be done. However, if accruals have been run past the closeout date (including on the closeout date) they will be reversed. In this latter case Accrual Processing will need to be run again to re-post them. The process will re-post as follows:

  • If a deal is fully closed out, the new accrual entries are identical to what they were before, up until the closeout date. There are no accruals after that.
  • If a deal is partially closed out, the new accrual entries are identical to what they were before, up until the closeout date. After that, accruals are based only on the remaining Face Value.

NOTE: Accruals may not be deleted if the Smart G/L Reversal and Deletion option is set.

Rolling (Yield Statement) deals - accumulated interest

The accumulated interest on a rolling deal is reflected in the Gain / Loss after a closeout. If a deal is fully closed out, the full amount of the accumulated interest is included. If a deal is partially closed out, the correct proportion of the accumulated interest is included.

General Ledger entries

The following G/L entries are created when deals are closed out.

  • For a Gain

    Dr

     

    Capital Premium / Discount Accrual (BS)

     

    Cr

    Realized Gain (P & L)

  • For a Loss

    Dr

     

    Realized Loss (P & L)

     

    Cr

    Capital Premium / Discount Accrual (BS)

NOTE: The Capital Premium / Discount Accrual accounts are cleared to zero after accruals are run.
