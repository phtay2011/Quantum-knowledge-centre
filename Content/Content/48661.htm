Interest Rate valuation methods

The following interest rate valuation methods are available for deals whose revaluations are based on interest rates - for example, Money Market, Securities, Interest Rate Swap, and Cross Currency Swap deals.

Bills

This method ignores any unknown floating cash flows and so assumes that the floating side is like a Discounted Bill.

  • For fixed rates, the individual cash flows will be discounted from the cash flow date to the revaluation date.
  • For floating rates, this method will discount (using the Quantum Zero Curve) only the known cash flow from the previous rate fixing and apply the principal discount from the same known cash flow date.

    Where a transaction incorporates margins, these are treated as known cash flows and are therefore also discounted from the cash flow date to the revaluation date.

In-arrears Interest Rate Swaps do not support the Bills revaluations methodology.

No

Select NO if you do not require revaluation.

Projected

This method essentially projects the value of floating cash flows based upon Discount Factors obtained from the Quantum Zero Curve.

  • For fixed rates, the individual cash flows will be discounted from the cash flow date to the revaluation date.
  • For floating rates, this method will project all future floating cash flows based upon the Quantum Zero Curve and discount the principal from the actual principal maturity date.

    For In-arrears Interest Rate Swap deal legs (floating rates, Yield Periodic formula only), the valuation requires that a convexity adjustment is made to the forward rate used for each period, which requires volatility for each period's forward rate. Bills Net

This method is as for the Bills method described above, except that any gains are posted net of Withholding Tax.

Projected Net

This method is as for the Projected method described above, except that any gains are posted net of Withholding Tax.

Imported

Use this method to revalue a deal using an imported revaluation method other than those already provided in Quantum.

Imported Separate

This method is as for the Imported method described above, except that it indicates when two separate values are imported for the deal - for example the Pay and Receive leg values of an Interest Rate Swap.

Methods also valid for Money Market Securities

NOTE: These valuation methods are not usually appropriate for customized deals, so the system will normally switch to the Bills method to revalue customized deals.

Market

The amount is calculated by marking the securities transaction to market value.

You can override this by selecting Retain Revals on Customization in the Other Details tab in System Defaults.

Market Cum

The gain or loss is posted on revaluation. The gain or loss is calculated by marking the Securities deals to market value as if they are cum dividend, irrespective of whether each security is cum or ex dividend.

Securities purchased Ex dividend are valued as Ex dividend while still in their first Ex dividend period.

FRN-Coupon

(With FRN instruments only)

An adaptation of the Market method, differing in the way the Swap rate is applied, as follows:

  • The Short Rate (used to discount the next coupon) is taken from the rate record identified by the associated Interest Rate and Rate Type settings( immediately below this one) at the date of the coupon. This is the same as the Short Rate used for the Market method.
  • The Swap Rate (used to discount the remaining future cashflows) is taken from the rate record identified by the Par Rate Name for Zero Curve field, at the Maturity Date of the deal. Unlike the Market method, this rate is not converted to a Discount Factor.
