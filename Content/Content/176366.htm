Cumulative Dollar Offset

This is one of the methods available for both Retrospective and Prospective effectiveness calculations.

Cumulative Dollar Offset is always used in the calculation of OCI amounts, regardless of which effectiveness method is selected.

Cumulative Dollar Offset calculations achieve a reduced averaging effect because they are based on a total time range. Without this smoothing, the smaller ranges associated with each periodic calculation can produce more volatile results, as shown in th following table.

As a Prospective effectiveness method

The system revalues the hedge relationship over the single range of time specified in the two Range fields on the Hedge Relationship page's Effectiveness tab.

If the revaluation date is the Inception date of the hedge relationship and the specified Range is one year for example, the system looks back in time from inception by one year. For each hedge and each exposure deal, it performs a revaluation calculation as at that date, as if it were the start of the hedge relationship.

It then performs another revaluation calculation, as at the revaluation date (in this case, Inception date). The revaluation amounts are compared to determine the total change in fair value for the period of all the hedge deals and all exposure deals.

The change in fair value of the hedge deals is expressed as a percentage of the change in fair value of the exposure deals.

This is the Effectiveness Ratio, which you can use to determine whether the hedge relationship is expected to be effective over the same period of time, but in the future.

Example:

If an Exposure of $100 is revalued at $80 (a loss of $20 for the period of the Range) and a Hedge of $50 is revalued at $75 (a gain of $25 for the same period), the ratio would be 1.25 or 125% (25/20), and the hedge relationship could be expected to be highly effective.

As a Retrospective effectiveness method

The system tests the effectiveness of the hedge relationship from inception - whether designation or transition - to the:

  • Position Date - for Position Reporting or Processing, or...
  • As At Date - for a Revaluation Analysis.

The calculation period is from the last revaluation date to the current revaluation date. For example; a hedge relationship with an effectiveness calculation period of Quarterly has the following calculation periods:

  • Inception date - 3 months,
  • 3 months - 6 months,
  • 6 months - 9 months, and so on.

The overall return of a hedge relationship is determined using the following formula:

(Amount as at current calculation date) - (amount as at last calculation date) + (accumulation of similar previous calculations)

If a hedge relationship is ineffective for a period, then the change in gain and loss for the derivative is not posted to OCI.

Limits in Effectiveness calculations

Associated Upper and Lower Range limits must be specified when the Cumulative Dollar Offset method is specified.

The limits define the boundaries within which the results must fall if the hedge relationship is to be considered effective (and therefore eligible for Hedge Accounting functionality). The ratio of changes in the fair value of the Exposure to changes in the fair value of the Hedge, when expressed as a percentage, must lie within the specified range.
