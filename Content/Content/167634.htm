Deal Analyze

Use the Deal Analyze page to perform a detailed valuation and view analytics for a single, open deal - particularly when you want to see the effects of varying Assumption parameters.

TIP: While you can also perform deal valuations from the Pricing pages, Deal Analyze offers more options and in most cases provides more analytics - for example, Modified Duration and Convexity values.

To value an open deal

  1. Select the deal by EITHER:
    • entering the Deal Number or Deal Identifier directly, OR
    • clicking the browse button and selecting from the browse window.

      TIP: Click the Configure link in the browse window to filter the browse list and/or change its properties. For example, you can filter on Deal Type to view Actual, What If, Forecast, Estimated, Hypothetical, and/or FX Requests.

    The Deal Number, Deal Identifier, Instrument, and Counterparty fields are populated from your selected deal. Other values default as indicated in the next steps.

  2. Optionally change the date defaults if required (from the current date in each case), where:
    • Position Date must be a date when the deal is or was open,
    • Rates Date must be a date for which the applicable rates exist, and
    • As At Date must be a date between creation of the deal and the current date. The deal data as at that date will be used.
  3. Optionally change the Assumption settings from their defaults if required, as below.
    • Rule - the Valuation Rule that determines the applicable rates and valuation parameters to be used and the defaults for Currency Conversion and Method (where applicable); default to the Default rule.
    • Revaluation Timing - the Revaluation Timing to be used in valuing the deal. Start of Day, Intra Day (the default), or End of Day.
    • Currency Conversion - the display currency for Deal Risk Measures; defaults to the Base Currency from the Valuation Rule.
    • Method (applicable deal types only) - the valuation method to be used. The available options are as for the Valuation field on the Pricing page for the deal (Details tab, Price sub-tab).
    • Round Bond Pricing - determines whether or not Bond Pricing should be rounded; cleared by default.
    • Shift Method - the interest rate shift method: Par (the default), Zeros, IR Return, or Derivative.
    • Shift Direction - the rate shift direction for calculating the Basis Point Delta value: Up (the default), Down, or Average.
    • Shift Frequency (applies when Shift Method is IR Return or Derivative) - the rates frequency on which application of the shift is based: Quarterly, Semi Annual, Annual (the default), or Continuous.
    • Delta Shift Points - the number of points the rate should be shifted to calculate Delta; defaults to 1.
    • Gamma Shift Points - the number of points the rate should be shifted to calculate Gamma; defaults to 100.
    • Vega Shift Points - the number of points the rate should be shifted to calculate Vega; defaults to 100.
  4. Click Calculate.
