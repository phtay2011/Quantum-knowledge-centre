Overview of hedge accounting

Under FAS, all derivative instruments must be recognized and subsequently measured on the entity's balance sheet at Fair Value, regardless of whether the instrument is designated as a Hedge or is used for some other purpose. This effectively eliminates the previous practice of Off Balance Sheet Accounting, and standardizes the accounting for all derivatives.

The Hedge Accounting processes in Quantum Six are applicable to the requirements of International Standards such as IAS39 and IFRS9, which are modeled on the same Fair Value framework.

Derivatives as hedging instruments

The accounting treatment of a derivative is based on why the instrument is held. This means that the same instrument will be revalued and accounted for differently depending on whether it is part of a hedge relationship or not.

To qualify for Hedge Accounting, an instrument must:

  • qualify as an effective hedging instrument or Hedge, and
  • be designated (named) as such.

Exposures

For an explanation of how Hedge Accounting affects non-financial exposures, see Actual Cash Flows in Hedge Accounting or Projected Cash Flows in Hedge Accounting, as relevant.

Impact on profit and loss

If a derivative instrument does not meet these criteria and is not part of a valid hedge relationship, it will be marked to market and any gain or loss will immediately impact Profit and Loss (earnings).

If the instrument is part of a hedge relationship, it is eligible for Hedge Accounting and you can control when the gain or loss appears in the Profit and Loss. Changes in the Fair Value Hedge of the Hedge can be:

  • Recognized in income at the same time as offsetting changes in the fair value of an Exposure to which it is linked. See Fair Value Hedge.
  • Deferred in Other Combined Income (OCI)* in the Equity area of the balance sheet. From OCI, it can enter the Profit and Loss in a way timed to coincide with the impact of the Exposure. See Cash Flow hedge.

    * Other Comprehensive Income (OCI) is an accounting presentation term for a Balance Sheet account. The account is in the Shareholder Equity section of the Balance Sheet. If a Cash Flow hedge is effective, FAS 133 allows you to defer the effective portion of the Mark-to-Market posting from Profit and Loss to OCI. When the amounts are transferred back to the Profit and Loss, the net effect on the Balance Sheet accounts is nil.

    There is no concept of OCI for a Fair Value hedge.

NOTE: The system initially calculates all revaluation results in Profit and Loss. Any amounts deferred to OCI are moved from there to the balance sheet. When it is time for them to enter the Profit and Loss, they are brought back in.

Summary of accounting requirements

  • All derivatives are recognized on the Balance Sheet.
  • They are measured at fair value.
  • Changes in fair value are accounted for according to the type of hedge.

Currency translation

All General Ledger entries associated with hedge relationships are in base currency, and so are not affected by currency translation (Currency Translation Processing, Currency Translation). See Spot to Spot Adjustment.

The Hedge Reclassification run by the Position Inquiry is independent of an Currency Translation update, so the order in which you run these is not significant.
