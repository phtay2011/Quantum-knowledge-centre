South African Repo Pricing Formula

The South African Bond pricing model uses the standard Bond Periodic formula in Quantum with specific settings (Days Convention, Rounding, and so on).

When a Repo deal based on a Repo instrument with the Apply South African pricing option selected has a vanilla South African bond entered as collateral, the Settlement Price (AIP1) and details calculated and displayed on the Deal Details tab for the underlying collateral item are consistent with the South African Bond pricing model.

The following formula is applied when calculating the settlement price for the second leg of the Repo (AIP2). This formula takes AIP1, adds in the Repo interest between Settlement date (d1) and Maturity date (d2), then subtracts any coupons and coupon interest. Coupon interest is calculated for each coupon that falls within the Repo period between the coupon date (cdi) and d2, with the calculation dependent on the settlement date d1 falling within the Ex period for the coupon or not.

Once the initial AIP2 value is found, Quantum iteratively solves for a closest yield. This yield is then rounded to five decimal places, and the Settlement Price AIP2 is recalculated with the rounded yield.

The Repo Interest cash flow is calculated to be the difference between the leg 1 settlement value AIP1 and the unadjusted settlement on leg 2. This will be slightly different to the interest amount calculated on the Deal Details tab as the price on the second leg is rounded before the interest is recalculated, as per the South African methodology.

NOTES:

  1. The party handing over the bonds as collateral in return for putting cash on deposit at the Repo rate is still entitled to receive the coupons from those bonds. These coupon payments are accounted for in the second leg of the Repo.

    If there are no coupon consequences then cdi will be zero and the second term in the equation does not apply.

  2. If the second leg of the Repo falls in the Bond's Ex period, the coupon is discounted back to d2 and subtracted from the proceeds to be paid to the counterparty as they will receive the next coupon.

    However, if the second leg of the Repo follows a coupon date and the counterparty receives the coupon, the interest that could have been earned on the coupon is also subtracted (using the Repo rate for the interest calculation) as well as the value of the coupon.

    If the Repo spans many coupon dates, each respective coupon and the interest it could earn are subtracted, up to the second leg of the Buy/Sell-back.
