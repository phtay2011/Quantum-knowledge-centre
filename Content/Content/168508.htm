Header (Cash Flow Counterparty Instructions)

In Account mode, you can change the default values in some of these fields.

In Adhoc Instruction or Third Party mode, most fields are available for entering "one-off" details (no defaults apply).

See also: Changing Counterparty Instructions.

Currency and Amount

Read-only; the currency of the counterparty bank account and the settlement amount.

Beneficiary Name

(Editable only in Adhoc Instruction or Third Party mode.)

The individual or entity to whom the payment will be made. To define the Beneficiary on the Adhoc or Third Party Instruction, either select from the drop-down list of counterparties, or enter a free-format name as required.

Account Number / Identifier

The Counterparty bank account number OR an identifier value for the account (for example, BIC). To change, select from the drop-down list of Counterparty Bank Accounts in the currency of the cash flow.

This list may or may not be limited to accounts for the deal counterparty, depending on the setting of the system-wide option Display bank accounts for all counterparties (under Bank Accounts on the Operational Settings page).

TIP: Selection of another Counterparty's bank account allows for settlement to an intermediary bank account.

If the deal is CLS-eligible, and CLS is:

  • required - you cannot change to an account that would make the deal non-eligible, or
  • optional - you may change to an account that would make the deal non-eligible (a warning message will display and you can continue or cancel).

IBAN Account

(Account mode; SWIFT-like payments only)

Optional. This value is used when processing SWIFT EFT output. It may be used where applicable to enter an International Bank Account Number (IBAN), in countries where the IBAN format is used for account numbers. The aim of the IBAN is to facilitate the automatic processing of cross-border credit transfers.

Within Quantum, an IBAN is a series of alphanumeric characters that uniquely identifies a bank account held anywhere in the world. It contains all the information necessary to route a payment order through any national clearing system.

Enter up to 34 alphanumeric characters, where:

  • The first two alphabetic characters identify the country in which the account is held.
  • The next two digits are the check digits. They validate the complete IBAN.
  • The final part of the IBAN is the domestic Account Number, which in most cases consists of the Account Number itself - that is, the number that identifies the bank and the branch, and one or more check digits.

    If no value is entered in this field then the Account Number value will be used instead.

Example: Dutch IBAN

Domestic Account Number: 041 71 64 300

Electronic IBAN: NL91ABNA041764300

Account Name / Payment Method

The default is EITHER:

  • the name of the counterparty bank account, OR
  • (if Account / Identifier is an identifier) the Payment Method.

You can either select from the drop-down list, or enter a new name in free format. This value must match with the Account Number / Identifier value.

Ideally, this should be as recorded with the correspondent bank.
