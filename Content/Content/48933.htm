Yield Conventions

The quoted yield convention defined for a Money Market or Securities Bond-style instrument (or a Swap instrument leg), is used to calculate the yield value on any deal (or deal leg) based on that instrument.

NOTE:

  • Not all yield conventions are available for all instruments.
  • For instruments with Exact coupons, the Coupon Frequency should correspond to the Quoted Yield Convention to give accurate yield results. For example, if a coupon has a monthly coupon frequency, then the quoted yield convention should be Monthly Compounded or Monthly Compounded (NET). If you change either value at deal time you must enter the corresponding other value to maintain accuracy.
  • COMPOUNDED (NET) conventions apply only to BOND instruments and are as for the corresponding COMPOUNDED conventions less any Withholding Tax.
  • Unless otherwise stated, the Day Count convention defined for the instrument applies.

The available yield conventions and how they work are listed below.

  • WEEKLY COMPOUNDED (BOND legs of Swap instruments only)
  • MONTHLY COMPOUNDED
  • MONTHLY COMPOUNDED (NET)
  • QUARTERLY COMPOUNDED
  • QUARTERLY COMPOUNDED (NET)
  • SEMI ANNUAL COMPOUNDED
  • SEMI ANNUAL COMPOUNDED (NET)
  • ANNUAL COMPOUNDED
  • ANNUAL COMPOUNDED (NET)
  • MONEY MARKET - The yield is calculated from the simple interest formula above using the Day Count convention defined for the instrument.
  • TRUE YIELD (not available for BOND Instruments) - The yield compounds annually, and the cash flows are adjusted using the Day Count convention defined for the instrument.
  • US STREET - The yield compounds semi-annually using the ACTUAL/ACTUAL Day Count convention, reverting to ACTUAL/360 in the final period.
  • US TREASURY - The yield compounds semi-annually using the ACTUAL/ACTUAL Day Count convention. Partial periods are discounted using the simple interest formula, reverting to ACTUAL/360 for the final period.
