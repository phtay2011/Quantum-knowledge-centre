Working with Hedge Relationships

You can perform the following additions and removals of linked deals:

  • Add deals (Designation)
  • Remove deals (Non Designation)
  • Remove deals (Dedesignation)

Adding deals (Designation)

You can add (link) a deal to a hedge relationship at any time, provided the Disable Deal Linking option on the Deal Details tab of the Hedge Relationship page is not selected.

When you link and save a deal to a hedge relationship, the deal is referred to as designated.

NOTE: If the deal has any existing General Ledger postings (generated by a position processing run), you can choose whether to reverse or keep these. (If you keep them, you could enter the new Maturity Date G/L balance.)

Removing deals (Non Designation)

You can remove a linked deal from a hedge relationship at any time, provided the deal has not been included in a position processing revaluation.

Removing deals (Dedesignation)

When a linked deal has been included in a position processing revaluation, you must perform a dedesignation in order to remove a percentage of the deal, or the entire deal, from the hedge relationship. Dedesignation creates a calculation point that position processing (or position reporting) uses for the next revaluation calculation.

NOTE: You can remove a linked deal without dedesignation provided the deal is not included in previous position processing revaluations for the hedge relationship.

You can perform the following types of dedesignation:

  • Partial Dedesignation - where the percentage of a deal allocated to a hedge relationship is increased or decreased
  • Full Dedesignation - where an entire deal is removed from a hedge relationship on a specified date

Hedge Relationship Dedesignation - where an entire hedge relationship is terminated, at which point Hedge Accounting for the underlying deals ceases (in effect, this removes all deals from the hedge relationship on a specified date)

NOTE: Dedesignated deals remain listed in the Linked Deals grid until the dedesignation date (End Date) is reached, but they are no longer included in revaluation calculations. You can dedesignate a previously dedesignated deal, provided you use an earlier date.

A linked deal can be dedesignated from a hedge relationship:

  • as a result of an action in another dealing application - see Dedesignation from Linked Deal Actions
  • using the Linked Deals tab - see Removing deals from a Hedge Relationship (Dedesignation)

When a linked deal is dedesignated, instructions for the accounting treatment of other comprehensive income (OCI) or mark to market (MTM) default from the Hedge Dedesignation setting on the Deal Details tab (Hedge Relationship page). For a Cash Flow Hedge Type, the Default Release Account setting also applies.

  • If the linked deal is a Hedge, you cannot change the accounting treatment on the Dedesignation form.
  • If the linked deal is an Exposure or Hypothetical deal, you can change the accounting treatment on the Dedesignation form. See Partial Dedesignation.

Viewing dedesignated deal details

When you dedesignate a deal and save the hedge relationship, the Deal Map reflects the dedesignation. You cannot see details of the dedesignated deal once the change has been saved, so dedesignate with care.

TIP: For information about Deal Map items in general see Viewing Deal Details.
