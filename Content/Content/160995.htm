Entities and Business Structure in Quantum

Your business structure of group-level and transaction-level entities is fundamental, as it establishes the portfolio or entity structure for your treasury database and affects the management reporting structure.

NOTE: When you have to rearrange a business structure, remember that the existing structure may have been used to define various defaults, including those that control Accounting, Settlements, and Limits processing. If you need to reorganize the overall structure (as opposed to simply adding new entities), it is advisable to discuss your planned changes with FIS Support staff.

Each component of the structure is seen as an entity, and each entity may be either a transaction- or group-level item. Any number of entities may be created, and they can be grouped in a hierarchical structure.

  • Transaction-level entities are the lowest level of the business structure and are the only entities against which deals may be recorded.
  • A transaction entity may be a member of one entity group, or of none.
  • A group entity has one or more members (lower-level groups or transaction-level entities), and it may be a member of one higher-level group, or of none. (This reflects real-world business structures.)
  • There is currently a limit of 26 levels in the hierarchical structure.
  • You can identify entities that are linked to a General Ledger, and entities that are General Ledger owners.
  • The current record's level in the hierarchy is indicated by the breadcrumb trail at the top of the page. The default is the highest level - for example, when you first open the page, with no record having yet been selected.
  • A graphical view of the hierarchical structure is available, and you can use this to navigate to any entity or group in that view, as follows:
    1. Click the button (top right of page) to open a graphical view of the current* hierarchy.

      If the current record is:

      • a transaction-level entity - its parent group and its sibling items are displayed, or
      • a group - that group and any lower-level items are displayed.
    2. Click on a group or entity box to open that Entity record.

      *TIP: The current level in the hierarchy is indicated by the breadcrumb trail at the top of the page.
