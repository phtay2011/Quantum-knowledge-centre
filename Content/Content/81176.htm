Deal v Settlement Date Accounting - Treatment of Capital

One of the main differences between Deal (Trade) Date and Settlement Date accounting in Quantum is the treatment of Capital. The important points to note are:

  • The Capital Premium/Discount initial accrual amount is posted on Deal Date rather than on Settlement Date.
  • When Position Processing is run, the daily amount of the Capital amortization is moved from the accrual account to the Capital Deferred account (based on the days basis of the deal)
    • Start = Deal Date
    • Finish = X number of days before Maturity Date (where X = number of days between Deal Date and Settlement Date)
  • When Accruals Processing is run, the daily Capital amortization amounts are moved from the Capital Deferred account to the Profit and Loss account.
    • Start = Settlement Date
    • Finish = Maturity Date
  • The only difference between Accruals Processing runs for Deal Date accounting deals and Settlement Date accounting deals is the posting of the Balance Sheet entry relating to the capital amortization to the Capital Deferral account rather than the Capital Accrual account. All amounts are identical for the two deals.

Amounts Posted

The deferred amount for each day from Deal Date is based on the amortization amount for each day from Settlement Date. Effectively the deferral posting (Position Processing) on Deal Date is the same as the amortization posting (Accruals Processing) on Settlement Date. For each day after Deal Date, the deferral posting matches the amortization posting for the same number of days after Settlement Date.

However, as the amount deferred each day is based on a mapping of the GL date to the Settlement Date, if a non business day occurs within X number of days from Settlement Date (where X is the number of days between Deal and Settlement dates) the total amount deferred at Settlement Date may not equal the total amount amortized X number of days after Settlement Date. This will be back in line after the same non business day has been processed by Position Processing. For an illustration, see the following example.
