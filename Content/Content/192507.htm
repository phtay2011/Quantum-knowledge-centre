How Analytics Determines Final Trading Days

The following process is used to find the final trading day for a Futures contract in Analytics.

  1. The contract delivery month determines the month you want to find a date for.
  2. The Location and base Rules settings - Day, Day No, and Business Days - define a base rule for determining a day in that month.

    With these fields on their own, you can set up a rule such as the second Wednesday in the month, or the actual date, and also set a business day rule to control how to move that date if it falls on a non-business day.

  3. If the 2nd Rule Applied setting is None, the primary rule is unaffected.

    Otherwise, the additional rule settings may change the date derived from the base rule, as follows.

  4. If 2nd Rule Applied is Before or After, the second rule is applied to find a date before or after the primary rule date respectively, and the following fields are displayed.
    • 2nd Day - the secondary rule day type - select a day of the week, Actual, Actual (Preceding Month), Business Day, or End of Month.
    • 2nd Day No - used in conjunction with the 2nd Day value to specify the additional rule timing, when applicable. If 2nd Day is set to:
      • Monday through Sunday - set 2nd Day No to a week number in the month (1 through 4),
      • Actual - set 2nd Day No to a day of the month,
      • Actual (Preceding Month) - set 2nd Day No to a day of the month before the delivery month,
      • Business Day - set 2nd Day No to a number of business days with which to offset the calculated delivery date, or
      • End of Month - set 2nd Day No to zero (type the numeral 0; do not delete the contents of the field).
    • 2nd Business Day - the rule to determine the day to be used instead when the secondary rule results in a non-business day: Actual, Following, Modified Following, or Preceding.
    • Exception - (a common example is 24 December with the Preceding Business Day rule).
  5. If 2nd Rule Applied is Exception, the second rule is applied to specify a single date in the year, and the following fields are displayed.
    • Month - used in conjunction with the adjacent Day setting to specify a single date in the year for the additional rule timing, when applicable.
    • Day - used in conjunction with the adjacent Month setting to specify a single date in the year for the additional rule timing.
    • 2nd Business Day - the rule to determine the day to be used instead when the secondary rule results in a non-business day: Actual, Following, Modified Following, or Preceding.

      TIP: A common example is 24 December with the Preceding Business Day rule.
