Calculating Bond Pricing Values

Perform the following steps to calculate settlement and market values for the deal.

  1. On the Details tab:
    1. In the Calculate field (selectable only for new deals when Price Type is Yield), either select Value or select Yield to solve the Yield for a specified price.
    2. If Calculate is Yield, enter the specific Cash Price to solve for.
  2. On the Price tab, change the Position Date, Rates Date, and/or As At Date settings, as required.
  3. If Create Call/Put is:
    • cleared - select the Valuation method (Market, Repricing, Projected, or Market Cum) or
    • selected - select the Valuationmodel and enter the Steps per Coupon period.
  4. Select Cum or Ex to specify whether or not the coupon value will be included in calculation of the price.
  5. Enter the Previous Coupon and Next Coupon dates.
  6. In the Price tab's Yield OR Capital Price OR Cash Price field (depending on the Price Type), enter the Yield OR Capital Price/ OR Cash Price for the Market value calculations.
  7. Click the Price button.

The Settlement grid (above the Price tab) displays the results for the settlement-related settings (from the top of the Details tab), giving Position and Per Unit values for the Capital Price, Accrued Interest, and Cash Price at settlement.

The Price tab displays the calculated market value results for the settings on that tab, in one or three results grids, as follows:

  • Market - Bond grid - displays Position and Per Unit values for the bond's Capital Price, Accrued Interest, Cash Price, Greeks, Duration, and Convexity, as shown below.
  • Market - Bond Option grid (shown when Create Call/Put is selected) - displays Position and Per Unit values for the option Price and Greeks, as shown below.
  • Market - Total grid (shown when Create Call/Put is selected) - displays Position and Per Unit total values for the bond and option Capital Price, Accrued Interest, and Cash Price.

In each case the Rates tab shows the rate curves used.

Except whenValuation is Market or Market Cum, the Valuation tab displays the calculation results for each valuation cash flow, giving Date, number of Days, Description, Amount, Zero Rate, Discount Factor, Market Value, and Greeks for each cash flow.

The result values are summarized below. For details of the calculation formulas used, see Bond Pricing Formulas and/or Bond Option Pricing Formulas, as relevant.

  • Price - Values for the deal or option price.
  • Delta - A measure of the change in the value of a deal or option relative to a change in the price of the underlying.
  • Gamma - A measure of the change of a deal or option Delta, relative to a change in price of the underlying.
  • Vega - The sensitivity of a deal or option's value to a one percentage point change in the volatility of the underlying.
  • Theta - The rate of change of the value of a deal or option as its time to maturity decreases, with other terms held constant (usually negative).
  • DurationDur = MDur * ( 1 + [ IRRF / F ]), where:
    • MDur = Modified Duration (see below)
    • IRRF = Internal Rate of Return for the relevant frequency
    • F = Frequency
  • Mod DurationMDur = Delta / (MV * 0.0001)
  • ConvexityConvx = 108 * ( Gamma / MV), where MV = Market Value
