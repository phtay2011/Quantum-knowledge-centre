Introduction to Bank Reconciliation

Bank Reconciliation is a vital control mechanism.

Reconciling actual bank movements against Quantum's view of the bank accounts can highlight settlement failures, required bank adjustments or fees, and any errors in your Quantum data or at the bank.

Bank Reconciliation applies to the matching of Quantum cash flows with confirmed prior-day or unconfirmed Intraday bank-reported data.

Using Quantum's Import and Bank Reconciliation functions, you can:

  • import cash flows from external (non-Quantum) sources, and/or import external bank statement balances and/or individual bank flows (bank statement item details),
  • automatically or manually run a rules-based matching process to suggest matches between Intraday or Prior Day cash flows and bank statement information (balances and/or individual bank flows), at varying levels of certainty,
  • make balance adjustments, individual adjustment entries, bank account transfers, and/or Deal changes while reconciling,
  • modify display options, matching rules, query filters, and report views,
  • produce an interim report giving a summary reconciliation (only if the bank reconciliation is run as a scheduled task),
  • review matching results,
  • (Prior Day only) mark accounts as reconciled to a certain date, or roll back to a previous reconciliation mark, and
  • explicitly check for Quantum database changes (made by other users) that will break any existing matches.

NOTE: You can also:

  • Use Module Security to allow or disallow access to the various Bank Reconciliation functions on a user-by-user basis.
  • Use Resource Security to restrict user access to Bank Accounts and other Resources (Currency, Entity, Instruments, and Counterparties).
