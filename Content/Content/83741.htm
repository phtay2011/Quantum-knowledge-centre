Accounting for Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS)

Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS) are a type of Indexed Bond. They are used as a mechanism to guard the principal from inflation. They pay a fixed rate of interest. The value of the principal is adjusted based on changes in the Consumer Price Index (CPI). The interest rate is applied to the inflation-adjusted principal, not to the original face value. Therefore, if inflation occurs throughout the life of the security, every interest payment will be greater than the one before it. The converse is also true in the event of deflation.

Upon maturity, Treasury will pay the greater of either the inflation-adjusted principal or the original face value.

When accounting for Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS) in Quantum, the following applies:

  • Capital (Premium/Discount) amortization and Interest accruals are CPI-adjusted. This applies to Straight Line Accruals only.
  • The Principal is CPI-adjusted. This adjustment will be posted by the accruals engine. This is applicable to Straight Line Accruals only.
  • On Revaluation (in Quantum or imported from Analytics), the Book Value used to calculate the gain/loss reflects the CPI adjustment for the Interest, Premium/Discount, and Principal.
  • Upon closeout, the accounting will reflect the difference between the actual CPI on the closeout date and the CPI accrued/amortized to that date.
  • Both Settlement and Trade Date (Deal Date) Accounting are supported.

See also: Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS)

Smart Reversals should be enabled for accounting purposes. This prevents unnecessary reversals (back to the beginning of the deal) following an Indexation update.
