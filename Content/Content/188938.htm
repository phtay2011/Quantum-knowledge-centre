Interest Rate Option Posting, Balance Sheet tab

  • Premium Payable - This G/L tag is used if the premium is payable on a date other than the deal date. The account is credited at deal save. If the Accrue premium check box in Accounting Rules is:
    • Selected, the contra account is Premium Expense Accrual.
    • Clear, the contra account is Premium Expense.

    The account is debited on the premium settlement date, the contra account being Bank Account.

  • Premium Receivable - See Premium Payable n this topic (reverse the direction of the accounting entries)
  • Settlement Payable - This G/L tag is used when an interest rate option is cash settled, and settlement date is on a date other than the exercise date. The account is credited at exercise date. If the Accrue settlement check box in Accounting Rules is:
    • Selected, the contra account is Settlement Expense Accrual.
    • Clear, the contra account is Premium Expense.

    The account is debited on the settlement date of the cash settlement, the contra account being Bank Account

  • Settlement Receivable - See Settlement Payablein this topic (reverse the direction of the accounting entries).
  • Premium Expense Accrual - This G/L tag is used when the Accrue premium check box in Accounting Rules is selected. The account is credited when Accrual Inquiry is run in posting mode, the contra amount being Premium Expense (P/L).

    This amount is credited on Deal date, the contra account being either Bank Account or Premium Payable depending on the Payment date.

  • Premium Income Accrual - See Premium Expense Accrual in this topic (reverse the direction of the accounting entries).
  • Settlement Expense Accrual - This G/L tag is used when the Accrue settlement check box in Accounting Rules is selected. The account is debited on exercise, the contra account being either Bank Account or Settlement Payable, depending on the settlement date.

    This account is credited when Accrual Inquiry is run in posting mode, the contra being Settlement Expense (P/L).

  • Settlement Income Accrual - See Settlement Expense Accrualin this topic (reverse the direction of the accounting entries).
  • Revaluation Gain: Realized - This account is debited when a P&L gain is calculated, and credited with a decrease in a P&L Gain, the contra account being Gain: Realized (P/L).
  • Revaluation Gain: Unrealized - This G/L tag is used when the applicable Revaluation check box on the Accounting Rules tab is selected.

    This account is debited when a P&L gain is calculated, and credited with a decrease in a P&L gain, the contra account being Gain: Unrealized (P/L).

  • Revaluation Loss: Realized - See Revaluation Gain: Realized .in this topic (reverse the direction of the accounting entries).
  • Revaluation Loss: Unrealized - See Revaluation Gain: Unrealizedin this topic (reverse the direction of the accounting entries).
