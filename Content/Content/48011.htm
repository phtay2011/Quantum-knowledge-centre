Swaption Background

A European Swaption provides the holder with the right, but not the obligation, to enter into a specific swap deal on a specific future date.

Swaptions provide a way to reduce the uncertainty associated with a swap transaction. If a company wishes to enter into swap deal to convert their floating-rate loan into a fixed-rate loan in the future they cannot be certain of the fixed rate at the time of the swap.

One way to eliminate the uncertainty is to purchase an appropriately structured Swaption. A company could enter into a Swaption agreement giving them the right to receive LIBOR and pay a fixed rate of 7.5% starting in six months time.

If the swap rate in six months time turns out to be greater than 7.5%, then the company would exercise their option to enter into a swap where they are only required to pay 7.5%.

Conversely, if the market swap rate turns out to be lower than 7.5%, then the company would lapse the Swaption and enter into a swap at the lower fixed rate. Either way, the fixed rate of the swap is guaranteed to be no higher than 7.5% and the company is assured that the net funding cost of the proposed loan will not exceed 8.5%.

The reduction in uncertainty provided by a Swaption has value. The Blacks option pricing model is one way to estimate an exact Swaption premium. In addition, the value of the Swaption is higher when:

  • The fixed rate of the swap underlying the Swaption is substantially lower than the fixed rate that is expected to prevail in the market at the time the Swaption will expire. This will allow company A to pay a lower fixed rate than the market rate when the swap is exercised resulting in a call option that is well in the money.
  • The uncertainty surrounding the fixed rate that will prevail in the market when the Swaption matures is greater. The greater the uncertainty, the higher the value of an instrument that is structured to eliminate that uncertainty. This reflects the positive relationship between the option value and the volatility of the underlying asset.
