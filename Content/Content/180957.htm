Cash Concentration - Hierarchy and Transfers

Cash concentration is the amalgamation of funds from several accounts into one or more control accounts. Cash concentration is used to sweep surpluses and fund deficits using centralized bank accounts where the funds can be more effectively managed. Cash concentration simplifies cash management, allows economies of scale, and helps to avoid short-term borrowing and interest payments.

A CashXplorer Data Version calculates details of bank-generated cash concentrations and cross-currency cash concentrations. In addition, it also calculates recommended cash concentrations according to the settings on the Bank Account's Worksheet Settings tab and the related query's Additional Options (Properties tab, Configure Query).

Using the Cash Concentration function, you can:

  • view details of the recommended cash concentration movements,
  • make changes to the details,
  • create the deals that will generate the concentration transfers, and
  • automatically perform an incremental data refresh on the Data Version to see the effect of the cash concentration.

In addition, you can view read-only simulated concentrations that are shown when either:

  • the currency of the cash concentration account is different from that of the source account (Cross Currency concentrations), or
  • the currencies are the same AND the Suggested Concentration Transfer setting for the Account is NONE (Bank Generated concentrations).

Concentration Hierarchy

CashXplorer‘s Concentration Hierarchy window shows a graphical representation of your cash concentration hierarchy.

To view the Concentration Hierarchy for the current worksheet

  • In the page menu, select Concentration Hierarchy.

    The Concentration Hierarchy dialog box opens, displaying Bank Accounts in hierarchical sequence.

    TIP: If Resource Security applies, you'll see only the Bank Accounts you have access to. In this case, the hierarchy may look incomplete.

Cash Concentration Transfers

To view and create Cash Concentration Transfers for the current Data Version

NOTE: Suggested Settlement dates will not be modified by deal defaulting logic for deals created in this way.

  1. In the page menu, select Concentrate Cash.

    The Cash Concentration Transfers dialog box opens (see below for details).

  2. To optionally edit any Suggested Transfers grid item on the Today's Transfers or Other Transfers tab†:
    1. Click a value in the grid row and select Edit Concentration Transfer.

      Either the Cash Transfer or Actual Cash Flow page opens in a new window, depending on the Type setting (AT or AC, respectively).

    2. Edit values as required and click OK.
  3. To action selected transfers on either the Today's Transfers or Other Transfers tab†:
    1. Select the grid rows for the transfers you want to action.
    2. Do one of the following:
      • click Transfer & Refresh to create the transfer deals and refresh the main display, or
      • click Create Transfer Only to create the transfer deals without refreshing the main display.

      If the Write information messages to message log Configure Query option is selected (Properties tab, advanced options), a detailed message is added to the Data Version History and Message Log for each transfer created. The message will include details such as the currency, amount, source account, and destination account.

NOTE: You cannot edit nor action items on the Bank Generated or Cross Currency tab. These are listed for informational purposes only.

About the Cash Concentration Transfers tabs

The Today's Transfers tab displays suggested transfers:

  • whose Settlement Date is today*, and
  • where the currency of the cash concentration account is the sameas the source account's, and
  • where the Suggested Concentration Transfer setting for the source account is ACCOUNT TRANSFER or TWO ACTUAL CASHFLOWS. and
  • which you can optionally change and action as required (see above).

The Other Transfers tab displays suggested transfers:

  • whose Settlement Date is not today*), and
  • where the currency of the cash concentration account is the sameas the source account's, and
  • where the Suggested Concentration Transfer setting for the source account is ACCOUNT TRANSFER or TWO ACTUAL CASHFLOWS, and
  • which you can optionally change and action as required (see above).

The Bank Generated tab displays simulated bank-initiated concentrations for informational purposes only:

  • where the currency of the cash concentration account is the sameas the source account's, and
  • where the Suggested Concentration Transfer setting for the source account is NONE, and
  • that you cannot change nor action, and
  • which are often actioned by the bank at the close of day and reported the following day in Bank Reconciliation Prior Day Match query results.

The Cross Currency tab displays simulated cross currency concentrations for informational purposes only:

  • where the currency of the cash concentration account differs from the source account's (regardless of the Suggested Concentration Transfer setting for the source account), and
  • that you cannot change nor action.

* Today's date is determined by EITHER the best-matching Cut-Off Time rule location OR the user's location (if no matching rule is found). If the CashXplorer Horizon date is Today and the Settlement Date of a Suggested Transfer is equal to Today’s date, then the transfer will be shown on the Today’s Transfers tab.

About the grids on each tab

Each tab's grid lists the applicable transfers. If Resource Security applies, only those transfers where you have access to both the To Account and From Account will appear.

A Suggested Transfers grid appears on both the Today's Transfers and Other Transfers tabs, with the following columns.

  • The first column () indicates row selection (click the column heading to select all items; click it again to deselect all items).
  • The From Account and To Account column groups display details of the source and destination accounts respectively,where:
    • Ccy gives the currency of the transfer (the same for source and destination),
    • a Cut-Off Time column for each account appears only on the Today's Transfers tab (see Cut-Off Time Rules below), while
    • a Settlement Date column for each account appears only on the Other Transfers tab.
  • Phase shows the suggested concentration Processing Phase, as specified for the From Account (Worksheet Settings tab). This gives the sequence for processing this account within in a chain of cash concentration flows.
  • Type is AC (Actual Cash Flow) or AT (Account Transfer), depending on the default Suggested Concentration Transfer setting for the Account.
  • Instrument also defaults to the setting for the Account.

The grids on the Bank Generated or Cross Currency tabs also have From Account and To Account column groups and Phase, Type, and Instrument columns.

Cut-off Time Rules

You can create Cut-off Time rules that apply to Cash Transfer and Actual Cash Flow deals created from the Todays Transfers tab of the Cash Concentration dialog box.
