Option Strike Prices, Futures and ETO Instrument

Use this area to control the values that will appear in:

  • The Strike Price lookup in Futures and ETO Deals (Details tab) for a particular contract and exchange when either CALL or PUT (for an Option) is selected in the Contract Type field on that form
  • The Strike Price grid column on the Rates tab in Exchange Traded Option Rates (a variant of Interest Rates)
  • The Strike Price grid column on the Rates tab in Volatility Rates for Exchange Traded Options

Enter at least one range with its allowed Interval. Add one or two more ranges where different (tiered) intervals apply to each range, ensuring that your ranges do not overlap. See Strike Price Range Examples, Futures Options.

For each Strike Price range, enter the following.

  • From and To - the lower and upper limits between which Strike Prices for the Option contract should be generated; used, with the Interval, to generate valid strike prices. The fractional parts of these values must correspond to the Interval values for the range.

    TIP: To maximize reporting performance, it is best to limit the strike price range to a subset of all the strike prices listed by the Exchange.

  • Interval - the interval between valid Strike Prices; validated and formatted according to settings in the Price Entry area on the Futures Contract Details tab:
    • If Entry Type is DECIMAL, Interval values must be whole multiples of the Minimum Price Move value.
    • If Entry Type is FRACTIONAL, Interval value fractions must be less than the Tick Basis value, but not less than 1.

  • There is no limit to the number of Options strike prices you can enter on a single contract.
  • Quantum does not currently support different strike price intervals for different ETO contract months.
