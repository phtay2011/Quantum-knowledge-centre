Pools

Pools (user-defined consolidations of cash flows across different groups and transactional levels) are a way of creating consolidated cash flows across different groups and transactions. They enable you to see aggregated cash flows in a flexible, multi-dimensional way.

Pools build on Quantum's powerful Groups functionality by allowing you to build simple parent/child relationships with any combination and level of Quantum Groups.

A Pool can include any Quantum group or transaction-level record, at any level.

Within a Pool, you can choose to include and exclude individual groups and transaction levels to tailor the resulting cash flow selections to your exact needs. You can create:

  • simple Pools that consolidate cash flows across a multi-level group,
  • more complex Pools that consolidate cash flows across more than one multi-level group, and
  • complex Pools that are refined at the group and transaction levels.

Pools exist only within CashXplorer and cannot be used in other parts of Quantum.

For CashXplorer Pools to work correctly, users must have rights to both Transactor and Group levels.

Flow Certainty

Flow Certainty reflects the confidence of whether a cash flow will occur. Each cash flow has a rating that suggests how likely it is to occur.

  • The Name of the Flow Certainty is prefixed with a number reflecting the certainty order. The closer the number is to 01, the more likely it is the cash flow will occur.
  • The Short Name of the Flow Certainty is a number reflecting the reverse certainty order. The higher the number, the more likely it is the cash flow will occur.

Pools related to Intraday Matching include flows that are Probable and Possible matches.

Performance

CashXplorer resolves the four account-related Pools (Account, Account Entity, Account Currency, and Bank) very quickly, as these Pools are based on data that is always retrieved. The other Pool Types take longer to resolve. Therefore CashXplorer displays cash positions and cash flow forecasts more quickly when there are fewer non account-related Pool Types defined.

Within each Pool Type, the number of pools defined does not significantly affect performance.

The Unallocated Pool does not impact performance.
