FID Calculation Examples

Example 1

The FID calculation methods show how to assess the FID amount, butQuantum must also determine:

  • whether the FID amount is a recovery or a deduction

    TIP: With Money Market deals, you receive principal amounts on a Borrowing, and pay principal amounts on an Investment.

  • where in the Deal Map the FID amount is to be inserted

FID is processed by offsetting the FID amount against interest or principal repayments. A FID cash flow will be inserted on the same Effective and Action dates as the first interest payment. This cash flow will be netted by the settlement process, so that not all the interest monies are paid out.

A deal map with FID Deductions would be identical, except that:

  • the FID RECOVERY cash flow would become a FID DEDUCTED cash flow, and
  • all signs would be reversed.

However, the FID amount may well be larger than the interest amount. When this happens, Quantum does not pay the interest out. Instead, an interest accumulator event is used instead of the interest payment cash flow. The FID is charged at the next interest payment which will include the interest accumulated in the first interest period.

In this example, note that:

  • Once again, deductions would be processed as FID DEDUCTED with all signs reversed.
  • Although this deal is a short term deal, it did not attract the short-term calculation due to the principal being lower than the minimum principal eligible for short-term treatment.
  • On a short-term deal that has been rolled over on a daily basis, no interest is paid out as the total of the accumulated interest is less than the FID amount. Quantum recovers the FID on the Maturity Date where the total interest, FID amount and principal repayment are all netted together.

NOTE: If you customize deals and or transact structured deals, you must manually enter the FID entries for the correct amount on the appropriate date.
