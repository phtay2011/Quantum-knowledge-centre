Effectiveness methods

Effectiveness methods determine how effectiveness calculations are made, while effectiveness targets provide the measure against which the system can compare results to establish whether the hedge relationship is effective.

Define methods and targets on the Hedge Strategy page's Effectiveness tab. These become the defaults on the Hedge Relationship page when you select that Hedge Strategy page.

NOTE: You cannot change any effectiveness settings once the hedge relationship has been saved. Selected parameters are applied consistently to the linked deals for the duration of the hedge relationship.

  • If Short-cut effectiveness is selected, no further settings are required.
  • If Do not apply effectiveness targets (IFRS9) is selected, the system will post the hedge accounting revaluation entries regardless of whether the effectiveness test results exceed the effectiveness targets (also specified on the Effectiveness tab). You can still select a Retrospective and or Prospective Test and specify targets that will be useful when making a forward-looking assessment of effectiveness.
  • You can select a Retrospective (backward-looking) effectiveness method of either Cumulative Dollar Offset or Periodic Dollar Offset. An effectiveness Range is required, and effectiveness is evaluated relative to its upper and lower limits.
  • In some cases, you can also select a Prospective or Retrospective effectiveness method of Regression.
  • If required, you can enter Hypothetical deals for Swaps, Interest Rate and Currency Options, FX Deals, or Futures and Exchange Traded Options (ETO), respectively.
  • For each linked Money Market,Security, Interest Rate Swap, or Currency Swap deal (and subject to other conditions), you can also specify either Clean or Dirty pricing. Otherwise, Dirty pricing applies. For more details, see Clean or Dirty Pricing under Effectiveness testing.
