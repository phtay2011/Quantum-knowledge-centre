Terms used in Money Market and Securities Formulas

Term

Description

AI

Accrued interest

AIPP

Accrued interest for the period preceding the payment of the first coupon of a partly paid issue

an

Sum of present value of 1 paid, per period in arrears, for n periods

Basis

Interest basis (days in the year); usually 365, but in some cases 360 or 366 may be used

CPIdate

Reported Consumer Price Index for a given date

CV

Contract Value

ce

Cum/ex factor - if cum then 1, else 0

cfi

Amount of the ith cash flow

d

Days in period

D

Coupon % per annum

d(p)

Nominal discount rate % convertible p times per annum

DCV

Capital Value of a deal

DWACV

Weighted Average Capital Value of a deal in a portfolio of Money Market deals

D1

Coupon payable on next coupon anniversary (that is, on date1)

date-1

Previous coupon anniversary date (the effective date in the system)

date0

Value date

date1

Next coupon anniversary date date the next coupon will be paid or interest accrued to

(Must be a valid business day)

date2

Date of the coupon anniversary following date1

(Must be a valid business day)

datei

Date of ith coupon anniversary (must be a valid business day)

dateissue

Date the security was issued

datePPi

Date of ith part payment

dateSXD

Special ex div date

dateXD

Ex div date

dfi

Discount factor applicable to the ith cash flow

e

Annual inflation rate %

Days to next coupon

FV

Face Value

g

Coupon paid per period

i

Effective annual yield %

Nominal yield % convertible p times per annum

Real nominal yield % convertible p times per annum - this is adjusted for inflation

IssuePrice

The minimum tender price (that is, the sum of partly paid installments)

In the Euro markets, the Issue price is par.

m

Number of part payments that have been made prior to Value Date

n

(Number of coupon anniversaries remaining) - 1

p

Number of coupon payments per annum

pc

Clean price per £100 nominal

pd

Dirty price per £100 nominal

PFV

Portfolio Face Value; total of Face Values of all deals in a portfolio of Money Market deals

PWACV

Weighted Average Capital Value of a Securities portfolio

q

Total number of part payments between issue date and first coupon date

Ref_CPIdate

CPI index offset required for calculating inflation-adjusted value at a given date for an Indexed Bond - for example, Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS)

RPI-12

Retail price index 12 months prior to the latest RPI available on Value Date

RPI0

The latest retail price index available on value date.

RPI1

Retail price index 8 months prior to the date of the next coupon payment anniversary

RPI2

Retail price index 8 months prior to the second coupon payment anniversary

RPIissue

Retail price index % 8 months prior to the stock issue

RPIdate0

Date the most recent RPI figure applies to (assumed to fall on the first of the month)

For example, an RPI announced in October 2004 for August 2004 would have:

RPIdate0 = 01/08/2004

RPIdatei

Date that applies to the RPI figure for the ith coupon anniversary (assumed to fall on the first of the month)

For example, if the fifth coupon anniversary of a particular bond falls on Sunday 26/09/2004, then the RPI figure applicable to this coupon will be dated eight months before, so:

RPIdate5 = 01/01/2004

RPIdaten+1

Date that applies to the RPI figure at maturity (assumed to fall on the first of the month)

For example, if a particular bond has a Maturity (redemption) date of 26/09/2005, then the RPI figure applicable to this coupon will be dated eight months before, so:

RPIdaten+1 = 01/01/2005

t1

Tax rate % applicable to coupon payments; used in net redemption yield calculations

t2

Tax rate % applicable to capital redemption, as used in net redemption yield calculations

(Held constant at zero at present. It will be fully implemented at a later date.)

Gdate

Multiplication factor for calculating inflation-adjusted value at a given date for an Indexed Bond - for example, Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS)

v

Discount factor (1+i)-1

vn

Maturity Discount factor = (1+i/p)n
