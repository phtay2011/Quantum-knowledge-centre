Inception Rule in Cross Currency Swaps (IFRS 9)

Only the aligned element of the Cross Currency Basis Spread change can be deferred in OCI.

At inception, Quantum calculates the currency basis between the hedging instrument (the HEDGE) and the hypothetical instrument (the HYPOTHETICAL) using a weighted average calculation of the recorded currency spread of each linked Cross Currency Swap deal.

Weighted Average Basis Value of Actual Trades > Weighted Average Basis Value of the Exposures

  • The Fair Value change in the Basis Value of the Exposure(s) is posted to the OCI.
  • The difference in the change in the Basis Value of the Exposure(s) and the change in the Basis Value of the actual (HEDGE) is treated as an excluded component and remains in P&L.

Weighted Average Basis Value of Actual Trade(s) < Weighted Average Basis Value of the Exposure(s)

  • The lower value of the change in the Basis Market Value of the Hedges and the change in the Basis Market Value of the Exposures is posted to OCI Balance (Hedging Costs) for the entity (Posting Rules, BALANCE SHEET tab).
  • Any remainder of the change in fair value of the hedge's Basis Value is treated as an excluded component and remain in P&L.

NOTE: The Cross Currency Basis Spread field on the individual Cross Currency Swap deal, designated into the hedge relationship, is used to record the basis spread for that deal.

The calculation depends on whether the legs of the linked Cross Currency swap deals are in the Testing and Posting Currency* of the hedge relationship.

* NOTE: The Testing and Posting Currency of the hedge relationship is either the base currency of the Entity value or the currency used in the linked deal/s.
