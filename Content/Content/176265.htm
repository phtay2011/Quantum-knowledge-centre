Projected Cash Flows in Hedge Accounting

You can use Projected (and Actual) Cash Flows* to enter 'non-financial' transactions as Firm Commitments or Forecast transactions. Once linked to a Hedge Relationship, the resulting Exposure becomes eligible for Hedge Accounting.

*CAUTION: If you use these transactions within a Hedge Relationship, it is recommended that you enter them as Actual Cash Flows. The system will image changes in the face value of Actual Cash Flows, ensuring that the correct value is used in revaluation calculations over the life of the Actual Cash Flow. This facility is not supported for Projected Cash Flows.

If you choose to enter these transactions as Projected Cash Flows, you must be aware that changes in their face value are not included in revaluation calculations. These types of cash flow are generally converted to Actual Cash Flows (see below) when the anticipated amounts are paid or received.

TIP:

  • To add and maintain any deals in a Hedge Relationship, use the Linked Deals tab on the Hedge Relationship page.
  • The Dealing page shows a Hedge Relationships tab if the current deal is a Linked Deal within one or more Hedge Relationships. If so, you can quickly access the Hedge Relationship from this tab by clicking on the HR Deal Number in the Allocations grid.

To convert a linked Projected Cash Flow to an Actual Cash Flow

  1. On the Actual Cash Flow page, recall the Projected cash flow deal.
  2. In the page menu, select one of the following options.
    • Freeze Unchanged* (strongly recommended) - the original Projected Cash Flow is frozen or locked in as it was at the time of the conversion, and an Actual Cash Flow is added to the Hedge Relationship.
    • Delete Projected - the original Projected Cash Flow is deleted when the Actual Cash Flow is created.
    • Freeze with Balance - the Projected Cash Flow is modified so that the Amount equals the difference between the original Projected Cash Flow and the new Actual Cash Flow, and the Projected Cash Flow is then frozen.
    • Leave with Balance - the Projected Cash Flow is modified so that the Amount equals the difference between the original Projected Cash Flow and the new Actual Cash Flow (the Projected Cash Flow is not frozen).

      *NOTE: It is strongly recommended that you select Freeze Unchanged.

  3. If you selected Freeze Unchanged, a message alerts you to the fact that a change to the Projected Cash Flow will affect a Hedge Relationship. Click Yes to continue.

    The Projected Cash Flow is updated and given a new Value Date (Conversion Date -1 day). A new Actual Cash Flow is added to the Hedge Relationship from the conversion date.

  4. If you selected another method in Step 2, a message asks if you want to remove the Projected Cash Flow from the Hedge Relationship. Then:
    • If you choose No, the Projected Cash Flow will remain in the Hedge Relationship.
    • If you choose Yes, a second message asks if you want the Actual Cash Flow to replace the Projected Cash Flow in the Hedge Relationship. If you choose Yes again, the Projected Cash Flow will be replaced by the new Actual Cash Flow. If you choose No, the Projected Cash Flow will be removed from the Hedge Relationship and not replaced by the new Actual Cash Flow. You will then need to manually review the status of that Hedge Relationship. The system will unwind calculations for the Hedge Relationship back to the last valid revaluation before this change occurred.s
