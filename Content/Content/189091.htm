Accounting for Murabaha Instruments (Money Market)

When accounting for the Murabaha deal In Quantum, the Profit is recognized on the Balance Sheet at the start of the deal, and then amortized to the Profit and Loss account. In addition, contingent entries (consisting of the Principal and the sum of the Profit amounts) are posted between Settlement and Maturity Dates.

Define Accounting Rules, Murabaha Instrument
  • To enable contingent accounting for Murabaha, make the following selections:
    1. From the Balance sheet timing options, select Settle Date.
    2. From the Contingent Accounting Settings make the following choices:
      • Apply from deal date to settlement date. Select this option. The contingent entries will be the Principal amounts.
      • Apply settlement to maturity date.Select this option. The contingent entries will be the sum of the Principal and the Profit amounts.
  • To define the Murabaha accrual treatment, that is to ensure that the Profit is amortized to the Profit and Loss account, set the Interest Accrual Amortization option to Amortize.
Define Posting Rules, Murabaha Instrument

The Murabaha contingent accounting entries post to different accounts so it is necessary to define these accounts in the Murabaha instrument Posting Rules. They are defined as Off Balance Sheet accounts and are represented as Borrowing Exposure, Investment Exposure and Exposure Contra.

Define G/L Account Template, Murabaha Instrument

A specific General Ledger Account Template will be required when the following conditions are met:

  • Contingent accounting between Settlement and Maturity date is enabled
  • The underlying instrument is based on either Yield - Periodic or Yield - On Maturity formula, for example, the Murabaha instrument
