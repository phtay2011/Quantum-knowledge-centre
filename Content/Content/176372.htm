Regression

Quantum Six applies standard regression calculations. The system uses an Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) calculation to determine the likely future effectiveness of the hedge relationship at its inception and on an ongoing basis.

When regression is applied, the system generates a range of dates prior to the position date. At each of these dates, the system revalues each linked deal.

Retrospective regression testing at inception tests the hedge relationship "deal" based on four historic data points, to ascertain a regression data set. These historic data points use an image of the deal at inception and look at historic rates to provide the data set based on the Intervals selected.
However, past the inception date, regression data points have Position/Revaluation Date and Rate Date moved back in time so that the system revalues deals based on what the deal looked like at the historical interval selected, using rates for that time.

The system plots the series of changes in the value of the total Hedge position against an equivalent series of changes in the value of the total Exposure position. Where there are multiple Hedge deals and/or multiple Exposure deals linked to a single hedge relationship, the system applies the sum of each side.

The system compares the two sets of results and evaluates relative to an intended or target range of results to see how effectively the hedge relationship has performed, and so is likely to perform in the future.

For Retrospective Effectiveness using Regression, Quantum Six also supports effectiveness calculation using a single leg of the Swap, while the OCI movement is based on the complete Face Value.

Sample and measurement parameters

You specify the parameters that describe:

  • the set of dates used as the sample, and
  • the measurements used to evaluate the results.

The sample determines what revaluation rate dates the system includes in the calculations, and the measures determine the line of best fit and the acceptable tolerance boundaries on either side of it.

Regression calculations generate a series of rate dates, and:

  • if the market value of a deal is calculated by the system, the rate dates are used to locate the rates that determine the market value of the deal at each date, but
  • if market values are imported, there are no rates or rate dates to draw on. Instead, each date generated by the regression calculations is regarded as a position date, and the system uses those position dates to locate the corresponding imported market values for each date.

Where a hedge relationship is effective, both sets of results remain within the required tolerance levels, indicating that movement on one side corresponds to the movement on the other.

Revaluation results

When the system performs a revaluation, it compares the actual results with the targets to see whether they are within tolerance and whether effectiveness has been achieved.

A position processing or deal revaluation run recognizes where regression calculations are required, and completes them as part of a normal revaluation run. See Hedge Reclassification.

You can drill down from the Deal Revaluation's Retrospective or Prospective tab (as applicable) for details of how the displayed results were derived. See Hedge Relationships, Revaluation Analysis.

If posting is requested, results also appear on the Hedge Relationship page's Effectiveness tab,. Displayed results include a tolerance range for the slope and the intercept. These are based on the target slope and intercept settings and the selected confidence interval.

NOTE: The Position Date is always included as one of the points in the Regression calculation.
