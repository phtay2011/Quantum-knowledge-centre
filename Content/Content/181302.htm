Security Reval Method (Accounting Rule)

(Available only when Revalue security deals is selected.)

Select the valuation method for calculating the amount to post to Gain or Loss on revaluation, for deals using this rule.

The methods available depend on whether the Money Market Type setting is Standard or Embedded, as detailed in the following topics:

  • Standard Reval Methods (Security Accounting Rule)

  • Embedded Reval Methods (Security Accounting Rule)

Standard Reval Methods (Security Accounting Rule)

(Available when Revalue security market deals is selected and Money Market Type is Standard.)

Select the valuation method for calculating the amount to post to Gain or Loss on revaluation, for standard Security deals using this rule. The system calculates the amount by marking the deals to market. Select one of the following.

  • Bills - to post the incremental Gain or Loss in the deal currency, using the specified Interest Rate name and Interest Rate type, for:
    • floating rates - applying the Bills revaluation methodology, or
    • fixed rates - discounting the individual cash flows from the cash flow dates to the revaluation date.
  • Bills net - to post the Gain or Loss on revaluation as for the Bills option, but posting any gains net of Withholding Tax.
  • Projected - to post the incremental Gain or Loss in the deal currency, using the specified Interest Rate name and Interest Rate type, for:
    • floating rates - applying the Projected revaluation methodology, or
    • fixed rates - discounting the individual cash flows from the cash flow dates to the revaluation date.
  • Projected Net - to post the Gain or Loss on revaluation as for the Projected option, but posting any gains net of Withholding Tax.
  • FRN-Coupon (for use only with FRN instruments) - to use an adaptation of the Market method, applying the following Swap rates:
    • Short Rate (used to discount the next coupon) - taken from the rate record identified by the associated Interest Rate and Rate Type settings at the date of the coupon (the same Short Rate used with the Market method), and
    • Swap Rate (used to discount the remaining future cash flows) - taken from the rate record identified by the Par Rate Name for Zero Curve field, at the Maturity Date of the deal (unlike the Market method, this rate is not converted to a discount factor).
  • Market Cum - to post the gain or loss on revaluation, calculating the amount by marking the securities deals to market value as if they are Cum Dividend, irrespective of whether each security is Cum or Ex Dividend.

    Securities purchased Ex Dividend are valued as Ex Dividend while still in their first Ex Dividend period.

  • Market Ex - Post the gain or loss on revaluation, calculating the amount by marking the securities deals to market, taking account of whether the security is Cum or Ex dividend.

    For securities instruments for which no Market Valuation method is available, such as a customized Bond, the Bills method will be used.

  • Imported - to use an externally derived market value, by importing a revaluation amount for each deal.

    In Revaluation Reporting, Position Reporting, or Position Processing runs, the profit/loss is calculated using the Book Value and the imported Market Value. See also Imported market values.

    TIP:Hypothetical deals can also use imported market values.

Embedded Reval Methods (Security Accounting Rule)

(Available when Revalue security market deals is selected and Money Market Type is Embedded.)

Select the valuation method for calculating the amount to post to Gain or Loss on revaluation, for Security deals with embedded options (for example, Callable/Puttable bonds) using this rule. Select one of the following.

  • Market Cum - to post the gain or loss on revaluation, calculating the amount by marking the securities deals to market value as if they are Cum Dividend, irrespective of whether each security is Cum or Ex Dividend.

    Securities purchased Ex Dividend are valued as Ex Dividend while still in their first Ex Dividend period.

  • Market Ex - Post the gain or loss on revaluation, calculating the amount by marking the securities deals to market, taking account of whether the security is Cum or Ex dividend.

    For securities instruments for which no Market Valuation method is available, such as a customized Bond, the Bills method will be used.

  • Imported - to use an externally derived market value, by importing a revaluation amount for each deal.

    In Revaluation Reporting, Position Reporting, or Position Processing runs, the profit/loss is calculated using the Book Value and the imported Market Value. See also Imported market values.

    TIP:Hypothetical deals can also use imported market values.

If you are licensed for Analytics, the following revaluation methods are also available.

  • Black Derman Toy - to use a given initial zero rate term structure of interest rates and a specification of the yield volatilities of long rates to build a tree representing the evolution of the interest rates.
  • Black Karasinski - to use this more general model where the reversion rate is decoupled from the volatility.
  • Ho Lee - to use this no-arbitrage model of the term structure, presented as a binomial tree of bond prices with two parameters: the Short Rate Standard Deviation and the Market Price of Risk of the Short Rate.
  • Hull White - to use this model: similar to the Ho Lee model, with the addition of mean reversion.

For more details, see Bond Option Pricing Formulas.
