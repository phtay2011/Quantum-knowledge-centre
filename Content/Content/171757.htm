Tradable Yield Securities Instruments

A Tradable Yield instrument (or Auction Note) is a Bond-style Securities instrument with a Maturity Date that can be extended. It is used for dynamic hedging, portfolio insurance, and short-term investment.

Changing interest rate

The coupon rate on the instrument is usually reset for each successive interest period. The interest rate for the next period is determined by bids received by Dutch auction* near the end of the current period. How often the interest rate is reset depends on the individual instrument.

There is a single payment of interest for each period as its maturity date is reached. If the maturity date is extended, you choose whether to capitalize, pay, or accumulate the interest for the period just passed. If the interest rate is to change, it does so when the deal is rolled.

TIP: In a Dutch auction, an organization re-offers securities in the market place as their maturity date approaches. The organization accepts those bids that offer the highest market clearing price (the lowest yield to the bidder). It then allocates notes or preferred shares to the successful bidders at the new interest rate.

The interest rate is determined by auction, so it reflects the current credit standing and perception of the issuer, as well as reflecting conditions in the market place.
If, on maturity, you win the bid and retain the stock, the instrument is rolled. If you are not successful, the instrument matures.

Capital amount

You can also change the capital amount when a deal is rolled. Quantum will calculate the next coupon on the new Face Value.

Setting up the Security Instrument

To enter a Tradable Yield instrument (such as Auction Preferred Stock) in Quantum, set Formula Timing to BOND ROLLING. This combines the interest calculation for a single period Bond with the rolling interest timing of a typical Yield instrument.

    NOTE:
  • Always set the Coupon Frequency on the Pricing tab to AT MATURITY. There are no interim coupon payments on a Tradable Yield instrument, and the coupon is fixed from settlement (or roll) date to maturity.
  • Each instrument must have a unique CUSIP number.
  • Tradable Yield instruments do not support Zero Coupon Bonds.

Rolling the Deal

If you win the bid and retain the stock, the instrument is rolled. If you are not successful, the instrument matures. Use the Quotes page (Dealing menu) to record the quotes of other competitors.

To extend the maturity date for a deal based on a Tradable Yield instrument, use the Roll Deal function (Security deal page menu) and enter the new maturity date and the new coupon.

NOTE: Where the Counterparty is not the original issuer, you will need to enter a new transaction in order to extend the maturity date.

Price of Capital

This is the lowest price necessary to sell the full issue becomes the price at which all securities in the issue are sold. It is assumed that the price of the capital at the roll dates is the same as the original purchase price.

Accounting

You can set up a Tradable Yield instrument to use Straight Line accounting. This accounting method is also used if you close out the deal prior to maturity. Quantum calculates and posts the gain or loss on closeout. It generates the accruals prior to and after closeout, and revalues the deal taking into consideration any portion of the deal that is closed out.

If flexible settlement agreements result in ad hoc interest flows, customize the deal. Quantum can close out a Level 1 customized deal on a Straight Line basis.

A Tradable Yield instrument is generally marked-to-market by price. If the deal is customized, however, a market valuation is not usually appropriate. In this case, Quantum usually switches to the BILLS method to revalue the deal.

NOTE: Tradable Yield Instruments (Bond-Rolling) do not support Matched Closeouts.
