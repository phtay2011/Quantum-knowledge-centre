About Initial Margins

Initial Margin is the collateral that a Broker requires from you in respect of all Futures and Exchange Traded Option (ETO) contracts open in your account on any given day. The amount of this margin changes daily according to the type, composition, and risk characteristics of your open positions.

Generally, each day the Futures and ETO exchanges will mark-to-market the position, and if the value of this position drops below the initial margin amount, the exchange will generally require additional deposits in the form of a variation margin (see Note below).

You can make only one Initial Margin entry to a particular Broker account on any one day. If additional margin is required intraday, recall the existing Initial Margin deal for that day, and change the amount entered, and save the deal with the new amount (keeping the original deal number).

Initial Margin Deals do not have any unique deal actions. The main function is to manage margin accounts with futures/ETO brokers that must be maintained in order to trade or hold futures/ETOs positions. This is done as follows:

  1. Create an Initial Margin deal, which creates an initial margin position.
  2. Adjust an existing Initial Margin deal in order to add/subtract from the current margin balance.

NOTE: An Initial Margin deal simply enables Quantum to model what is happening on the Broker statement. No cash flows are associated, and no deal map is created.

Use a Futures Margin Query run (via the Reporting menu) to report on the Initial Margin and calculate what the Variation Margin should be. Use a Margin Summary view.

Use a Futures Margin Processing run (via the Accounting menu) to post Initial Margin adjustments and Variation Margins. When generating an updated Margin Summary, the query uses the Initial Margin Deal's closing balance for the selected margin date. It indicates the various margins due to the Broker specified when you set up the query, and calculates the total margin excess or shortage.

You can then check that the Broker account balance covers the margin requirements and, if necessary, arrange to transfer the shortage.

To pay the Broker in response to a margin call, use the Cash Transfer page. This results in an actual cash flow across the account.
