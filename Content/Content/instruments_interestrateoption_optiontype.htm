Option Type

One of the following:

  • For a Simple Interest Rate Option:
    • CAP - An agreement, between the seller or provider of the cap and a borrower, to limit the borrower's floating interest rate to a specified level for a period of time.
    • FLOOR - An agreement, between the seller or provider of the floor and an investor, guaranteeing that the investor's floating rate of return on investments will not fall below a specified level over an agreed period of time. Floors can be transacted alone, but are typically associated with a COLLAR transaction.
    • COLLAR - A variation on a CAP where the seller of the Collar agrees to limit the borrower's floating rate of interest to a band limited by a specified Cap rate and Floor rate. The transaction is two-sided, with the Cap side determining the buying or selling of the Collar. That is, buying a Collar comprises buying a Cap and selling a Floor; while selling a Collar comprises selling a Cap and buying a Floor.
  • For a Bond Interest Rate Option:
    • CALL - An option that gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy the underlying Money Market bond at the exercise price.
    • PUT - An option that gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to sell the underlying Money Market Bond at the Exercise price.
  • For a Swaption:
    • PAYER - An option on a valid Swap giving the buyer the right to pay a fixed interest rate and receive a floating interest rate.
    • RECEIVER - An option on a valid Swap giving the buyer the right to receive a fixed interest rate and pay a floating interest rate.
