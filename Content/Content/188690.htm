Key Concepts (Brazilian Yield Compounding)

Brazilian CD

A Brazilian CD is a Money Market Instrument (not the CD in the Quantum sense where a CD is the Certificate of Deposit formulas used in MMDEALS).

Investing in a CD can be the first step towards investing in another instrument. For example, you invest in a CD, pay the standard withholding tax, then create a Swap deal to swap the CD into CDI. You could also swap the CD into PTAX.

CDI

CDI is an Interbank interest rate (with characteristics similar to libor).

Investing in a CD can be the first step towards investing in another instrument. For example, you invest in a CD, pay the standard withholding tax, then create a Swap deal to swap the CD into CDI. You could also swap the CD into PTAX.

PTAX

PTAX is a synthetic US Dollar rate used in Brazil.

Investing in a CD can be the first step towards investing in another instrument. For example, you invest in a CD, pay the standard withholding tax, then create a Swap deal to swap the CD into CDI. You could also swap the CD into PTAX.

Brazilian Yield Compounding Instruments

Interest rates in Brazil are strongly influenced by daily issued Government Interest Rates or Indexes. One of these is the Taxa de Juros de Longo Prazo, or TJLP, which is used for a type of Brazilian Bond. This instrument differs from others as it can be traded with various rateset periods - for example, Quarterly or Annual.

You can use Quantum to define a TJLP, and to record and price deals that use TJLPs, using the Quantum YIELD - CDI formula with a Days Convention of Actual/360.

This enables you to:

  • Define instruments and enter deals that use the annualized government bond rate (TJLP).
  • Calculate interest for the deal based on your chosen compounding or rateset frequency.
    In most areas of Quantum the interest rate must be consistent with the compounding frequency. To enable the definition of TJLPs, your chosen interest rate convention can be entered independently of the chosen rateset/compounding frequency.

The selected YIELD is the yield that appears on Confirmations and Reports. Ratesets occur based on the selected Rateset Frequency.

This functionality is available for Money Market Instruments and Deals, and for Swap Instruments and Deals.

NOTES:

  • The Withholding Tax rate changes over time. If a deal spans multiple years, each cash flow that occurs within a particular year is taxed at the Withholding Tax rate for that year.
  • For most CDI deals, the Withholding Tax will have already been paid when the CD was dealt. However, if you have a deal that requires Withholding Tax, use the Taxes Applicable field on the Other Details tab of the Swap deal to switch on the tax.

Unwinding a CD, CD/CDI, CD/PTAX

You can partially or fully unwind a CD, CD/CDI, or a CD/PTAX if you do not want to hold the deal until its full maturity date.

If you unwind a CD that has been swapped into a CD, a CDI, or a PTAX, and that had a floating rate, you need to manually adjust the underlying CD.

To adjust the CD

  1. Create another (contra) money market deal.
  2. Include a portion of WHT and accrued interest as well as the principal. Do this by inserting an ad hoc amount for the WHT and an ADHOC INTEREST amount for the interest earned up to the closeout date.

Characteristics of the Quantum Yield - CDI Formula

The Yield - CDI formula has the following characteristics.

  • It can be used with the ACTUAL/360BD and ACTUAL 252/BD Business Day Conventions, which calculate interest for business days only - that is, excluding holidays and weekends.
  • Ii is based on the CDI rate which is quoted daily.
  • The Yield CDI Discount functionality values Brazilian Yield Compounding Instruments(TJLPs).
  • For examples of the formula, see Yield - Compound Formula.
