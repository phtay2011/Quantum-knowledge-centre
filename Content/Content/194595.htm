Bank / Broker / Counterparty accounts

Bank/Counterparty Account Conversion - End Date added

(First introduced in Release SP 6.7.1, August 2018.)

You can now optionally apply an End Date filter when processing cash flow movements for Bank Account Conversion or Counterparty Account Conversion, where:

  • End Date must not precede Effective Date,
  • cash flows falling on the End Date are included in conversion run, and
  • leave End Date blank to update all future flows.

History

Previously, you could only update all future flows.

Bank interest settlement date as last working day of month

(First introduced in Release SP 6.7.1, August 2018.)

You can now set settlement of Bank Account interest to the last business day of monthly, quarterly, semi-annual or annual interest periods. For interest periods that end on a weekend or holiday, the interest will then be settled on the preceding business day.

Implementation

On the Bank Account page's Interest tab or Third Party Interest tab when:

  • Calculate Interest is YES or GROUP, and
  • Frequency (under Periods) is NOTDAILY nor ACCOUNTING PERIODS, and
  • Interest Period Ends is END OF MONTH, then...

...the Cash Flow Due field's drop-down list now includes MODIFIED FOLLOWING BUSINESS DAY.

When this new setting is selected, interest cash flows will occur on the last business day of the final month of each interest period, EXCEPT THAT if an interest period ends on a non-business day:

  • if Allow cash flows on non-business days is selected, the interest date will not be changed, otherwise...
  • the interest date is moved back to the preceding business day.

History

Previously, interest settlement before the end of any interest period was not supported.

Broker Account page - Delivery Details tab added

The Broker Account page now includes a Delivery Details tab.

Use this tab to record, for individual broker accounts, the following delivery details for Account Transfer and Actual Cash Flow payments to or from broker accounts (in the same way as currently for bank accounts):

  • the default Counterparty and Counterparty account for payments to and from this account,
  • allowable Payment Methods for this account, and
  • the default Payment Method.

NOTE: These defaults may still be changed on broker flows in Settlements processing.

The Static Data Import Spreadsheet now also supports these additional items (for both Broker and Bank Accounts).

History

Previously:

  • Counterparty delivery details and the Payment Method could not be defaulted for Account Transfer and Actual Cash Flow deals for broker accounts,
  • you could only set Counterparty delivery details and Payment method in Settlements processing (on selected broker flows), and
  • the Static Data Import Spreadsheet did not support Delivery Details items for Broker accounts, nor for Bank accounts.
