Indexed Gilt Formulas

Index-linked Gilts differ from conventional gilts in that the coupon stream and redemption amount are weighted by the Retail Price Index (RPI). RPI figures are maintained using the Update Index page (Processing menu).

The system projects future RPIs by scaling the RPI figures already entered.

Pricing

The pricing calculations differ, depending on the timing of the valuation relative to maturity date, and on whether the RPI percentage eight months prior to the second coupon (RPI2) is known.

NOTE: The discount factor (V) used throughout the Indexed Gilt formulas is based on an effective annual yield calculation rather than the nominal yield.

UK Government Semi-Annual Yield formulas

From Release SP 6.7.1, Quantum supports the UK Government's Semi-Annual Yield formulas for Indexed Gilt deals based on Security instruments that have the following settings:

  • Formula Timing is INDEXED GILT PERIODIC (Details tab),
  • Day Count is ACTUAL/ACTUAL (Dates tab),
  • Yield Type is UK SEMI ANNUAL (Pricing tab),
  • Assumed Inflation Rate and Coupon Rate Rounding (Pricing tab) are set to the required values, and
  • Index Name is set to an appropriate Index Rate Series that gives Published Dates (Other Details tab).

Deals based on such instruments apply indexation and rounding to coupon rate and accrued interest calculations, using the assumed inflation rate and specified Index Rate Series to apply the UK Government's Semi-Annual Yield formulas.These formulas

For more details, see Indexed Gilts - new Yield type.

Final period pricing

Pricing in the final period for instruments using the Indexed Gilt formula is defined in the Final Period Pricing area on the Pricing tab of the Money Market or Securities instrument page.
