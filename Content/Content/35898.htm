Principal Accounting (Money Market & Securities Accounting Rule)

Available on the New or Edit dialog boxes for a Money Market & Securities Accounting Rule.

This setting determines how to present the Principal Amount in the Balance Sheet for Discounted and Bond-style instruments.

  • FV - to post the Face Value amount of the transaction to the Balance Sheet,
  • FVC - to post only the nominal Face Value amount (available only when Balance Sheet Timing is SETTLE DATE and the Contingent accounting option Apply from deal date to settlement date is selected), or
  • NET - to post the Net value of the transaction to the Balance Sheet

The actual posting entries differ depending on the Balance Sheet Timing setting, as detailed in the following topics.

  • Principal Accounting Options - Deal Date Accounting

  • Principal Accounting Options - Settlement Date Accounting

Principal Accounting Options - Deal Date Accounting

When Balance Sheet Timing is set to DEAL DATE, select one of the following:

  • FV - to post the Face Value amount of the transaction to the Balance Sheet, using the Face Value G/L account codes defined in the applicable Posting Rule record.

    On Deal Date, a four-legged entry is created to post:

    • the Face Value to the FV Investments Balance Sheet account.
    • the Interest amount to a Balance Sheet account on the Deal date (as an off-setting Debit if the instrument is a liability, Credit if an asset). Accruals Processing then posts from this account to the Profit & Loss account.
    • the Capital Premium/Discount to the Capital Premium/Discount Accrual account. Accruals Processing then posts from the Capital Premium/Discount Deferred account to the Profit & Loss account. Position Processing (Revaluation) then posts from the Capital Premium/Discount Deferred account to the Capital Premium/Discount Accrual account.
    • an entry to the Unsettled Payables Balance Sheet account. On Settlement Date, this amount is posted from Unsettled Payables to the Bank account.
  • NET - to post the Net Value amount of the transaction to the Balance Sheet, using the Net Value G/L Account codes defined in the applicable Posting Rule record.

    On Deal Date, a three-legged entry is created to post:

    • the Face Value less the Discount (or plus the Premium) to the NV Investments Balance Sheet account.
    • the interest amount to a Balance Sheet account (Interest/Coupon Income Accrual, as an off-setting Debit if the instrument is a liability, or Credit an asset). Accruals Processing then posts from this account to the Profit & Loss Account.
    • an entry to the Unsettled Payables Balance Sheet account. On Settlement Date, this amount is posted from Unsettled Payables to the Bank account.

      TIP: The main difference in the NET setting between Deal Date accounting and Settlement Date accounting is the treatment of Capital Premium/Discount. For Deal Date Accounting, Accruals Processing posts from the Capital Premium/Discount Deferred account to the Profit & Loss account, and Position Processing (Revaluations) posts from the Capital Premium/Discount Deferred to the Capital Premium/Discount Accrual account. On Maturity, an entry is created to post from the Capital Premium/Discount Accrual to the NV Investments account.

Principal Accounting Options - Settlement Date Accounting

When Balance Sheet Timing is set to SETTLE DATE, select one of the following:

  • FV - to post the Face Value amount of the transaction to the Balance Sheet, using the Face Value G/L account codes defined in the applicable Posting Rule record.

    On Settlement Date, a four-legged entry will be created to post:

    • the Face Value to one account.
    • the Interest amount to a Balance Sheet account on the Settlement Date (as an off-setting Debit if the instrument is a liability, Credit if an asset). Accruals Processing then posts from this account to the Income Statement.
    • the entry to the bank account.
    • the Capital Premium/Discount to a Balance Sheet Accrual account (Capital Premium/Discount Accrual). Accruals Processing then posts from the Capital Premium/Discount Accrual account to the Profit and Loss account.

    This presents the total value of the deal in the Balance Sheet from the Settlement Date.

  • FVC (Face Value Contingent) - to post only the nominal Face Value amount.

    NOTE: This option is available only when the Contingent accounting option Apply from deal date to settlement date is selected.

  • NET - to post the Net Value amount of the transaction to the Balance Sheet, using the Net Value G/L Account codes defined in the applicable Posting Rule record.

    On Settlement Date, a three-legged entry will be created to record:

    • the Face Value less the discount (or plus the Premium) to one Balance Sheet account (NV Investments).
    • the interest amount to a Balance Sheet account (Interest/Coupon Income Accrual, as an off-setting Debit if the instrument is a liability, Credit if an asset). Accruals Processing then posts from this account to the Profit & Loss Account.
    • the entry to the Bank account.

      TIP: The main difference in the NET setting between Settlement Date accounting and Deal Date accounting is the treatment of Capital Premium / Discount. For Settlement Date accounting, Accruals Processing then posts from the Capital Premium / Discount Accrual account to the Profit & Loss account. On Maturity, an entry is created to post from the Capital Premium / Discount Accrual to NV Investments.
