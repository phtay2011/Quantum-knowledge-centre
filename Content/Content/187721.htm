Customizing Interest Rate Option Deals

NOTE: To create and update customized deals, you must have the required Module Security rights for the Dealing application.

You can customize deals to have irregular or modified cash flows to suit specific circumstances. You can change an event date or cash flow amount on the Deal Map tab, and insert or delete items as required.

Before customizing a deal, you must create and save a regular (un-customized) deal that matches the mandatory settings of the irregular deal such as Counterparty, Entity, and Dealer. In addition, you should supply the Principal, Interest Rate, Start Date, and Maturity Date as a minimum.

NOTE: When customizing Deal Map items, the Cash Flows and G/L Entries tabs are dynamically updated, enabling you to preview the effects of your customization before saving the customized deal. In this preview state only:

  • the bank accounts shown on the Cash Flows tab are determined by the Bank Account Default rules and DO NOT reflect any previous changes to accounts that may have been made on the Settlements page (Our Instructions). However, such changes are retained for cash flows dated prior to the customization date, and again show once the deal has been saved.
  • the G/L entries are shown as if the deal was a new one, so WILL NOT include process or reversal G/L entries if those exist for a deal previously saved. When the deal is saved after customizing, the G/L entries will be re-calculated.

Use the Dealing page's Deal Map tab to perform customizing actions on an existing deal. For procedure summaries, see:

  • Customizing Single Deal Map Items
  • Bulk Replace and Insert for Deal Map Items

If you amend a Deal Map item that has already been Actioned, or add an item that impacts items that have already been Actioned:

  • you may have to manually reprocess those items. This is indicated by the Flow Lifecycle value, which you can display as a grid column on the Cash Flows tab, in Settlements and Confirmations results grids, and use as a filter in deal or flow queries or result views (for more details, see Flow Lifecycles, States, and Phases); also...
  • depending on whether or not Deal Map item changes have a financial effect, the changed deal will be badged as Customized - Major or Customized - Minor, respectively.

Components

For each of the events within the transaction, the Component value describes the type of event - for example Premium Amount, Rate Set, Interest, Repay Notional*, and so on.

For items that are not events, this is simply a descriptive label.

NOTE: Multiple Repay Notional items apply to Simple Interest Rate Option deals when Amortizing/Accreting is selected. You can add, modify, or delete Repay Notional items, subject to the Customization Restrictions for Simple Options (see below).

Principal Balance

This grid column of read-only values applies only to Simple Interest Rate Options (Caps, Floors, or Collars).

The values indicate the Notional Cap or Floor Principal amount as at each principal-related Cap or Floor event, respectively.

For a Collar when Allow amortization is selected (Deal Details tab), the Cap and Floor balances must remain equal and opposite for each leg - that is, they sum to zero. See also the Note above under Components.

Customization Restrictions

General

You cannot change a Repay Notional amount for a calculation period in which the option has been exercised or lapsed. You must first roll back the Exercise or Lapse action before you can changing the Repay Notional amount.

You cannot change the Component setting of the following items where present (although you may delete them, subject to other restrictions):

  • NOTIONAL
  • REPAY NOTIONAL
  • STRIKE RATE

You can insert new items with the following Component settings only:

  • PREMIUM AMOUNT
  • RATE SET
  • REPAY NOTIONAL (Simple Cap, Floor, and Collar options only)

Bond Options

Only coupons and principal amortization cash flows may be added, changed, or deleted. You cannot customize coupon amounts, as they are automatically recalculated.

To change the sEttlement Amount of priced transactions, change one of the Components of the price rather than the Settlement Amount itself. This is to ensure that the correct accounting treatment is being applied to the transaction. For example, changing the Bond Income item will update the Bond Settlement item automatically.

Simple Options (Caps, Floors, and Collars with Amortizing/Accreting Schedule)

Multiple Repay Notional items apply to Simple Interest Rate Option deals when Amortizing/Accreting is selected. You can add, modify, or delete Repay Notional items,provided that:

  • the Effective Date is a Rate Set date,
  • there is only one Repay Notional item for a given Rate Set Date, and
  • the cumulative sum of Repay Notional amounts does not cause the Notional Balance to pass through zero (thus changing sign).
