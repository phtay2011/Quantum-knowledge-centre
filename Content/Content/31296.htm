Calculations and Postings during Hedge Reclassification

A full Hedge Reclassification run can revalue deals that are linked to a Hedge Relationship, calculate the effectiveness of the Hedge Relationship, and perform any transfers and postings that are required.

Revaluing Deals

To revalue a deal that is linked to a Hedge Relationship, Quantum applies the rates and revaluation methods described for each Transaction Type in the Accounting Rules tab of Accounting Setup. The Nature of Risk specified on the Deal Details tab of the Hedge Relationship also influences postings. For example:

  • If Exchange Rate Risk is specified for a Hedge Relationship that has no exchange component, then no revaluation amount is posted.
  • If Interest Rate Risk is specified, then all non-floating cashflows are excluded from the effectiveness calculations.

Hedge Accounting also introduces the concept of risk components, with revaluation changes split into parts. You can choose which risk component is hedged by a linked deal, in the Reval Method column on the Linked Deals spreadsheet. Quantum provides default components based on a matrix of Transaction Type, Hedge Type, and Accounting Rule. See Defaulting of revaluation methods.

No Accounting Rule record exists for Actual and Projected Cashflows. You can supply revaluation details for these only on the Linked Deals tab of the Hedge Relationship.

See also: Capturing Realized P&L in OCI for FX Deals and Currency Options below.

Calculating Effectiveness

Generally Quantum calculates the effectiveness of a Hedge Relationship at the following points, in addition to the Position Date effectiveness:

  • The point of dedesignation, when a deal is removed prior to the end of the Hedge Relationship.
  • The date from which a change is made that effects the value of a sub deal; for example, a partial close out.
  • The end of a specified accounting period.

The applicable value change since the last revaluation date is for the entire day regardless of the contract rate. That is, the value change is determined from the rate at the beginning of the day compared to the rate at the end of the day.

Where a Hedge Relationship contains an Actual Cashflow deal that has changed, the effectiveness calculation relies upon the deal images of the Actual Cashflow to determine the correct values based on the image dates. See Actual and Projected Cashflows in Hedge Accounting.

  • Projected Cashflows do not have deal images and therefore do not work in this way.
  • Quantum automatically applies the effectiveness method associated with each Hedge Relationship.

See also: Capturing Realized P&L in OCI for FX Deals and Currency Options below.

Calculating Posting Amounts

Posting calculations are made when a Hedge Relationship is proved highly effective. To calculate posting amounts, Quantum:

  1. Calculates the period gain/loss for all derivatives in the Hedge Relationship, using the settings defined within Ledger Properties.
  2. Converts the amount to base currency.
  3. Calculates the ineffectiveness amount.
  4. Calculates the transfer amount for either Other Comprehensive Income (OCI) for Cashflow hedges, or Mark to Market (MTM) for Fair Value hedges.

The determined value is transferred from Profit and Loss to OCI or MTM, depending on the hedge type.

The Position Inquiry function calculates and posts the incremental General Ledger entries that reflect the OCI/MTM posting. If any OCI/MTM Release actions are triggered, the Hedge Reclassification run automatically processes them as well. See Events tab, Hedge Relationship.

Cashflow hedges with interest rate risks have the margins on Interest Rate Swaps (SI) and Money Market Securities (SE) excluded from posting and effectiveness calculations.

See also: Capturing Realized P&L in OCI for FX Deals and Currency Options below.

Deferred Tax Postings

Where you have specified to report OCI net of tax, the OCI postings are:

  • Balance sheet

    Divided between the following two balance sheet accounts:

    • OCI (current)
    • Deferred tax

      Where Deferred tax = total OCI to be posted * tax rate defined for the entity

      NOTE:Deferred tax assets / liabilities should be measured at tax rates that are expected to apply. When the OCI Tax rate is changed, a tax reassessment adjustment is generated at the next revaluation date to align the total deferred tax amount to the updated OCI Tax Rate. See Example: OCI Tax Reassessment Adjustment.

  • Profit & Loss

    The Profit and Loss entry shows the total OCI amount.

Adjustments for the deferred tax amount are also made in the following instances:

  • Amortization of OCI

    Where amortization of OCI is specified, the deferred tax amount is split out and amortized separately, consistent with the net OCI.

  • Spot to spot adjustment

    Where the hedge relationship is subject to currency translation spot to spot adjustments, and those adjustments are also net of deferred tax.

  • Forward points with amortization

    Where the amortization of forward points on an FX Deal is also net of deferred tax, then the following occurs on the freeze date:

    • OCI postings are frozen with the deferred tax posted separately.
    • The remaining forward points are amortized from the Freeze Date to maturity.

Capturing Realized P&L in OCI for FX Deals and Currency Options

Revaluation

When revaluing a linked AC, PC, FX, or CO deal at Maturity Date, the Maturity Gain/Loss setting for the Hedge Relationship affects the Effectiveness calculation, as follows.

When Maturity Gain/Loss is...

Effectiveness calculation is based on...

REALIZED

The Maturity Date Spot rate for the linked deal's rate type

UNREALIZED

The prior day's revaluation

Position Inquiry

The maturity revaluation for linked AC, PC, FX, or CO deals changes when the Maturity Gain/Loss setting for the Hedge Relationship is set to REALIZED. The process takes into account the transaction type, and whether the revaluation is required for Hedge Effectiveness or Reclassification, as follows.

  • Effectiveness calculations are based on the Maturity Date End of Day Spot Rate record for the linked deal's rate type (as set for the Hedge Relationship) for all transaction types - that is, for:
    • FX - Vanilla
    • AC - Vanilla
    • PC - Vanilla
    • NDF/CO - Cash or Physical Settlement
  • Hedge Reclassification calculations for linked:
    • FX, AC, and PC deals - are based on the Maturity Date End of Day Spot Rate specified for the Ledger (as set in Currency Translation /Realized Rate Type on the Ledger tab in Accounting Static Data)
    • CO/NDF deals with Cash Settlement - are based on the Exercise / Settlement Amount
    • CO/NDF deals with Physical Settlement - are based on a zero value

When the Maturity Gain/Loss option is set to UNREALIZED for the Hedge Relationship, the pre-existing method applies - that is:

  • Position Inquiry revaluation is to the day before Maturity, and is posted as the Unrealized Gain/Loss.
  • On Maturity, this Unrealized Gain/Loss is reversed and Exchange Fluctuation then calculates the Realized Gain/Loss using current spot rates (as at Maturity Date).
  • Hedge Effectiveness and Reclassification calculations for linked:
    • FX, AC, and CO deals - are based on the Maturity Date End of Day Spot Rate specified for the linked deal instrument (as set on the Accounting Rules tab in Accounting Static Data)
    • PC Deals are based on the Maturity Date End of Day Spot Rate specified for the Ledger (as set in Currency Translation /Realized Rate Type on the Ledger tab in Accounting Static Data)
