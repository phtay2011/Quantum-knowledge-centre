Currency Translation Phases

You can use the Currency Translation Processing page for calculating currency translation at both Deal level and Account level. This is done by breaking down the processing into two phases: Deal level in Phase one, Account level in Phase two.

Phase One: Deal Level Currency Translation

Phase one will calculate the currency translation gains/losses for each deal that has Deal Level currency translation activated. For these deals, this phase of the process will:

  1. Identify all postings to or from the Balance Sheet for each deal. These are called events and include:
    • cash flows,
    • accruals,
    • withholding tax,
    • deal revaluations, and
    • ad hoc interest and amounts.
  2. Calculate Unrealized gains/losses. For any event that has not been settled on the date that you are running Currency Translation, an Unrealized gain or loss will be calculated and posted to the Unrealized Foreign Exchange Gain or Loss accounts.

    NOTE: You can define separate Unrealized Foreign Exchange Gain or Loss accounts for different events - for example, Bank, Principal, Interest, and so on.

  3. Calculate Realized gains/losses. If any of the events is a cash flow, Quantum transfers the Unrealized Foreign Exchange Gain or Loss on all the events relating to that cash flow to Realized Foreign Exchange Gain or Loss and identifies those events as Realized.

    NOTE: A single deal can have many Realized currency translation gains or losses. For example, amortizing deals will create a realized currency translation gain/loss on each principal repayment. Also, where interest is paid periodically, a realized currency translation occurs on each interest payment.

NOTE: Deal Level currency translation is applied by selecting a Translation setting option in the Accounting Rule for each deal type.

Phase Two: Balance Sheet Account Level Currency Translation

Phase two ensures that the closing balances of all Balance Sheet accounts are accurately revalued in the reporting (base) currency. Therefore, during this phase, currency translation gains and losses are calculated at an Account Level, taking into account any gains/losses already calculated at Deal Level. During this second phase the process will:

  • identify any Balance Sheet accounts that require Account Level currency translation,
  • include the currency translation gain/loss amounts calculated at Deal Level, and
  • calculate Unrealized gains/losses for the account.

To ensure that the closing balances of all Balance Sheet accounts are accurately revalued, the final step is to revalue the balance of every Balance Sheet account that has the Revalue option selected in the Accounting Rule for the deal type.

Phase one will have already accounted for foreign currency exchange (FX) gains or losses relating to open deals, so this final part of the process effectively revalues the residual balance, if one exists. Any variation between the actual base balance and the base revaluation amount is posted as an Unrealized exchange gain or loss, as appropriate.

Bank and Taxation accounts are the only accounts that would normally have residual balances after transactions have been settled, although other accounts can contain items that are not deal-related (and hence, residual) such as G/L Journal entries.

Account Level currency translation is controlled by the G/L Account Templates.
