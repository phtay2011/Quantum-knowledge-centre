Analytics: Pricing for Dual Currency Deposits

You can now capture and price Dual Currency Deposits (DCDs) in Analytics.

A DCD is a foreign exchange linked deposit in which the principal can be repaid at maturity in an alternative currency, depending on the Strike Rate and the Spot foreign exchange rate. A DCD combines a Money Market deposit with a Currency Option. The premium for the option is paid through the yield.

You can use the new Dual Currency Deposit Pricing page to:

  • enter both the time deposit Money Market deal and the corresponding Currency Option deal (instead of having to use two separate Pricing screens),
  • solve for Strike,
  • solve for Enhanced Yield, and
  • calculate the Value of the DCD.

Implementation

Instruments

To define the defaults for a DCD deal, you must set up two instruments, as follows.

  1. On the Currency Option Instrument page, define the option default settings, ensuring that:
    • CO Type is Vanilla European,
    • Call/Put is Call, and
    • CO Link Type is Standard.
  2. On the Money Market Instrument page, define the Time Deposit default settings and link to the instrument defined in Step 1, ensuring that (on the Details tab):
    • Fixed/Floating is set to Fixed,
    • Frequency is set to Maturity, and
    • the new Currency Option Instrument field (under Dual Currency Deposits) is set to the instrument you defined in Step 1.

NOTE: If you will be exporting DCD deals from Analytics to Quantum, then the Money Market Instrument must be set up in Quantum and imported into Analytics. The new Currency Option Instrument field will then require updating in Analytics.

DCD deals exported from Analytics to Quantum will be split into one Money Market deal and one Currency Option deal, with no link between them.

Pricing

Use the new Dual Currency Deposit Pricing page to create and maintain your DCD deals.

An example showing this page is given below.

The header and most tabs show the Money Market (time deposit) side on the left and the Option side on the right. The fields default to the settings in the respective instruments.

Valuations (Details tab)

Use the Price sub-tab (at the bottom of the Details tab) for the valuation of both the Money Market and Currency Option trades, and the DCD as a whole. The DCD Value is the sum of the Money Market PV and Currency Option Market Position amounts. When the DCD Value is zero, the DCD is priced at fair value.

NOTE: It is possible to recall either the Money Market deal or Currency Option deal part of a DCD deal (using the Money Market or Currency Option Pricing page respectively), and do a separate valuation on that deal alone. (No changes can be made to the deal using either of these pages.)

Valuation Control

You should set up Money Market and Currency Option Valuation Control details such that the comparable Market Rate components agree for all your DCD deals.
