30E/360ISDA

The 30E/360ISDA day count convention is referred to as 30E/360 (ISDA) by the International Swaps and Derivatives Association Incorporated (ISDA).

This day count calculation is based on the following values:

  • Numerator = the number of days in period
  • Denominator = 360

30E/360ISDA Formula

The 30E/360ISDA day count formula is:

Day Count Fraction = ([360 x (Y2 - Y1)] + [30 x (M2 - M1)] + [D2 - D1]) / 360

where:

  • Y1 is the year in which the first day of the period falls.
  • Y2 is the year in which the day immediately following the last day included in the period falls.
  • M1 is the calendar month in which the first day of the period falls.
  • M2 is the calendar month in which the day immediately following the last day included in the period falls.
  • D1 is the first calendar day of the period, except where that day is the last day of February, OR that day is 31, in either case D1 will be 30.
  • D2 is the calendar day immediately following the last day included in the period, except where that day is the last day of February (but not the Termination Date), OR that day is 31, in either case D2 will be 30.

NOTE: All the formula values are expressed as a number.

Rolling Formula

The coupon amount calculated prior to a deal being rolled will not change after the deal has been rolled even if the maturity date is the last day of February. For example, assume the following Bond-Rolling, where:

  • Formula = Bond - Rolling
  • Face Value = 1,000,000.00
  • Coupon = 5.00%
  • Settlement Date = 30 Nov 2006
  • Maturity Date = 28 Feb 2007
  • Coupon Amt = 122,222.22 = 1,000,000.00 x .05 x 88/360

If the deal is rolled for another quarter to 30 May 2007, then the original coupon amount of $122,222.22 is not recalculated even though it is no longer the current maturity date. This logic is used by all formulas whose Interest Timing = Rolling
