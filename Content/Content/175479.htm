Calculating Cap/Floor Pricing Values

Use the Price tab (inside the Details tab) to calculate market values for the deal, as follows.

  1. Change the Position Date, Rates Date, As At Date, Currency, and/or Exchange rate settings, as required.
  2. Select the Valuation method: Repricing or Projected.
  3. If Collar is Yes:
    • Select the results View you want: Cap, Floor, or Collar.
    • Enter the Cap Volatility and Floor Volatility rates to be used.
  4. In the Calculate field (selectable for new deals only), select either:
    • Price - to calculate the price, or
    • select a variable (Volatility or Strike) to solve for a specified value, AND enter that value OR percentage in the respective Price field next to your selection.
  5. Click the Price button.

The Market grid displays the calculation totals for your chosen settings. The Valuation tab displays the calculation results for each valuation cash flow.

TIPS: On the Valuation tab:

  • In some cases, not all result cells apply.
  • For each cash flow, both Position and Per Unit values are shown for each of the following result values.
  • For Collar options, a Cap grid lists caplets and a second Floor grid lists the floorlets.

The result values are summarized below.

  • Value - total market value
  • Delta - a measure of the change in the Option's value relative to a change in the price of the underlying
  • Gamma - a measure of the change of the Option's Delta, relative to a change in price of the underlying
  • Vega - the sensitivity of the Option's value to a one percentage point change in the volatility of the underlying
  • Theta - the rate of change of the Option's value as its time to maturity decreases, with other terms held constant (usually negative, as Options with longer periods before maturity have greater time value)
  • Rho - a measure of the sensitivity of the current Option value relative to a change in the domestic interest rate
