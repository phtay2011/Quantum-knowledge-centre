Hedge accounting for Cash Flow hedges

The system is able to mark a derivative to market using Accounting Rule settings, and post the gain or loss on it to Profit and Loss (earnings). As with other revaluations, the amount posted is the incremental change in value of the derivative since the last revaluation. This can cause fluctuations in earnings over time.

To smooth this effect, a derivative intended as a Hedge can be accounted for as part of a documented and valid hedge relationship. If eligible for the Quantum Hedge Accounting process, it can be revalued as part of the hedge relationship, and a different revaluation method may be applied.

For a Cash Flow hedge the incremental change in the value of the hedge* is compared with the incremental change in the value of the Exposure*.

Use an Accounting > Position page to determine whether the Hedge is effective, and to transfer the effective portion of the changed amount to OCI* on the Balance Sheet. Only the ineffective portion of the revaluation remains in Profit and Loss.

*TIP:

  • In a hedge relationship, the hedge (deal) is the derivative or non-derivative that is hedging the Exposure, and the Exposure (deal) is the transaction that is being hedged.
  • OCI (other comprehensive income) is an accounting presentation term for a Balance Sheet account. The account is in the Shareholder Equity section of the Balance Sheet. FAS allows you to defer the entire fair value change in the hedging instrument to OCI. See Cash Flow Hedge Posting for more information.
  • Postings to OCI relate to the hedge relationship, not individual deals within it. These postings occur even if the measured effectiveness is within the specified limits.
  • There is no concept of OCI for a fair value hedge.

Maturity

If a hedge relationship has not been changed since its inception, the balance in OCI on the Derived Maturity Date is reversed to Profit and Loss on that date (by default), to coincide with the effect of the Exposure.

For a 100% effective hedge relationship, the amount to be transferred from the Balance Sheet to Profit and Loss would exactly equal the Derivative. This would provide a net effect on the Profit and Loss of zero.

NOTE: The amount moved from Profit and Loss to the Balance Sheet can never exceed the amount that was posted to Profit and Loss.

See OCI calculation rules for the calculations associated with effective, under-effective, and over-effective hedge relationships.

To specify a different date for the transfer to Profit and Loss:

TIP: You could use this if cash flows were actually only due to be made later than the Derived Maturity Date, for example.

  • On the Hedge Relationship page's Events tab:
    1. Ensure that Set release date is selected, and set Release From and Release Date as required.
    2. Select Transfer to another entity at release date, select the Transfer to Entity, and set the required Release Date of Transfer.

OCI and Dedesignation Adjustment

Because of changes to the hedge relationship, some OCI amounts may be released during the life of the hedge relationship.

If the hedge relationship is changed between inception and maturity (for example, if you change the percentage of the derivative allocated to the hedge relationship), OCI dedesignation adjustment occurs. This can be initiated by a manual change on the Linked Deals tab or by a change to the underlying deal. See Dedesignation.

A Hedge may be taken out of a hedge relationship if, for example:

  • it fails to provide adequate cover, or...
  • it makes a profit.

The system revalues the hedge relationship before and after the change and, if the Hedge or part of the Hedge is removed from the hedge relationship, disposes of the OCI relating to the dedesignated portion according to the Hedge Dedesignation setting specified on the Deal Details tab of the Hedge Relationship page, which may (for a CASH FLOW Hedge and EXPOSURE or HYPOTHETICAL linked deal) be changed on the Dedesignation form.

The options are for the affected OCI amount to be:

  • Released or reclassified immediately (if a CASH FLOW Hedge and the dedesignated linked deal is an EXPOSURE or HYPOTHETICAL) on the effective date of the dedesignation,
  • DEFERRED to the Accounting End Date of the hedge relationship, or...
  • AMORTIZED to the Accounting End Date of the hedge relationship.

    NOTE: When the OCI/MTM is amortized from the Maturity Date to the Release Date, the Release Date is the Accounting End Date.

Even after this dedesignation occurs, the hedge relationship will continue until the underlying Exposure has matured.

OCI and deferred tax

FAS 130 Reporting Comprehensive Income provides that OCI be reported net of deferred tax. You can specify this functionality for Cash Flow Hedge Relationships and the deferred tax rate to apply, for the required Entities (Accounting tab, Entity page).

  • For information about winding back a hedge relationship, see Adding a deal with a start date prior to revaluation.
  • Deferred tax assets / liabilities should be measured at tax rates that are expected to apply. When the OCI Tax rate is changed, a tax reassessment adjustment is generated at the next revaluation date to align the total deferred tax amount to the updated OCI Tax Rate.
