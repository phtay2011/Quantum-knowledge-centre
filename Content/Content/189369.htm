About Variation Margin on Futures and ETO Deals

Additional margins may be payable when open Futures or ETO deals are revalued. To calculate and report on these, enter the End of Day prices for the contracts (see Futures Rates and Exchange Traded Option Rates) and then run a Futures Margin query (either in Reporting or Processing mode).

The system can report Variation Margin separately on settled or open contracts, and transfer the Variation Margin to different accounts on settlement date (or later if a Variation Margin Payment Delay applies - see below). It can post:

  • Variation Margin on open Futures and ETO deals,
  • Realized Gain/Loss on closed Futures and ETO deals, and
  • Aggregate Variation Margin on expired Futures and Options deals - these are deals that have been cash settled and have passed their settlement date.

For a description of the posting rules used, see Futures & ETOs Posting Rules.

NOTE: Variation Margin, Premium, Closeout, and Gain/Loss related cash flows are all posted to the Clearing Broker account. If a Variation Margin Payment Delay applies, you can also post Variation Margin expense and income to specific Balance Sheet accounts if required (if there is no margin delay, these accounts are not involved and the posting is direct from Cash to P&L or vice versa, as applicable).

Variation Margin Payment Delay

You can delay the payment date for settlement of the Variation Margin by entering a number of business days. in the Margin Settlement Offset field for the underlying Futures and ETOs Instrument (Futures Contract Details tab). The Variation Margin will then be paid that number of business days after the deal Settlement Date.

The default setting is blank or zero (no delay).
