Yield Margin for Zero Rate Type

Rate Type = Zero and Margin Type = Curve

The Yield Margin Curve is applied to the term structure of the Zero Curve and the Zero Margin Frequency determines the Frequency of the Zero rates defined in the Yield Margin.

How they are applied will depend on the Zero Margin Frequency defined in the Rate Definition area of the Yield Margin page. The system calculates an annualized Zero Curve, therefore where the Zero Margin Frequency is defined as:

  • Annual, the rates are applied directly to the Annualized Zero rates calculated by Analytics (as they have the same Frequency)
  • Semi Annual / Quarterly / Monthly, the the Zero rates are converted to the same frequency as the Yield Margin, then combined to the Yield Margin rates and converted back to annualized (see Example: Semi Annual below for details).

Annualized

That is, where Zero Margin Frequency is set to Annual.

The following rules are applied:

  • The Zero Base rate is calculated as the Zero Curve of the Par rates only.
  • The Zero Margin takes the Yield Margin (when Annual) and applies to the corresponding Days/Dates directly.

    As the Yield Margin has a Zero Margin Frequency of Annual, then the Margin is applied directly for the corresponding Days using the following rules. If:

    • margins do not exist at the start of the Zero Curve, the first margin point is carried back to the start of the Zero curve.
    • margins do not exist to the end of the Zero Curve, the last margin point is carried forward to the end of the Zero curve.
    • a Margin does not exist for a given point on the curve (but points before and after exist), the margin rate is straight-line interpolated for the missing point.
  • Zero Rate is the sum of the Zero Base Rate and Zero Margin columns.
  • The Discount Factor field is applicable to the Rates defined in the Zero Rate column.
  • No Margins are applied to the Par Rates with this setup.

Semi Annual

That is, where Zero Margin Frequency is set to Semi Annual (same for Quarterly/Monthly).

The following rules apply:

  • The Zero Base rate is calculated as the Zero Curve of the Par rates only.
  • Where the Zero Margin Frequency is defined as Semi Annual, Quarterly, or Monthly then the Zero Base rates are converted to the same frequency as the Yield Margin, then combined with the Yield Margin rates and converted back to annualized, as represented in the Zero Rate column. If:
    • margins do not exist at the start of the Zero Curve, the first margin point is carried back to the start of the Zero curve.
    • margins do not exist to the end of the Zero Curve, the last margin point is carried forward to the end of the Zero curve.
    • a Margin does not exist for a given point on the curve (but points before and after exist), the margin rate is straight line interpolated for the missing point.
    • The Zero Margin is then calculated as the difference between the Zero rate and the Zero Base rate.
  • The Discount Factor field is applicable to the rates defined in the Zero Rate column.
  • No Margins are applied to the Par rates with this setup.

Example: Calculation

This example works its way through the following steps using the 730 Days point in the graphic above.

Step 1. Convert the annualized Zero Base rate to the Zero Margin Frequency defined in the Yield Margin Rate definition.

For example:

0.048604108704 = 2*((1+0.0491946856)^(1/2)-1)

Step 2. Combined the Zero Margin Frequency defined Zero rate with the Yield Margin and Covert to Annual (this returns the Zero rate in the graphic above).

For example:

5.1551915775 = ((1+(0.048604108704+0.0023)/2)^2-1) * 100

Step 3. To return the Zero Margin, subtract the annualized Zero Base from the annualized Zero Rate.

For example:

0.232521723 = 5.15199157750 - 4.9194698550

Rate Type = Zero and Margin Type = Deal Maturity

The number of days from the Position Date to the Maturity of the deal is used to interpolate a rate from the Yield Margin curve. This interpolated rate is applied as a flat spread to the Zero curve.

How the interpolated Yield Margin is applied depends on the Zero Margin Frequency defined in the Rate Definition area of the Yield Margin page. The system calculates an annualized Zero Curve therefore, where the Zero Margin Frequency is defined as:

  • Annual, the interpolated Margin is applied directly to the annualized Zero rates calculated by Analytics (as they have the same Frequency)
  • Semi Annual/Quarterly/Monthly, the Zero rates are converted to the same frequency as the Yield Margin, then combined to the interpolated Yield Margin rate and converted back to annualized (see the Example: Semi Annual below for details).

In these examples there are 730 days (corresponding Margin 0.23) from the Position Date to the maturity of the swap.

Annualized

That is, where Zero Margin Frequency is set to Annual.

The following rules are applied. The:

  • Zero Base Rate is calculated as the Zero Curve of the Par Rates only.
  • Zero Margin is the interpolated Margin from the Yield Margin record for the Days from Position Date to Maturity Date.
  • Zero Rate is the addition of the Zero Base Rate + Zero Margin.
  • Discount Factor applies to the Rates in the Zero Rate column.

Semi Annual

That is, where Zero Margin Frequency is set to Semi Annual (same for Quarterly/Monthly), for example:

The following rules apply:

  • The Zero Base Rate is calculated as the Zero Curve of the Par Rates only
  • Where the Zero Margin Frequency is defined as "Semi Annual / Quarterly / Monthly" then:
    • the initial Margin is interpolated from the Yield Margin record for the Days from Position Date to Maturity Date,
    • the Zero Base Rates are then converted to the same frequency as the Yield Margin, and..
    • then combined to the Yield Margin rates and converted back to annualized. This represents the Zero rate.
  • The Zero Margin is then calculated as the difference between the Zero rate and the Zero Base rate.
  • The Discount Factor field is applicable to the Rates defined in the Zero Rate column.
  • No Margins are applied to the Par rates with this setup.
