About Hypothetical Derivative deals

Hypothetical derivative deals can be used to measure the effectiveness of cash flow hedges where the Short-Cut method criteria have not been met.

The FAS Board Implementation Guidelines [G7] for Swaps and [G20] for Interest Rate and Currency Options reference this solution explicitly where the Short-Cut method cannot be applied to Cash Flow hedges.

TIP: [G7] and [G20] allow the full market value of the option or swap, assuming no ineffectiveness, to be posted to Other Comprehensive Income (OCI).

In the Default perspective, you can:

  • create a Hypothetical "perfect hedge" deal for an exposure,
  • link this Hypothetical deal to your exposure and live hedge deal in a one-to-one Hedge Relationship, and
  • compare your actual live hedge deal to the Hypothetical perfect hedge to measure its Effectiveness in Revaluation Analysis.

CAUTION: The Quantum Six solution provides complete user control over the Hypothetical deal creation. Therefore it is recommended that you follow these guidelines carefully to ensure that an accurate measure of Effectiveness is generated.

Hypothetical derivative deals can be created for the following types of Quantum deal:

  • Currency Options (Vanilla)
  • FX Deals
  • Interest Rate Options (Caps, Floors, and Collars)
  • Swap Deals (Currency, Interest Rate)
  • Commodity Swaps / Commodity Exposures

Hypothetical derivative deal types are also supported in Analytics. They can either be replicated from Quantum or directly entered in the applicable Analytics Pricing pages. See Hypothetical Deals in Analytics.

Exclusions

Hypothetical derivative deals do not support:

  • one-to-many, many-to-one, and many-to-many relationships,
  • Auto Allocation, and
  • Deal allocation for Swap deals.

Deal entry limitations

Hypothetical deals are used for analysis purposes only and are not (and never will be) live deals; therefore, the actions available to the deals are limited. The following functions are unavailable on deal entry pages for Hypothetical deals:

  • Cash Flows tab, G/L Entries tab, and Analysis Codes tab

    Hypothetical derivative deals do not generate cash flows and General Ledger entries - therefore, they will not appear in Settlements processing. They are used for analysis only and are not (and will never be) live deals.

  • Exercise action

    Lapse is the only action supported for Interest Rate Options. For Effectiveness calculation purposes, Lapse operates as Exercise.

  • Approvals and Confirmations

    Hypothetical deals are not (and never will be) live deals, and as such do not require either approval or confirmation.

NOTE: For a full explanation and description of when to use the Hypothetical Derivative method, see the relevant documentation from the Financial Accounting Standards Board.
