Linking Deals in a Hedge Relationship

Use the Linked Deals tab to link the underlying deals making up a hedge relationship.

Introduction

  • The hedge relationship must already exist, or be in the process of being created, before you can complete this tab.
  • To achieve the desired accounting result and assess effectiveness, your selection of revaluation method for each linked deal is crucial. See Setting up Hedge Accounting.
  • Identify each deal as either a Hedge or an Exposure, specify what percentage and, where relevant, which component (part) of the deal is in the hedge relationship.
  • Use the Hedge Type and Designated Risk values to ensure you link deals that are suitable for the hedge relationship.
  • Linked deal details are listed in the grid on this tab. These are the underlying deals (or sub-deals) that make up the hedge relationship. For some types for deal, for example commodity swaps, details are listed to the level of period (strip), so you can link to a period.
  • The Face Value Ratio displayed below the grid gives the ratio of Hedge to Exposure for all linked deals. The adjacent Ratio Date shows when that ratio was calculated.

    An Exposure (deal) is the transaction that is being hedged in a hedge relationship.

    A Hedge (deal) is the derivative or non-derivative that is hedging an Exposure in a hedge relationship.

  • A Hedge Rate appears below the grid when Exposure deals for Interest Rate Options or Forward Rate Agreements are revalued. That rate indicates that the Market Value calculation for the derivative is net of the derivative strike rate.
  • Interest Rate Hedges can only hedge floating Money Market or Security deals, and you cannot mix different types of Interest Rate Hedges in the same hedge relationship. For example a hedge relationship cannot contain both a Forward Rate Agreement Hedge and an Interest Rate Option Hedge.
  • If a deal is part of one or more hedge relationships, the In HR badge appears in its dealing page.

To link deals or hypothetical deals

For instructions on how to link deals, see Linking Hypothetical Deals. The instructions are basically the same for deals or hypothetical deals.
