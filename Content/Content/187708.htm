Ad Hoc Cash Flows for Money Market Deals

Generally, ad hoc payments and receipts represent cash adjustments to account for credit risk fees, discrepancies between dealt swap margins and current market margins, or accrued interest purchased or receivable.

IMPORTANT NOTE: When customizing Deal Map items, the Cash Flows and G/L Entries tabs are dynamically updated, enabling you to preview the effects of your customization before saving the customized deal. In this preview state only:

  • the bank accounts shown on the Cash Flows tab are determined by the Bank Account Default rules and DO NOT reflect any previous changes to accounts that may have been made on the Settlements page (Our Instructions). However, such changes are retained for cash flows dated prior to the customization date, and again show once the deal has been saved.
  • the G/L entries are shown as if the deal was a new one, so WILL NOT include process or reversal G/L entries if those exist for a deal previously saved. When the deal is saved after customizing, the G/L entries will be re-calculated.

To insert an ad hoc amount

  1. Select the grid row you want to insert above.
  2. In the grid menu, do one of the following:
    • IF it is present - select Customize*, otherwise...
    • select New.

      *TIP:Customize is available only for Money Market and Security deals based on Instruments with Formula Timing of BOND PERIODIC or FRN - CGL PERIODIC (New also appears but offers only Profiling options - see Other Bond Formulas - Customization Restrictions).

  3. in the New Map Items dialog box, enter or select the following values.
    • Effective Date - the date when the ad hoc cash flow is to occur; the date Interest is calculated to.
    • Action Date - the Action date of the ad hoc cash flow (for example, the date interest is paid or received).
    • Component - select ADHOC AMOUNT or ADHOC INTEREST, as required.
    • Amount/Rate - the amount of the ad hoc cash flow.
  4. Click OK.

    The new Deal Map item row is inserted in the grid. If you have added or modified dates, rows will be re-sorted if required.

  5. Save your change.

TIP: Ad hoc items are given Minor customization status. See Revaluing and Accruing Customized Money Market deals.

Timing of Ad Hoc Cash Flows

The applicable Accounting Rule's Balance Sheet Timing setting determines whether you can insert ad hoc cash flows prior to the Settlement Date of the deal.

  • If Balance Sheet Timing is set to Deal Date you can insert ad hoc amounts from the Deal Date onwards. These will be accrued or amortized depending on the Accounting Rule's Accrue adhoc amount setting. For items to be accrued, the accrual start date is the Deal Date.
  • If Balance Sheet Timing is set to Settlement Date, you cannot insert ad hoc amounts before the Settlement Date. For items to be accrued, the accrual start date is the Settlement Date.

Accruals on an Ad Hoc Cash Flow

Ad hoc interest is accrued according to the Accounting Rule's Interest Accrual Amortization setting.

Ad hoc amounts are accrued according to the Accounting Rule's Adhoc Amount Translation setting.

When either of these is set to:

  • AMORTIZE - the applicable ad hoc amount is amortized from its Effective Date to the deal Maturity Date OR to the Effective Date of the next ad hoc amount if there is one; or
  • ACCRUE - the applicable ad hoc amount is accrued from the deal start date to the Effective Date of the ad hoc amount OR from the Effective Date of the previous ad hoc amount, if there is one, to its own Effective Date.

NOTES:

  • Accrual and amortization periods for ad hoc amounts cannot overlap. To manipulate start and end dates, insert zero ad hoc amounts with appropriate dates. The same applies to ad hoc interest items.
  • If set to ACCRUE, for example, the system will accrue the first ad hoc amount entered from the deal start date to the Effective Date of the ad hoc amount. To force the accrual to begin some time after the deal start date, enter a zero ad hoc amount on the date you want the real accrual to begin. Because the real accrual period cannot overlap the zero ad hoc amount, its accrual will begin the day after the Effective Date of the zero amount.
  • Ad hoc amount and Ad hoc interest items entered before the Settlement Date of a deal that uses Deal Date accounting will be accrued from the Deal Date onwards.
