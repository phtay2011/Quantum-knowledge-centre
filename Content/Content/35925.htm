Accrue issue fee (Repo Accounting Rule)

Use this option to determine how to treat the fee component of a Repurchase Agreement (Repo).

When Accrue issue fee is NOT selected, no accruals are required and the fee is taken straight to Profit & Loss on the fee payment date, as specified on the deal.

When Accrue issue fee is selected:

  • You must also select the Fee Translation date method: Posting Date or Cashflow Date.
  • The system posts the full Fee amounts to the Balance Sheet and expensed to Profit and Loss over the life of the deal, from Settlement Date to Maturity Date.
  • The fee payment date is defined on the specific deal record, and fees may be paid in advance or in arrears.
  • The Straight Line method is used to calculate accrual amounts.
  • On income receipt:
    • fees payable in advance/arrears are posted as a debit entry to the Fees Expense Advance/Arrears Accrual Balance Sheet account,
    • fees receivable are posted as a credit entry to the Fees Receivable Advance/Arrears Accrual Balance Sheet account, and
    • the contra entry is to the Bank Account.
  • In Accruals Processing, the Balance Sheet accounts are reduced, with the contra entry being:
    • for fees payable - debit P&L account Fees Expense Advance/Arrears, or
    • for fees receivable - credit P&L account Fees Receivable Advance/Arrears.
