Our Instructions function (Stock Flow)

(Quick menu function on a Stock Flow item.)

For a stock flow, Our Instructions concern the stock account you are delivering stock from or receiving stock into (the transaction Entity's stock account).

Use the Our Instructions quick menu function to view and possibly change Our Delivery Instructions for one selected stock flow in the Settlement (or Settlement Approval) results grid. A separate Our Instructions window opens for the purpose.

Change Restrictions

You cannot change any fields IF you have selected a stock flow:

  • from the Settlement Approvals page,
  • on Maturity of the stock (STOCK REPAY FACE VALUE), or
  • linked to a cash flow that has a Payment Sent status of YES (Deliver/Receive against Payment).

Otherwise, you can optionally change the Entity Identifier, SWIFT MT Format, Payment Method, and (except for a Stock Account Transfer*) the Stock Account Number.

*NOTE: For a Stock Account Transfer, any changes you make here will be reflected on the Settlement Details tab for the transfer deal - in either the Source or Destination panel (depending on whether the stock flow is outgoing or incoming, respectively).

Field Descriptions

Currency and Amount

Read-only; the currency and amount of the stock flow.

Custodian

Read-only; the name of the custodian holding "our" stock account.

Account Number

"Our" (the transaction Entity's) Stock account number (with the Account Name shown below the field).

To change to another stock account, select from the drop-down list of Entity Stock Accounts in the currency of the stock flow.

TIP: If you change this account, the Payment Method will or will not change to the Default Payment Method for the new stock account, depending on whether the system-wide Operational Setting Apply new payment method when changing our instructions is selected or not, respectively.

BIC Code

Read-only; the Bank Identification Code for the custodian holding "our" stock account.

Payment Method

If required (and allowed), select a different payment method from those valid for the stock account, as applicable.

Entity Identifier

Identifiers are used to identify the entity at the bank or clearing house when using external confirmations and EFT systems.

If required, select a different identifier from those available for the selected Payment Method and the transaction's G/L Owner.

See About Entities and Business Structure for more information about creating and changing entity identifiers.
