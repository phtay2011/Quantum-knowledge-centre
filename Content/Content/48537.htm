Day Count Conventions

Day count conventions determine how interest accrues over time in a variety of transactions. Interest accrual is usually expressed as a rate per annum (reference period). It is often due and payable at shorter intervals (interest period).

A day count convention is commonly expressed as a fraction. The numerator is the convention for the number of days in the interest period - usually actual or a notional 30. The denominator is the convention for the number of days in the reference period - usually 360 or 365.

Day Count Conventions in Quantum

Quantum supports a number of day count convention formulas for determining the basis for the accrual and payment of interest.

In the Days Convention lookups throughout Quantum , the term to the left of the slash (numerator) indicates how the number of days in the interest period is calculated, while the term on the right of the slash (denominator) indicates how the number of days in the year is calculated, for example, 30/360 or ACTUAL/365.

In Quantum, the day count always includes the start date, but excludes the end date of the period.

Quantum supports the following day count conventions:

  • 30/360
  • 30/360ISDA
  • 30E/360
  • 30E/360Eurobond
  • 30E/360ISDA
  • 30E+/360
  • ACT INCL/365
  • ACTUAL/360
  • ACTUAL/364
  • ACTUAL/365
  • ACTUAL/365CA
  • ACTUAL/365EQ
  • ACTUAL/365ISDA
  • ACTUAL/365LD
  • ACTUAL/365LE
  • ACTUAL/ACTUAL
  • NL/365
  • USE CURRENCY
  • YIELD-CDI Formula-specific Day Count Conventions
  • Overnight Index Swap-specific Day Count Conventions

Day Count Conventions in Analytics

Analytics uses the Days Convention (which is a "day count" convention) to determine the basis for calculation and accrual of interest. This setting determines how days and year days (the number of days per annum) are handled.

In the Days Convention, the first term (numerator) indicates how the number of days in a period are calculated, while the term on the right (denominator) indicates how the number of days in the year are calculated.

The following conventions are supported in Analytics (select a convention name for calculation details).

  • Actual/360
  • Actual/365
  • Actual/Actual
  • 30/360
  • 30E/360
  • Bond
