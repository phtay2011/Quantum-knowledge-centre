Delivery Charges for Bank Account Transactions

You can optionally use the Charges fields on the Bank Account page's Delivery Details tab to automatically create separate Bank Account Adjustment deals for charges on account transactions.

These in turn can output EFT Payment messages when:

  • the External Method is SWIFT or SWIFT-compatible, and
  • the payments are NOT to a Financial Institution.

Transaction charges for EFT payments can differ from bank to bank, depending on the circumstances - for example, whether or not the payment leaves the bank, or if the amount is paid to another country.

Make sure that you set Charges to a valid value for the relevant External Method and Counterparty in the applicable Counterparty Bank Account (Cparty delivery instructions) record.

Use the Charges fields (see below) to define the amount and currency of bank charges for the Sender and/or Receiver, where:

  • the Sender is the Entity - the account Owner (specified on the Bank Account page's G/L Setup tab), and
  • the Receiver is the Bank with whom the Entity has a cash account (specified in the Bank Account page header).

NOTES:

  • Sender's Charges must be present if Charges are paid by the beneficiary - that is, Detail of Charges is BEN (in the applicable Counterparty Bank Account record).
  • If Receiver's Charges are not present and Charges are paid by the Ordering Customer (Detail of Charges is OUR), they are assumed zero and no Charges Adjustment Deal is created.
  • Charges do not have to be in the same currency as that of the payment. However, the system-generated Bank Account Adjustment for the Charges will be in the same currency as the payment - where required, the amount is converted using the currency Translation Rate Type (see below), which must be specified, and an FX Rate for that specified Rate Type must be present.
  • When Detail of Charges is changed, it is considered a Modified SSI, even if it is subsequently changed back to its original value.

Charges area field descriptions

This area of the Bank Account page's Delivery Details contains the following items.

  • Sender Charges field pair - select a currency (in the lookup on the left) and the Sender's charge amount (in the adjacent field).

    NOTE: The amount is finalized when payment occurs, not at the time of deal entry.

  • Receiver Charges field pair - select a currency (in the lookup on the left) and the Receiver's charge amount (in the adjacent field).

    NOTE: The amount is finalized when payment occurs, not at the time of deal entry.

  • Translation Rate Type - If the Currency defined for Sender's charges is different from the bank account Currency of the (as in the header), select an FX Rate in the drop-down list.

    The applicable value of this rate will then be used to translate the charges amount into the same currency as that of the payment.

  • Comments - optionally enter text information about anything that may affect the actual amount of a charge applied to a payment.

    TIP: The information entered in this field is displayed in Settlements for Adhoc Instructions and 3rd Party payments.

Example

Cash Flow Payment

= EUR 200.00

Sender's Charges

= EUR 10.00

Receiver's Charges

= EUR 15.00

Charges paid by Beneficiary would result in an adjustment deal of EUR 10.00 income with EUR 190.00 as the cash flow Settlement Amount.

Charges paid by Ordering Customer would result in a Charges Deal of EUR 15.00 expense and EUR 215.00 as the cash flow Settlement Amount.

Had the charges been expressed in USD, the amount of the Charges Deal would have been converted to EUR using the latest known FX rate for the specified Translation Rate Type.
