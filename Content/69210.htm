Net or Gross Interest Posting / WHT Calculation

You can choose one of two ways of calculating interest for the purposes of G/L posting and/or Withholding Tax (WHT) calculation:

(For details of the G/L codes used for interest-related postings, see Interest-Related G/L Postings.)

Net Interest basis

With this method:

  • one interest amount is calculated on the net account balance for the interest period,
  • one interest posting is made to the G/L,
  • if WHT applies:
    • it is applied only when interest is paid to the call account owner (no WHT is due on interest received), and
    • the transfer of the tax amount to the Tax Payment Account is posted to the G/L, and a later G/L posting is made when the WHT is actually paid to the Tax Authority.

To choose this method:

  • On the Bank Account page's Interest tab (when Calculate Interest is NOTNO), ensure that the Calculate Interest on a gross basis option is cleared. This is the default setting.

Gross Interest basis

TIP: You would normally use this method only with client call accounts.

With this method:

  • if the account balance does not change sign during the interest period, interest calculation and G/L postings are as for the Net Interest basis; but
  • if the account balance does change sign during the interest period:
    • two interest amounts are calculated: one for paid interest (on the average of the credit balances), and one for received interest (on the average of the debit balances),
    • Quantum splits the bank account interest G/L postings into separate Paid and Received components, and
    • if WHT applies, it is calculated on the paid interest cash flow amount only and the G/L postings are as for the Net Interest basis.

To choose this method:

  • On the Bank Account page's Interest tab (when Calculate Interest is NOTNO), select the Calculate Interest on a gross basis option.
