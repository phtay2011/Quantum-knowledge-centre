About hedge types

Hedge accounting requires all derivatives are marked to market and reflected on the Balance Sheet at fair value - that is, on the market value of the transaction, based on liquid market rates and willing counterparties.

How that derivative is accounted for depends on why the hedge relationship exists - the type of hedge - as well as the nature of risk it is being used to hedge.

If it is to qualify for Hedge Accounting, a derivative must be designated (linked) as one of the hedge Types, Fair Value or Cash Flow, at the inception of a hedge relationship.

TIP: Use the Linked Deals tab on the Hedge Relationship page to link a deal to that hedge relationship. A linked deal is said to be designated.

FASB specifies what criteria must be met before each of these hedge Types can be applied.

Cash flow hedge

A cash flow hedge is used to mitigate the uncertainty associated with cash flows generated by a:

  • Floating rate on a recognized asset or liability
  • Forecasted transaction (sale or purchase)

    This is a transaction that is expected to occur, but for which there is no firm commitment (no fixed price nor timing). This means that when the event does occur, it will be at the prevailing market rates.

Revaluation

In Quantum, enter a Forecast transaction as you do a Firm Commitment - by using the Projected Cash Flow or Forecast Cash Flow page. As there are no Accounting Rules for the revaluation of these transactions, they are revalued according to settings on the Linked Deals tab. For a cash flow hedge, these indicate:

  • Whether or not this is a Forecasted transaction (Forecast column)
  • Which Market Revaluation method is to be used (Component column)
  • What Rate Name and Rate Type are to be applied (Interest Rate Name, Rate Type columns)

See also Revaluation of Actual and Projected Cashflows.

Other deals are revalued as for a fair value hedge.

Other Comprehensive Income (OCI)

A cash flow hedge differs from a fair value hedge in the way that the effective and ineffective portions of the Hedge deal are handled.

The effective portion of the Hedge deal is transferred to the Balance Sheet as OCI. Its release into earnings can then be timed to coincide with the release into earnings of cash flows for the Exposure deal.

Any ineffective portion of the Hedge deal remains in the Profit and Loss account. Dedesignation will be necessary.

NOTE: To set up customized rules to control the release of the OCI amount into earnings, see the Hedge Relationship > Events tab.

Fair value hedge

A fair value hedge:

  • is used to mitigate the uncertainty associated with movements in the market value of an Exposure (the transaction that is being hedged);
  • is generally applied to a:
    • financial transaction, which can be either an asset or a liability, or a
    • firm commitment, which is characterized by a fixed price and timing; and
  • enables you to revalue exposures that otherwise could not be accounted for.

When designated (linked) as part of a hedge relationship, "non-financial" exposures, such as fixed assets for example, can be marked to market and accounted for in earnings.

TIP: A firm commitment is an agreement with an unrelated party that is binding on both parties and usually legally enforceable. The agreement includes a disincentive for non-performance, significant enough to make performance probable, and specifies all significant terms, including the:

  • quantity to be exchanged,
  • fixed price, expressed as a specified amount of the entity's functional currency, amount of a foreign currency, interest rate, or effective yield, and
  • timing of the transaction.

Balance Sheet

Individual deals that make up a hedge relationship - both Exposure and Hedge deals - are recorded on the Balance Sheet at fair value.

Revaluation

At the end of a reporting period, you run Hedge Reclassification in a position processing run and Quantum revalues the individual deals that make up the hedge relationship. It marks them to market, applying the revaluation methods specified for the respective hedge strategies in the applicable Accounting Rule record.

NOTE The revaluation of underlying deals in a hedge relationship is the same as their revaluation when not in a hedge relationship.

In Quantum, enter a Firm Commitment using the Actual Cash Flow or Projected Cash Flow page. As there is no Accounting Rule for their revaluation they are revalued according to settings on the Linked Deals tab which, for a fair value hedge, indicate:

  • that this is a Firm Commitment (in the Forecast column),
  • which Market Revaluation method is to be used (in the Component column), and
  • what Rate Name and Rate Type are to be applied (in the Interest Rate Name, Rate Type columns).

Profit and Loss (P & L)

Any change to the fair value of the individual deals is recognized in the P&L accounts. Changes to both the Hedge and the Exposure impact Profit and Loss results at the same time. Postings to the General Ledger are based on either the Hedge deals or Exposure deals, depending on your setting of the Postings based on option.

Effectiveness

When you run the Position Query in Hedge Reclassification mode, Quantum assesses the effectiveness of each hedge relationship, and:

  • If changes in the fair value of the Hedge deal fully counteract changes in the fair value of the Exposure deal, that hedge relationship is effective. The amounts are equal but opposite in effect, and affect the Profit and Loss accounts at the same time.
  • If changes in the fair value of the Hedge deal do not adequately offset changes in the fair value of the Exposure deal, that hedge relationship is ineffective. The two amounts affect Profit and Loss at the same time, but there will be a net effect on earnings. Dedesignation will be necessary.

NOTE: A hedge relationship can be effective or partially effective, ineffective or partially ineffective. A single hedge relationship can have both effective and ineffective portions.
