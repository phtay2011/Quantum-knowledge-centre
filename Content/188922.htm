Futures & ETOs Posting, Profit and Loss tab

  • Broker Commission / Clearing Fee / Exchange Fee (applies to Futures and ETOs; Debit account for fees that are payable)

    Credit Profit and Loss accounts are not required for commissions and fees. The reason for this is that the Futures Exchange or Broker always receives the fee. The Buyer or Seller always pays, which equates to a Debit fee.

    NOTE:These entries default to the Profit and Loss account, however if required you can direct them to the Balance Sheet. To do so, on this tab, attach Balance Sheet G/L account templates against the entries.

  • Expired Variation Margin Gain (Futures only)

    Credit account. For an explanation of this, see Variation Margin Gain below. On the contract Settlement date, <the system transfers aggregate Variation Margin Gain/Loss for all Futures deals to the Expired Variation Margin Gain/Loss accounts if:

    • in Accounting Rules, the Revalue futures, using field is populated, and the Posting method is set to Unrealized; and
    • the account code specified for the Variation Margin Gain is different from the account code specified for Expired Variation Margin Gain.

    This enables you to see Variation Margin for current deals separately from Variation Margin for cash settled deals that have passed their contract Settlement date.

    NOTE: If the account code for Variation Margin Gain is the same as the account code for Expired Variation Margin Gain, no transfer will occur.

    The transfer from the Variation Margin Gain/Loss account takes place on the date generated by the Futures Settlement Day Rule specified for the Futures or ETO instrument.

  • Expired Variation Margin Loss (Futures only) - Debit account; see Variation Margin Gain below.
  • Premium Expense (Futures and ETOs) - Debit account.

    If the Option deal is a purchase, the system posts Premium Expense. If the deal is a sale, it posts Premium Income. The date of the entry depends on settings in the Premium panel on the Futures and ETOs Instrument page, as follows.

    • If the Premium Type is DEAL DATE, the system posts the premium expense on the deal date for both opening and closing deals (if the opening deal is a purchase or the closing deal is a purchase). The premium is posted separately for each opening and closing deal.

      If a Premium Settlement Offset is specified, the premium expense is dated the required number of days after the deal date.

    • If the Premium Type is CLOSEOUT, the system creates entries that recognize the gross premium amount when you save a new deal, but these are dated the Option settlement date. They are debited to the Premium Expense account for purchases (or credited to the Premium Income account for sales). Contra entries are posted to the Broker account.

      When the deal is closed out, the system deletes these entries and replaces them with new entries. A net premium, based on the different premium prices of the opening and closing deal, is recognized and attached to the closing deal. This can be net premium expense or net premium income, depending on the respective prices of the opening and closing deals. Net premium expense and net premium income can be attached to either a purchase or a sale.

      When an opening purchase is only partly closed out, replacement contract settlement date entries for the remaining premium expense for the remaining lots are also created. If a Premium Settlement Offset is specified, the net premium entries are dated that number of offset days after the closeout match date.

      When the deal is exercised,, the system deletes the entries on the Option Settlement Date and replaces these with entries dated as the Exercise date (or the Exercise Settlement Date, if an Exercise Offset is specified).

      When a purchase is only partly exercised, replacement contract Settlement Date entries for the remaining premium expense for the remaining lots are also created.

  • Premium Income (Futures and ETOs) - Credit account; see Premium Expense above.
  • Realized Gain (Futures and ETOs) - Credit account.

    The system uses the Gain: Realized default if either of the following occurs.

    • A Futures contract is closed out, and the closeout results in a gain. The entry is posted when closeout occurs (usually when the closing deal is saved), and is dated the settlement date of the closeout. This entry occurs regardless of the Revaluation settings defined on the Accounting Rules tab.
    • An Option deal is exercised on a Cash Settled basis, the exercise price is different from the deal price, and the exercise results in a gain.

    The use of the account depends on the treatments applied in Accounting Rules.

    • If you choose to revalue, and the Posting Method is Realized, the system posts both Realized Gain/Loss and Variation Margin entries to the Realized Chart of Account defaults, using the Realized Gain and Realized Loss tags on the Profit and Loss tab to do so. The amounts are not disclosed separately.
    • If you choose to revalue, and the Posting Method is Unrealized, Variation Margin entries are posted to the Variation Margin Gain and Variation Margin Loss defaults, but realized amounts to the Realized Gain and Realized Loss tags.

    If the Revalue futures, using option is selected, the system posts revaluations using the:

    • Realized Gain default (before closeout) - if the Posting method is Realized, but the...
    • Variation Margin defaults - if the Posting method is Unrealized.

    However, if the Revalue futures, using option is cleared, the system does not post deal revaluations and the Realized Gain default is not used.

  • Realized Loss (Futures and ETOs) - Debit account; see Gain: Realized above.

    The system uses the Loss: Realized default if either of the following occurs.

    • A Futures contract is closed out, and the closeout results in a loss. The entry is posted when closeout occurs (usually when the closing deal is saved), and is dated the settlement date of the closeout. This entry occurs regardless of the Revaluation settings defined in Accounting Rules.
    • An Option deal is exercised on a Cash Settled basis, the exercise price is different from the deal price, and the exercise/assignment results in a loss.
  • Variation Margin Gain (Futures and ETOs) - Credit account; see Gain: Realized above.

    The system uses this default if the Revalue field in Accounting Rules is selected, and the Posting method is Unrealized. It is used for mark-to-market revaluation gains for Futures and Options deals. The contra entry is posted to the Broker account.

    Switch accounting is applied to each deal, so that if the deal changes from having mark-to market gain to having mark-to-market loss, the gain is reversed before any loss is posted.

    When you run the Futures Margin query to calculate Variation Margin, the system also creates entries dated the contract Settlement Date. These will either reverse or transfer the aggregate Variation Margin that is outstanding for each deal.

    For Options deals, aggregate Variation Margin is reversed on the contract settlement date. For Futures deals, it can be:

    • retained in the Variation Margin Gain/Loss accounts for all Futures deals, or...
    • transferred to the Expired Variation Margin Gain/Loss accounts on the contract Settlement Date.

    As a result, you can see Variation Margin for current deals separately from Variation Margin for cash settled deals that have passed their contract Settlement Date (see Expired Variation Margin Gainor Expired Variation Margin Loss above). Transfer from the Variation Margin Gain/Loss account takes place on the date generated by the Futures Settlement Day Rule specified on the Futures and ETOs Instrument.

    NOTE:

    • Variation Margin on closed deals is not reversed after a closeout until you run the Futures Margin query again.
    • If you specify the same account code for Variation Margin Gain and Variation Margin Loss, the system recognizes that no switch accounting is required.

    See Variation Margin above.

  • Variation Margin Loss (Futures and ETOs) - Debit account; see Variation Margin Gain above.
