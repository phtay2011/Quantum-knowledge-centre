Step 2: Adjust periodic payment amount to give zero residual

This step is required when the days convention is not 30/360 and not 30E/360. This is because the above formula assumes equal coupon periods. Since a 30/360 or 30E/360 days convention is the same as equal periods, using one of these will amortize the principal balance to zero at maturity.

Other day count convention formulas assume unequal coupon periods, and the periodic payment amount will not amortize the principal balance to zero, leaving a residual principal balance at maturity.

Therefore, when the days convention is not 30/360 and not 30E/360, the periodic payment amount calculated in Step 1 is iterated/adjusted by small increments until the residual principal balance at maturity is as close to zero as possible. The Newton-Raphson iteration method is used to adjust the periodic payment amount (similar to Microsoft Excel's Goal Seek forecasting function).

Other Information

If a transaction of this type is matured prior to the recorded deal maturity date, the date may only be modified to coincide with an existing payment date, because the amortization formula only works for complete periods.

TIP: If the matured date does not coincide with an existing payment date, you could create a new deal based on an instrument with a Yield formula, as follows.

1. Enter the deal details including the revised maturity date.

2. Copy the customize table from the existing deal.

3. Customize the maturity date cash flows as required.

4. Save your customized deal.
