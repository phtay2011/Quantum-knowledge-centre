First Coupon Date, Money Market Deal

(Displayed only if Formula Timing is BOND PERIODIC, FRN-CGL PERIODIC, or INDEXED BOND PERIODIC.)

This date applies when there is an odd first coupon period for the deal being traded.

The end date of the odd first coupon period (shorter or longer than the normal coupon frequency). The period from the Dated Date to this date defines the odd first coupon period. The system uses this date to determine the:

  • coupon date cycle for the life of the deal - by cycling forward from this date to the Maturity Date at the Coupon Frequency, and
  • penultimate coupon date (whether specifically defined by a user or not) - as a whole number of coupon frequencies from the First Coupon Date, which may result in an odd last coupon period that defaults to be short.

When settlement falls within an odd first coupon period Quantum calculates the amount due using a quasi-previous coupon date that defines a virtual full coupon period and is used along with the Coupon Day Count and Coupon values to calculate the proportion of a full coupon amount that is due for payment. See Short first coupon period example and Long first coupon period example.
