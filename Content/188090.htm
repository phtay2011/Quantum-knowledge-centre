Splits and Reverse Splits on Unit Holdings

Splits and Reverse Splits are Corporate Actions for Unit Holdings.

  • Split: the division of a Unit Holding's eligible balance of Units into a larger number of Units.

    Example: A 3-for-1 split by a company with 1 million shares selling at $75 per share results in 3 million shares selling at $25 per share. Each holder of 100 shares after the split would be entitled to 300 shares. Their proportionate equity in the company remains the same.

  • Reverse Split: a reduction in the number of Units, increasing the par value of its stock or its earnings per share.

    Example: A 1-for-2 reverse split means you get half as many shares, but at twice the price. The market value of the total number of shares (market capitalization) remains the same.

A Split or Reverse Split will not create a new Unit Deal.

If the Closeout Method on the Holding Instrument is:

  • WEIGHTED AVG - the Split/Reverse split is created at the Holding Deal level, but if
  • FIFO, LIFO, or MATCHED - the Split/Reverse split is completed on the Holding's OPEN Unit deals.

Use the Unit Split page to:

  • enter or edit details of a Split (or Reverse Split) Corporate Action on a Unit Holding,
  • enter or edit details of a book adjustment (and optionally a cash flow also) for the difference between the New Balance and Eligible Balance amounts, and
  • view recent Corporate Action Notifications that have been linked to the Holding and that match the Split details.

To create a new Split (or Reverse Split) Corporate Action:

  1. On the Unit Holding page, open the holding you want.
  2. In the page menu, select Split.

    The Unit Split page opens in a new window.

  3. Select the Split Type (SPLIT or REVERSE SPLIT), enter the Ratio or For number as required, and enter or select the Record Date, Deal Date, Settlement Date, and Dealer.
  4. Optionally edit either the system-calculated New Balance or Adjustment number of units if required.
  5. in the Create Book Adjustment area:
    • enter any remaining book adjustment Amount to be recorded after the Split or Reverse Split is applied (for example, where a fractional number of Units remains after rounding the result);
    • enter the Deal Date and Settlement Date for the Book Value adjustment; and
    • select or clear Create cashflow for adjustment to specify whether the adjustment amount is to be paid in cash or written off, respectively.
  6. (Optional) In the Recent Notifications grid, review any matching Corporate Action Notifications that have previously been linked to the Unit Holding and that match the Split Type and Record Date specified above.
  7. Click Save.

    The Unit Split page window closes. Your Split action is applied and displayed on the Corporate Actions tab.

To edit the last Split for the current holding:

(Only allowed when a Split is the most recent Corporate Action completed on the Holding.)

  1. On the Corporate Actions tab, click Edit Last Split.

    The Unit Split page opens in a new window, displaying the entered details for the last Split.

  2. Edit the data items as required (see above).
  3. Click Save.

    The Unit Split page window closes. Your changes are applied and displayed on the Corporate Actions tab.

To delete the last Split for the current holding:

(Only allowed when a Split is the most recent Corporate Action completed on the Holding.)

  1. On the Corporate Actions tab, click Delete Last Split
  2. When prompted, click OK to confirm.

    The Split is deleted and no longer shows on the Corporate Actions tab. All database changes caused by the Split are backed out.
