Hedging Cross Currency Swaps without basis spread (IFRS 9)

Quantum recognizes the Currency Basis spread as a separate cost of hedging and posts changes in Fair Value of the Currency Basis for the Cross Currency Swap to OCI, when the basis element is excluded from the Hedge Designation.

Designated Risk: Overall Market Risk v Cross Currency Swap - Basis

The Currency Basis Spread is a charge embedded in cross currency financial instruments that compensates for credit and liquidity risk as well as supply and demand factors.

When currency basis is designated in a hedge relationship by setting the Designated Risk to Overall Market Risk, ineffectiveness could arise as a result of the spread being irrelevant to a single currency hedged item.

When an entity excludes the foreign currency basis spread from the hedging designation by setting the Designated Risk to Cross Currency Swap - Basis, then the changes in the currency basis spread may be accumulated in OCI and recognized in profit and loss.

Hedging procedures

Configure Analytics queries

To Hedge Account using CROSS CURRENCY SWAP - BASIS requires the Analytics module to be configured to supply imported valuations to the Hedge Accounting module. To do this, configure the following Analytics queries:

  • 'ACCT' MVI Query - Calculating a standard Cross Currency Swap Market Valuation where the cash flows are discounted using a discount curve with a Currency Basis Spread margin applied.
  • 'HEDG' MVI Query - Calculating a Cross Currency Swap Market Valuation where,
    • the cash flows exclude the Currency Basis spread element implied in the Cross Currency Swap Rate; and
    • the Discount Factors exclude the Currency Basis Spread.

By excluding the Currency Basis Spread from the Cash Flows and Discount Factors, a near zero Market Value should be determined at Hedge Inception. Using this method of calculation, the amount accumulated in the Cost of Hedging Reserve should naturally amortize to zero over the life of the Hedge Relationship as the underlying Cash Flows are settled.

NOTES:

  • The Cross Currency Deal's Cross Currency Basis Spread field is used to identify the Currency Basis Spread embedded in the Cross Currency Rate, and can be referenced in Analytics query definitions.
  • An Accounting Rule that imports valuations from Analytics must be configured for the Cross Currency Swap deals in the hedge relationship.

Create the Hedge Strategy page

Create a valid Hedge Strategy page with the following options:

  1. A Fair Value Hedge or Cash Flow Hedge for the Hedge Type; CROSS CURRENCY SWAP - BASIS for the Designated Risk (Details tab).
  2. If required, set a release date for the adjustment amounts (Events tab).

Create the Hedges, Hypotheticals and Exposures

Create the following deals in Quantum according to the requirements of your hedge:

  1. Hedge: Create one or more Cross Currency Swap deals that represent the terms of the hedge.
  2. Hypothetical*: Create a Cross Currency Swap Hypothetical deal that represents the terms of hedged exposure. Typically this will reflect the Hedge with the Pay and Receive amounts and legs swapped, but otherwise matching its terms.
  3. For each Hedge deal created in Step 1, create a that counteracts the purpose of the hedge (hat is, with the Pay and Receive amounts and legs swapped), but otherwise matches the terms of the hedge exactly.

    * It is possible to account for the Currency Basis component of the Swap only to the extent that it relates to the hedged item.When the terms of the hedge do not match at inception, the Aligned Currency Basis is determined using a Hypothetical Derivative Deal, which represents the risk being hedged.

  4. Exposure: Create one or more Security deals to designate the risk being hedged.

Create the Hedge Relationship

Create a hedge relationship with the following options:

  1. Select the Hedge Strategy page with CROSS CURRENCY SWAP - BASIS as the Designated Risk, created above.
  2. Select the Maturity Date, and ensure that the Designated Risk is set to CROSS CURRENCY SWAP - BASIS (Deal Details tab).
  3. For time-period related hedges, select Amortize hedging costs (time value) (Deal Details tab).
  4. Add the Hedges, Hypotheticals†, and Exposures created above to the hedge relationship (Linked Deals tab).

    † For the Hypothetical Deal, select the Leg when designating the deal into the hedge relationship as follows:

    • For a cash flow hedge select Both.
    • For a fair value hedge this should be the leg corresponding to the Exposure.
