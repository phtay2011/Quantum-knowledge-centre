Specifying Non-Standard Business Days

These are specific dates falling on days of the week defined as non-business days (usually Saturday and Sunday) that are, despite that, scheduled to be business days.

For example, it is common practice in some Asian countries for the government to declare a particular Saturday or Sunday to be a regular Business day. This can occur in conjunction with an extension of a holiday period around the same time - see the example below.

IMPORTANT NOTE: If any of these dates fall on a Location or Currency holiday applying to a particular transaction, the holiday takes precedence - that is, the day remains a Non Business Day in such cases.

Use the Business Days tab to specify such days.

Business Days tab Data Columns

Name - a unique name identifying the non-standard business day

Fixed Date -the date that is to become a business day

Observable (read-only) - always set to Actual Observed

Non-standard Business Day Example

The standard currency setup for the Chinese Yuan (CNY) would be as for most currencies): to have Saturday and Sunday set as Non Business Days. In China, Wednesday 16 June 2010 was the Dragon Boat Festival statutory holiday. To create a three-day holiday break, the Chinese Government declared Monday 14 and Tuesday 15 as official holidays also, and that those Monday and Tuesday work schedules were to be transferred to Saturday 12 and Sunday 13.

To set this up, you would have specified (a year or more earlier):

  • on the Holidays tab: all three days - 14, 15, and 16 June 2010 - as Dragon Boat Festival observed holidays, and
  • on the Business Days tab: both Saturday 12 and Sunday 13 June 2010 as non-standard Business days.
