More about Rates

Quantum uses rates for a number of core treasury functions. This includes pricing, revaluing assets and liabilities, and currency translations. Key applications that use rates are:

  • Position Query - applies rates to revalue deals
  • Currency Translation - uses rates to calculate currency gains and losses
  • Deal and Settlement Approvals - use rates to convert deal cash flows before matching with Approval Control records
  • AvantGard Equities - creates and enters Unit prices as rate records

Quantum uses the following hierarchical structure to enter rates.

  • Rate Names - used to enter the unique names of the different rates to be used by the system; frequently reflect published rates
  • Rate Types - used to specify the same rate differently depending on what it is being used for
  • Rate records - either automatically uploaded or entered via various applications (for example, Interest Rates, Exchange Rates, Bank Interest Rates, Volatility Rates, and Index Rates).

    Rate records are unique and identified by a combination of Rate Name, Rate Type, Currency, and Date. Quantum uses this identification when saving a rate record, and when selecting a rate record for a revaluation, for example.

    For Futures Rates and Exchange Traded Option Rates, it is the combination of Contract, Exchange, Currency, and Date that identifies the record. A Rate Name is not required.

TIP: In addition to its deal-related usage, a Rate Name can also be specified in the:

  • Interest Rate column of Management Reporting Control
  • Interest rate field on the Accounting Rules tab in Accounting Setup
