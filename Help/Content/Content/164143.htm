About the Quantum Calendar

The calendar is used by Quantum to select business days for deal-saving programs. Cash flow and rate-setting events for deals may occur only on days specified as business days.

The calendar is initially setup with every day as a business day. During system setup (and subsequent maintenance), an Administrator user must specify non-business days at two levels: Currency-related and Location-related.

Currency and Location have separate structures and are not linked in any way.

  • Location has The World at the top of the tree. This would normally be followed by Country, State (or Province), and City.
  • Currency is not structured. The links between Currency and Country are made in the Country record when a currency is selected.

To use the calendar

  1. Use the Currency page to enter days of the week that are standard non-business days for each currency - usually the weekend days Saturday and Sunday.
  2. Use the Business Day Calendar page to enter and edit, for a specified Location or Currency:
    • on the Holidays tab - additional non-business days (usually statutory holidays) - see Specifying Holidays, and
    • on the Business Days tab - specific "non-standard" business days - see Specifying Non-Standard Business Days.

    You can also use the Test Holidays and Business Days function to list holidays and non-standard business days for a specified date range and (optionally) Currency and/or Location.

    The entry process is identical for Location and Currency.

The process of recording holidays has been made as simple as possible by enabling you to easily record a standard holiday only once, as a referential date.

Any saved changes to non-business days take effect only for deals saved after those changes.
