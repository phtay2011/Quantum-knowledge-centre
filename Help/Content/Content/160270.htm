Swaption Deals in Quantum

Swaptions (swap options) are used to create options on forward starting swaps.

A Swaption gives the holder the right - but not the obligation - to enter into a particular Interest Rate Swap deal on a specified future date, and at a specified swap rate.

Swaptions can reduce the uncertainty associated with a Swap transaction. If a company wishes to enter into swap deals to convert their floating rate loan into a fixed rate loan in the future, they cannot be certain of the fixed rate at the time of the swap. To eliminate the uncertainty, they can purchase an appropriately structured Swaption.

Example

A company could enter into a Swaption agreement giving them the right to receive LIBOR and pay a fixed rate of 7.5% starting in six months time.

If the swap rate in six months time turns out to be greater than 7.5%, then the company would exercise their option. Conversely, if the market swap rate turns out to be lower than 7.5%, then the company would lapse the Swaption. Either way, the fixed rate of the swap is guaranteed to be no higher than 7.5%.

Swaption types in Quantum

The following Interest Rate Option types are supported for Swaptions.

  • Payer - An option on a valid swap giving the buyer the right to pay a fixed interest rate and receive a floating interest rate.
  • Receiver - An option on a valid swap giving the buyer the right to receive a fixed interest rate and pay a floating interest rate.

Supported Interest Rate Swap structures

European-style, Vanilla Swaptions are supported for Interest Rate Swaps with:

  • different Fixed and Floating payment frequencies,
  • preset Margins, and
  • uneven periods.

NOTE: The underlying Interest Rate Swap instrument must have Allow Unwinds selected.

Unsupported Swaption types

Currently not supported are Swaptions on:

  • American and Bermudan style Swaps,
  • Cross Currency Swaps,
  • Basis Swaps,
  • Zero Coupon Swaps,
  • Swaps using the Principal and Interest formula,
  • Swaps with an amortization schedule (Allow amortization selected on either leg), and
  • customized swaps.
