About Interest Rate Swaps

In a "vanilla" or single currency Interest Rate Swap transaction, two counterparties agree to exchange interest cash flows on a periodic basis for a predetermined length of time. Usually, one party will pay a certain fixed interest rate to the other while receiving an amount that is based on a benchmark floating interest rate. This constitutes a fixed-for-floating swap, where the cash flows are based on the same notional principal sum and are denominated in the same currency.

Each swap can be linked to a normal Yield or non-indexed Bond-style formula, and is recorded as a single deal with many components.

Much of the input on the Swap Deal page is defaulted from the selected Instrument. You can choose whether to keep or change these values.

CAUTION: Do not use Swap Floating legs with the Principal & Interest formula (you are not prevented from specifying Swap instruments and deals with this combination, but deal payment amounts will be incorrect from the first rate set on).

TIP: You can use the Quantum Fees pages to automatically generate fees on Interest Rate swaps.

External Reference numbers

External Reference numbers and text will appear as read-only values on the Deal Details tab, but only when:

  • the deal data has been imported or input via an API, and
  • the External Reference fields or properties are populated.

Deal Allocation feature

Deal Allocation is optional for Quantum Interest Rate Swap deals, and enables you to link a master deal (the Source deal) to one or more automatically created additional deals of a similar type.

See Deal Allocation for Swap Deals.

Entering a Forward Rate

In Arrears Swaps only.

Rolling Back

You can roll back a Swap Deal, resetting it to an earlier state.

Unwinding

You can unwind an Interest Rate Swap deal prior to its start date, subject to the following restrictions.

  • Vanilla Yield-based Swaps only.
  • Quantum does not support Swap unwinds with:
    • Trade Date accounting,
    • closeouts prior to start date, or
    • Forward starting with Principal Exchange (initial, maturity, or both; or at unwind).
  • Currency translation is completed at the residual level rather than deal level.

Switching Pay/Receive

You can switch the Pay and Receive legs of a Swap Deal - for example, when you want to change a deal from the Entity's viewpoint to the Counterparty's.

In the page menu, select Switch Pay Receive.

In most cases. this does not require a rebuild of the deal map, and any customized entries are kept and included. The exceptions are:

  • If at least one leg calculates tax, the customized deal map entries will not be switched and the deal map will be recalculated.
  • If the Swap deal has been partially unwound, the deal maps will be regenerated.

Hypothetical Swaps

If you are licensed for Hedge Accounting, you can create a Hypothetical Swap deal to measure the effectiveness of your live Swap Hedge deal - by setting Deal Type to Hypothetical. See Hypothetical Derivative deals for more information.

Negative Rates

In Interest Rate and Currency Swap deal legs with Formula/Timing methods of YIELDCOMPOUND or YIELDPERIODIC, you can:

  • set the Rate and/or Margin fields to negative values in a Fixed or Floating leg (provided Formula/Timing is YIELDCOMPOUND or YIELDPERIODIC), and
  • set the Forward Rate field for an In-Arrears Swap leg to a negative value.

Deal Unwind, Customization, Revaluation, Bulk Renegotiation, accruals, and other accounting processes support such negative values.

When accounting for a Swap with a negative rate, interest will continue to be posted based on the leg of the deal, and:

  • a Pay leg will post to Interest Expense,
  • a Receive leg will post to Interest Income.

Deals in Hedge Relationships

TIP:

  • To add and maintain any deals in a Hedge Relationship, use the Linked Deals tab on the Hedge Relationship page.
  • The Dealing page shows a Hedge Relationships tab if the current deal is a Linked Deal within one or more Hedge Relationships. If so, you can quickly access the Hedge Relationship from this tab by clicking on the HR Deal Number in the Allocations grid.
