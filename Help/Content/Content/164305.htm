Overview of Hedge Relationships

In Quantum, enter a hedge relationship much as you would a deal. Similarly, set up a Hedge Strategy page as an Instrument Type with settings that default to the hedge relationship when you select the Instrument, providing the consistent treatment of like items that is a FASB requirement. Additional information you add to a hedge relationship deal is specific to that hedge relationship.

The Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB) is a standard- setting body within the United States.

While Quantum provides the mechanisms to satisfy the requirements of the FASB standards and others, such as IAS and IFRS, it does not police the requirements of any single standard. For example, it does not validate combinations of deals, assess the rules associated with hedges, or enforce documentation results.

If you try to open a new form before the current hedge relationship has been saved, you are given an opportunity to complete and/or save the current input, or simply abandon it before continuing.

Designation

A hedge relationship comes into existence - is designated - when a Hedge is linked to an Exposure. Deals in a hedge relationship are formally designated as hedging a specified risk. A single hedge relationship can link:

  • one Hedge and one Exposure
  • one Hedge and many Exposures
  • many Hedges and one Exposure
  • many Hedges and many Exposures

The Linked Deals tab is central to this process.

Relationship between hedges, exposures, hedge strategies, and hedge relationships

NOTE: A single Hedge or Exposure deal can be part of multiple hedge relationships.

Approvals

Initially, the system approves all hedge relationships automatically.

Revaluation

To revalue a hedge relationship, open the Position page via the Accounting menu, select, or configure, a suitable query, and click Update.

While each underlying deal can be marked-to-market in its own right, the part of the deal that is in the hedge relationship is revalued as part of that hedge relationship.

Accruals

Position Query also posts accruals for hedge relationships. There is no need to run Accrual Query for these.

Dedesignation

Regular effectiveness calculations determine whether the hedge relationship is achieving the intended results that were documented at the outset. If not, full or partial dedesignation (removal of deals from the hedge relationship) is required, with associated General Ledger postings.

The results of successive effectiveness calculations are recorded against the hedge relationship and can be reported on.

A hedge relationship ends when it no longer includes both an Exposure and a Hedge, because of full dedesignation or maturity. See Dedesignation.

Deleted hedge relationships

When a hedge relationship is deleted, all links to underlying deals are automatically removed. The deals are no longer displayed on the Linked Deals spreadsheet, and become available for linking to other hedge relationships.

TIP: To see what deals were previously linked to a deleted hedge relationship, view its audit history prior to the deletion (see "Audit History" below).

Deal Details tab

Hedge relationships can be viewed in the Deal Details tab.

Imaging

Imaging is available for all significant changes to hedge relationships. These images (which are like snapshots of a deal at a point in time) are required for backdated revaluations, for example when dedesignations occur between accounting period end processing.

Audit history

Use the Deal Audit page to see a record of 'before and after' changes that have been made to the hedge relationship.
