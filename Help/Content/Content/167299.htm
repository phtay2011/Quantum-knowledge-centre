Index-Linked Bond formulas

Analytics

Index-linked bonds (ILBs) are bonds where the principal is linked to an index, for example, the Consumer Price Index (CPI).

These bonds pay a coupon that is equivalent to the sum of the increase in the inflation index and the real coupon rate. However, it is not the interest rate that changes with inflation, but rather, the underlying principal of the bond changes, which results in a higher interest payment when multiplied by the same rate.

In Quantum, an Index Ratio (IR) is the multiplication factor that is applied to a cash flow amount on a particular date, to adjust the cash flow for inflation (or deflation).

Different Indexed Bond Instruments can have different definitions/rules to calculate the Index Ratio, while still using the same Index Rate Series table.

Quantum supports Index-Linked Bonds with either Exact or Equal coupon periods.

The following day count conventions are those supported by Quantum for Index-Linked Bonds (ILBs) using the INDEXED BOND Calculation Formula.

  • ACTUAL/365
  • ACTUAL/ACTUAL
  • ACTUAL/365ISDA
  • 30E/360Eurobond
